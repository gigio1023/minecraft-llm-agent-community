# Privileged Foresight Distillation (PFD)

- **title**: Privileged Foresight Distillation
- **year**: 2026
- **venue/source**: arXiv
- **arxiv_id**: 2604.25859
- **urls**: code https://github.com/PengchengFang-cs/PFD ; LaTeX at `papers/latex/2604.25859/`
- **source availability**: latex (full)

## Primary-source facts

- **Premise being examined**: WAMs jointly predict future video + action during training; a recent result (Fast-WAM, yuan2026) showed the future-prediction branch "can be removed at inference with little to no loss on common manipulation benchmarks." This raised the question: is future video merely a training-time regularizer on the shared backbone?
- **Claim**: joint training induces an **action-conditioned correction** that privileged future observations impose on action denoising; current-only policies capture it only partially. Formalized as a **foresight residual** = (action-velocity prediction given true future) − (prediction given only current frame).
- **Method (PFD)**: a training-time teacher (attends to full future video) and a current-only student share the same backbone, differ only in the attention mask over video tokens. A **small adapter** on the student distills the teacher-minus-student residual. **Future video is never generated at inference**; the adapter restores the foresight correction with only slight latency overhead.
- **Results**: PFD improves over Fast-WAM on LIBERO and RoboTwin; controlled experiments rule out capacity/regularization/budget confounds. Reframes future info "not as a target to predict, nor a regularizer to absorb, but as a **compressible correction to be distilled.**"

## Interpretation

- This is the sharpest primary-source evidence that **pixel-video generation at inference is not load-bearing** for the action benefit. The useful content of "imagining the future" is a small, distillable correction signal — it does not require rendering frames. For a project deciding whether to build a heavy pixel predictor, this says: the inference-time pixel rollout is dispensable; what matters is a compact future-conditioned signal.
- By analogy, a Minecraft social predictor need not generate rich future world-state at runtime; a compact "expected consequence" signal (predicted social-material delta + risk) is the operative part.

## Mechanically useful vs research contribution

- **Useful**: justification that the project should NOT invest in inference-time future generation; a compact advisory signal suffices. Conceptual support for keeping any predictor lightweight and training-time-grounded.
- **Avoid/overclaim**: PFD is a manipulation-policy distillation method; do not import its specifics. The lesson is the "future signal is compressible" principle, not the residual-adapter mechanism itself.
