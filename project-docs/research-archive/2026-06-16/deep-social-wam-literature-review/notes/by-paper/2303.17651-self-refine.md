## Primary-source facts (what the paper states/shows)

- The purest self-evaluated improvement method: a single LLM is the generator, the feedback
  provider, and the refiner. Generate an initial output; the SAME model critiques it; the SAME
  model revises using that critique; repeat until the model decides to stop or a step budget is
  hit. Driven only by few-shot prompts.
- Explicitly "does not require any supervised training data, additional training, or reinforcement
  learning" and uses no external feedback or reward model. "Supervision-free" in the sense that the
  only supervision is the few-shot exemplars.
- **Result**: across 7 diverse tasks (dialogue response, code optimization, sentiment reversal,
  acronym generation, math reasoning, etc.), Self-Refine outputs are preferred by humans and
  automatic metrics over one-step generation by the same model, ~20% absolute average improvement,
  including with GPT-4.
- The motivating examples are subjective/open-ended: making an email more polite, refactoring code
  for readability. Stated limitation: requires a strong base model with good few-shot /
  instruction-following ability; weaker models cannot self-critique usefully.

## Interpretation (flagged as inference)

- Self-Refine is the maximal self-evaluated end of the spectrum and the natural foil for the bright
  line: no external check anywhere. It improves on tasks where "correctness" is subjective
  (politeness, readability, style), which is precisely where there is no verifier to ground
  against, so self-critique is a reasonable (if unguaranteed) heuristic.
- The crucial counter-evidence the repo must pair with it: on tasks that DO have a ground truth,
  pure self-correction does not reliably help and can hurt. "Large Language Models Cannot
  Self-Correct Reasoning Yet" (Huang et al., 2310.01798) shows intrinsic self-correction (no
  external feedback) degrades reasoning accuracy; SCoRe (2409.12917) needed multi-turn RL against a
  correctness signal to make self-correction work. So Self-Refine's gains live in the
  no-ground-truth regime; extending them to checkable claims is where self-evaluation fails.
- For the repo this is the clearest illustration of WHY the verifier must be external. The repo's
  physical and material claims (Bob has a pickaxe, the chest now holds 3 iron) are checkable, so a
  Self-Refine-style "the actor critiques and refines its own success claim" is in the regime where
  self-evaluation is known to be unreliable. The actor may refine its PROSE freely; it must not be
  the judge of whether the physical/material/social outcome actually happened.

## Mechanically useful vs research contribution

- **Mechanically useful**: Self-Refine's generate-critique-revise loop is a fine pattern for
  improving the actor's PROSE and plans at test time (it is advisory by construction, no weights,
  no authority). It is NOT a safe pattern for deciding outcome success. The repo can use a
  self-refine step to draft better actions while keeping the runtime verifier as the sole judge of
  whether an action succeeded.
- **Research contribution it is NOT**: the contribution is supervision-free iterative
  self-refinement. The repo borrows the test-time refinement loop for prose, and explicitly does
  NOT borrow self-evaluation as a success signal.

## WAM layer(s) informed

Method-level. Useful for advisory prose/plan refinement across layers; a cautionary example at any
layer with a checkable outcome (Physical, Material, and the verifiable parts of Social), where the
self-as-judge configuration is known to be unreliable (2310.01798, 2409.12917).
