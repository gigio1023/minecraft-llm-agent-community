# Hypothetical Minds: Scaffolding Theory of Mind for Multi-Agent Tasks with LLMs

- title: Hypothetical Minds: Scaffolding Theory of Mind for Multi-Agent Tasks with Large Language Models
- authors: Logan Cross, Violet Xiang, Agam Bhatia, Daniel L. K. Yamins, Nick Haber (Stanford)
- year: 2024
- venue/source: ICLR 2025; arXiv 2407.07086
- arxiv_id: 2407.07086
- urls: paper https://arxiv.org/abs/2407.07086 ; hf https://huggingface.co/papers/2407.07086
- source availability: LaTeX (`papers/latex/2407.07086/iclr2025_conference.tex`), read abstract + method (ToM module) + results + limitations.

## Primary-source facts (what the paper states)

- Research question: can an LLM agent rapidly adapt to *other* agents (competitive,
  cooperative, mixed-motive) online, without the billion-step training and poor
  generalization of multi-agent RL (MARL)?
- Architecture: a generative-agents-style LLM agent (perception, memory, hierarchical
  planning) with a novel **Theory of Mind (ToM) module** and a separate **Subgoal
  module** + a hardcoded pathfinding **Action Planner**. The ToM module:
  1. **Hypothesis generation**: prompts the LLM to infer another agent's latent
     variables (strategy, goal, competence, location) as a natural-language hypothesis,
     conditioned on memory of past observations.
  2. **Hypothesis evaluation**: for each candidate hypothesis, the LLM makes a
     *counterfactual prediction* of the other agent's next behavior; the hypothesis is
     scored by whether the prediction matched the observed behavior (self-supervised
     intrinsic reward +c / -c), and the score is updated with a Rescorla-Wagner rule.
     This approximates Bayesian MAP inference over latent variables (argmax p(h | tau)).
  3. **Hypothesis refinement**: the top-k highest-valued past hypotheses are shown back
     to the LLM so it can self-improve the next hypothesis.
  4. A hypothesis crossing a validation threshold (V_thr = 0.7) is used to condition the
     high-level plan; the Subgoal module then sequences subgoals, and a hardcoded Action
     Planner turns them into primitive actions.
- Evaluation: Melting Pot MARL benchmark, 30 evaluation scenarios across Running With
  Scissors (competitive zero-sum), Collaborative Cooking Asymmetric (cooperative), and
  Prisoner's Dilemma in the Matrix (mixed-motive); tested against bots with policies
  unseen in any prompt, so strategy must be inferred online via in-context learning.
- Results: Hypothetical Minds beats LLM-agent baselines (ReAct, Reflexion, PlanReAct) on
  a large majority of scenarios in every environment, and beats MARL baselines (PPO,
  OPRE) on 3 of 4 environments despite those being trained on >1B steps. In RWS, reward
  is near zero/negative during the information-gathering phase and jumps to consistently
  positive *after* a hypothesis is validated, directly tying performance to correct
  agent modelling. Ablations show hypothesis evaluation and refinement are the critical
  components (the only difference between PlanReAct and HM is the ToM module).
- Stated limitation (theirs): on Prisoner's Dilemma, OPRE (MARL) gets the highest reward
  overall because the LLM has weak low-level spatial control (HM "frequently travels
  across the environment to pick up resources that were closer"). The honest tradeoff:
  "LLM agents provide better abstract reasoning about ... other agent's goals right out
  of the box ... but lack the low-level control necessary to maximize performance in a
  dense reward embodied environment (which RL excels at)."

## Interpretation (my reading, labeled inference)

- This is the single closest *mechanical* analogue in the literature to the project's
  advisory Social WAM. The ToM module is exactly a forward-predictive, evidence-scored
  model of another agent: predict the other agent's next behavior (the WAM's `o'` for the
  social layer), score the prediction against observed behavior (verifier-style evidence),
  keep the hypothesis only while it keeps predicting well. It "advises" the planner
  (conditions the high-level plan) but a *separate* Subgoal module and a *hardcoded* Action
  Planner own the actual action execution.
- That separation is the project's own rule in miniature: the predictive social model
  informs but does not execute; physical truth (pathfinding, primitive actions) stays with
  non-LLM machinery. The HM result is strong evidence the advisory split is workable.
- The "validated hypothesis then condition plan" loop is also a natural-language,
  LLM-native version of MBOM's (2108.01843) recursive imagine-and-mix and of the
  type-based-reasoning family in the Albrecht-Stone survey (1709.08071): maintain candidate
  models of the other, weight by predictive fit.

## Mechanically useful vs research contribution

- Mechanically useful for this repo:
  - The generate -> counterfactually-predict -> score-against-evidence -> refine loop is a
    directly adoptable design for a Social WAM that predicts another actor's next action and
    is *self-scored against the verifier-confirmed observed action*. The Rescorla-Wagner
    value update and a validation threshold give a cheap, interpretable confidence signal.
  - Cost discipline: HM uses (3 + top_k) LLM calls when no hypothesis is validated and only
    2 after validation. An advisory WAM can be made cheap by only re-hypothesizing when the
    current hypothesis stops predicting.
  - Keeping a separate hypothesis stream per other agent is a clean pattern for multi-actor
    Minecraft worlds.
- What this repo should avoid / overclaim risk:
  - HM's evidence is GPT-4-class closed-model + Melting Pot, not Minecraft and not the repo's
    runtime. Transfer the architecture, not the numbers.
  - Do not let the ToM hypothesis gate or replace action selection. In HM the hypothesis only
    *conditions* the plan; the Action Planner still owns execution. The repo rule is stronger:
    a WAM must never fill args, mark progress, or override the verifier.
  - The honest limitation (LLM weak at low-level control) is itself an argument FOR the repo's
    "LLM proposes, runtime owns physical truth" division, not against it.

## WAM layer(s) informed

- **Social WAM** (predicting another agent's strategy/goal/next action, scored by predictive fit).
- **Institutional WAM** secondarily (adapting to tit-for-tat, cooperation, and "corrective
  punishment" patterns is convention/norm-adjacent behavior).
- Depends on **Physical WAM** (the hardcoded Action Planner = the physical-truth layer the
  social prediction sits on top of).
