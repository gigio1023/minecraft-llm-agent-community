# Dreamer v1: Dream to Control - Learning Behaviors by Latent Imagination (Hafner et al., 2019)

The paper that replaced PlaNet's online planning with a policy (actor-critic) learned by
backpropagating value gradients through imagined latent rollouts. Source: LaTeX deep-read
at `papers/latex/1912.01603/` (sections/intro.tex, behavior.tex, dynamics.tex).

## Plain-language: what it introduced and why it mattered

PlaNet learned a good latent world model but chose actions by re-running an expensive
search (CEM) at every step, and that search only looked a fixed number of steps ahead, so
it was shortsighted. Dreamer fixed both problems with one idea: instead of planning online,
**learn a policy by "imagining" trajectories inside the world model and training an
actor-critic on those imagined trajectories**.

- **Imagination / rollout / dreaming**: starting from a real latent state, you let the
  world model predict a sequence of future latent states, rewards, and the policy's own
  actions, without touching the environment. Training a policy on these predicted
  trajectories is "learning in imagination."
- **Actor-critic**: two small networks. The **actor** (action model) proposes actions; the
  **critic** (value model) estimates the long-run return of a state. They improve each
  other (policy iteration): the actor maximizes the critic's value, the critic regresses
  the returns the actor achieves.
- **The decisive trick**: because the world model, reward model, actor, and critic are all
  differentiable neural networks, Dreamer can **backpropagate analytic value gradients
  straight through the imagined latent dynamics** to update the actor. This is far more
  sample-efficient than gradient-free search (PlaNet's CEM) or high-variance
  policy-gradient methods (A3C/PPO).

Why it mattered: it turned the latent world model from a thing you plan against into a
thing you *learn a fast reactive policy inside of*. On 20 visual control tasks Dreamer beat
prior model-based and model-free agents in data-efficiency, compute, and final performance,
with one hyperparameter set. This actor-critic-in-imagination template is what V2, V3, and
Dreamer 4 all inherit.

## Definitions (first use)

- **Latent imagination**: predicting future compact latent states with the world model and
  treating them as a training environment for the policy, with no real environment steps.
- **Analytic value gradients**: gradients of the predicted return with respect to the
  policy parameters, computed by differentiating through the chain
  action -> imagined next state -> imagined reward/value. Possible only because every link
  is a differentiable network; uses the reparameterization trick for continuous actions and
  straight-through estimators for discrete ones.
- **Lambda-return** (`V_lambda`): an exponentially weighted average of n-step return
  estimates that bootstraps with the learned value beyond the imagination horizon `H`. It
  balances bias and variance and lets the agent value rewards beyond the fixed horizon,
  curing PlaNet's shortsightedness.
- **Imagination horizon `H`**: how many steps the model imagines forward (Dreamer uses a
  short horizon and relies on the value model to account for everything past it).

## Primary-source facts

- **World model**: the PlaNet RSSM (deterministic + stochastic latent), trained from
  replayed experience; reused, not redesigned. "The world model is fixed while learning
  behaviors" (actor/critic gradients do not change the world-model representation).
- **Behavior learning**: imagined trajectories start at real latent states `s_t` drawn from
  the replay buffer, then roll forward `H` steps under the transition model, reward model,
  and the actor. The actor is trained to maximize the lambda-return value estimates by
  stochastic backpropagation through the dynamics; the critic regresses those lambda-returns
  (with a stop-gradient on the target).
- **Discount/termination**: in tasks with early termination, the model also predicts a
  discount/continue factor per latent state to down-weight steps by how likely the imagined
  trajectory would already have ended.
- **Contrast to other actor-critics**: A3C/PPO use high-variance Reinforce gradients with a
  value baseline; DDPG/SAC are reparameterized actor-critics but only maximize immediate
  Q-values and do not propagate gradients through learned transitions. Dreamer's distinctive
  move is propagating gradients *through the latent dynamics* over a multi-step horizon.
- **Result**: on 20 DM Control visual tasks, exceeds prior model-based (incl. PlaNet) and
  model-free agents in data-efficiency, computation time, and final performance with shared
  hyperparameters.

## Interpretation (labeled inference)

- Dreamer is the hinge between "world model as a thing you search against" (PlaNet) and
  "world model as a thing you grow a policy inside" (V2, V3, Dreamer 4, and the broader
  model-based-RL family the WAM survey treats as neighbors). The genealogy's per-step
  online planner becomes an amortized learned policy here.
- The power of imagination is sample-efficiency: a policy can be improved on cheap predicted
  trajectories without paying for real environment interaction. The cost is that the policy
  is only as good as the model; the same model-exploitation risk Ha and Schmidhuber named
  applies, now with gradient-based optimization that can exploit model errors even more
  efficiently. Lane 10 owns the compounding-error and model-exploitation treatment; this
  note marks Dreamer as where imagination-trained policies became the dominant pattern, so
  the failure mode's stakes rise.

## Mechanically useful vs research contribution

- **Mechanically useful**: the lambda-return-to-handle-horizon idea and the actor-critic
  decomposition are standard reusable RL machinery. The conceptual lesson (a compact latent
  model lets you imagine thousands of trajectories cheaply) supports the structured-state
  feasibility argument (cross-ref `wam-foundations.md` section 7).
- **Research contribution (not this repo's)**: "learn the policy by backprop through
  imagination" is a research method and is explicitly out of scope for this repo's advisory
  WAM rule (the LLM selects actions; a WAM must not become the action selector). Use Dreamer
  as the canonical example of imagination-trained control to *contrast against*, not to
  adopt as runtime authority.

## Relevance to this repo (one line)

Defines "actor-critic learned in imagination"; useful as the contrast case (an advisory WAM
predicts/evaluates, it does not train or replace the action selector the way Dreamer's actor
is trained inside the dream).
