## Primary-source facts

- **Goal**: automate the two human-labor bottlenecks of sim-to-real RL, reward design AND domain-randomization (DR) configuration, using LLMs, given only the target-task physics simulator.
- **Pipeline (3 stages)**: (1) build on Eureka to generate a reward function, but add a **safety instruction** to the prompt so the reward is safety-regularized; (2) roll out the resulting policy under perturbed simulation dynamics to build a **Reward-Aware Physics Prior (RAPP)**, a grounded sampling range over physics parameters; (3) the LLM, given the RAPP, generates DR distribution candidates; retrain policies on the synthesized reward + DR for real-world deployment.
- **Why the safety instruction (method, lines 270-278)**: optimizing the Eureka reward purely against the simulation fitness `F` often achieves a higher sim score by "over-exerting the robot motors or learning unnatural behavior," and the candidate sampler then favors rewards WITHOUT safety terms (e.g. no torque penalty). Post-hoc adding safety terms is brittle (must hand-define them; scale interactions can cause over-conservative degeneracy). So safety is injected at generation time.
- **Results (paper-stated, real-robot)**: on quadruped locomotion, DrEureka policies beat human-designed reward+DR by 34% forward velocity and 20% distance across real terrains; on dexterous manipulation, ~300% more in-hand cube rotations than the human policy in a fixed time; solves a novel "walking globe" (balance/walk on a yoga ball) task with minimal safety support.

## Interpretation (inference, labeled)

- DrEureka is the "robustness / safety of generated code" extension of the lane's core method. Its central observation is a reward-hacking instance: free `F`-maximization over generated reward code produces unsafe, unnatural behavior unless an explicit constraint is generated alongside.
- This is a second, independent demonstration (after Auto MC-Reward's back-and-forth gaming) that generated-reward search WILL exploit whatever the fitness leaves unconstrained. The fix here is generation-time safety instruction + a grounded prior, not a richer post-hoc metric.
- For the repo, the lesson is that an advisory-WAM predictor or skill generated by a loop must be bounded by explicit constraints AT GENERATION (schema, permission, safety contract), because the loop will otherwise find degenerate optima against any single score, especially one as under-specified as a social outcome.

## Mechanically useful vs research contribution

- **Mechanically useful**:
    - **Generation-time constraint injection**: put the safety/authority contract into the proposing prompt, do not bolt it on afterward. For the repo: the `author_mineflayer_action` prompt and any WAM-predictor-generation prompt should carry the runtime-authority and safety constraints up front.
    - **Reward-Aware Physics Prior** is a pattern for grounding an LLM's free generation in measured rollout behavior (perturb, observe, hand the observed-feasible range back as context) rather than letting it sample blind. Analog: feed a generator the verifier-measured behavior envelope, not just the task text.
- **What to avoid / overclaim**:
    - DrEureka is sim-to-real robotics; the repo is neither. Borrow the safety-at-generation and grounded-prior ideas, not the sim-to-real claim.
    - It still relies on a numeric fitness `F` (sim score) and a physics simulator. Open-ended social Minecraft has neither; the lane bound holds.
