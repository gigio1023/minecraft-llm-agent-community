# POET: Paired Open-Ended Trailblazer

- **title**: Paired Open-Ended Trailblazer (POET): Endlessly Generating Increasingly Complex and Diverse Learning Environments and Their Solutions
- **authors**: Rui Wang, Joel Lehman, Jeff Clune, Kenneth O. Stanley (Uber AI Labs)
- **year**: 2019
- **venue/source**: arXiv 1901.01753 (Uber AI Labs tech report; later GECCO 2019 line of work)
- **arxiv_id**: 1901.01753
- **urls**: paper https://arxiv.org/abs/1901.01753 ; code https://github.com/uber-research/poet ; hf https://huggingface.co/papers/1901.01753
- **source availability**: latex (papers/latex/1901.01753/main.tex), read intro/background/method/results/discussion + supplemental mutation and transfer sections.

## Primary-source facts (what the paper states/shows)

- **Research question**: can an algorithm generate the problems (environments) at the same time as it solves them, building its own expanding curriculum, rather than humans posing a fixed benchmark?
- **Method**: POET maintains a population of (environment, paired agent) pairs. It does three things in a loop: (1) generate new environments by mutating the parameter vector of an active environment; (2) optimize each paired agent with Evolution Strategies (ES) on its own environment; (3) periodically attempt transfer, copying a better-performing agent from another environment into a given environment if it scores higher (stepping-stone reuse).
- **Minimal criterion (MC), the learnability gate**: a generated child environment is admitted to the population only if it is "neither too hard nor too easy" for the current agents. Concretely (experiment values): the parent's paired agent must clear a reproduction-eligibility reward (>= 200, just below the full success threshold 230) before its environment may reproduce; and a child is kept only if `50 <= E_child(theta_child) <= 300`. If more children pass the MC than the per-reproduction cap (max_children = 512), the ones with the lowest score (hardest among the admissible) are admitted up to the cap. The MC is inherited from minimal-criterion coevolution (MCC), where each population earns the right to reproduce by satisfying a criterion against the other.
- **Open-endedness via diversity**: POET keeps environmental diversity through a novelty measure (in original POET this measure is domain-specific, e.g. terrain roughness, stump heights, gap sizes). Stepping-stone transfer plus a novelty-maintained population is what lets POET reach environments that direct optimization (and even a hand-built direct-path curriculum control) cannot solve.
- **Action / observation space**: the agent is a 2-D bipedal walker (Box2D BipedalWalkerHardcore-style), controlled by a neural network; the environment is a parameterized obstacle course (gaps, stumps, roughness). State = walker proprioception + terrain; action = joint torques.
- **Training objective**: per-agent, maximize ES return on the paired environment; system-level, sustain an expanding tree of solved-and-novel environments.
- **Evaluation protocol + metrics**: the authors are explicit that open-ended systems are hard to evaluate. A footnote-level author discussion (in the source) states open-ended systems "by definition cannot be asked to reproduce a defined target result," so you cannot run POET N times and report mean score against a fixed target. They instead show (a) POET-produced agents solve environments that ES-from-scratch and a direct-path curriculum control cannot, and (b) transfer ablation: removing transfer collapses the system's reach.
- **Results**: POET produces a diverse range of walking behaviors solving a wide range of courses; many target environments are unsolvable by direct optimization or by a same-budget direct-path curriculum; transfer between environments is essential.

## Interpretation (flagged as inference)

- POET is the cleanest articulation of my lane's central question: keep generating new, solvable-but-not-trivial challenges so a system never saturates. The MC (`not too easy and not too hard`) is the operational definition of "learnable", and reproduction-eligibility (parent agent must be competent) is the operational definition of "the curriculum has made progress here, so branch from it."
- The paper frames POET as an implicit automatic curriculum builder running many overlapping curricula simultaneously (its own words). That is a direct analog to the repo's hand-authored maturity ladder (proto-social, organization, settlement, village, society): the ladder is the human spine; a POET-like loop could branch sub-curricula off each rung.
- The honest-bound the paper itself supplies (open-ended systems have no defined target, so evaluation is genuinely hard and controversial) is exactly the warning for the wave-4 thesis at the upper WAM layers: there is no crisp 99%-success target for a "society", so a POET-style loop cannot hill-climb a single scalar there.

## Mechanically useful vs research contribution

- **Mechanically useful for this repo**: (1) the MC gate (admit a generated scenario only if it is solvable-but-not-trivial for current actors) is directly portable as a scenario filter; the repo's verifier supplies exactly the score that the MC thresholds on. (2) Stepping-stone transfer (reuse a policy/skill that worked in scenario A as the seed for scenario B) maps to reusing a learned skill or a WAM predictor across social-material scenarios. (3) Many-overlapping-curricula and the parent-eligibility rule are a concrete recipe for which rung of the maturity ladder to branch from next.
- **Not a contribution to copy / overclaim risk**: POET's research claim is open-ended invention in a 2-D physics domain with a clean scalar reward. The repo must not reframe its work as "POET for Minecraft societies." The MC and ANNECS-style gating only work where the verifier gives a clean solved/unsolved label, i.e. the Physical and Material layers; at the Social/Institutional layers "solved" is contested, so the gate degrades. POET also has no notion of obligation, possession, or other-regarding cost.
- **A noted POET limitation (theirs + spotted)**: original POET's novelty/EC measure is hand-coded per domain (a re-design burden, fixed by Enhanced POET, see `2003.08536-enhanced-poet.md`); the encoding is bounded so difficulty "maxes out"; reward functions are not generated (each environment shares one reward). For a social world, the per-domain hand-coded interestingness measure is the part that does not transfer.

## WAM layer(s) informed

Method-level, primarily Institutional/settlement (it is a curriculum-over-environments engine) with the gate operating where Physical/Material verification is clean. It does not itself model Social consequences; it supplies the loop structure that could schedule social-material scenarios.
