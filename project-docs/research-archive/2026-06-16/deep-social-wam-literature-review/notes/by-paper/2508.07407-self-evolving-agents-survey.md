# A Comprehensive Survey of Self-Evolving AI Agents

- **title**: A Comprehensive Survey of Self-Evolving AI Agents: A New Paradigm Bridging Foundation Models and Lifelong Agentic Systems
- **authors**: large multi-institution author list (survey).
- **year**: 2025 (published 2025-08).
- **venue/source**: arXiv survey.
- **arxiv_id**: 2508.07407
- **urls**: paper https://arxiv.org/abs/2508.07407
- **source availability**: latex (tarball, 4 .tex files). Deep-read (framework + taxonomy + safety sections).

## Primary-source facts (what the survey states)

- **Scope**: reviews techniques that let agent systems automatically improve from interaction data and environmental feedback, bridging static foundation models and "lifelong agentic systems."
- **Unified conceptual framework (the useful contribution)**: a closed feedback loop of four components:
    1. **System Inputs** (task spec: task-level or instance-level),
    2. **Agent System** (single- or multi-agent),
    3. **Environment** (provides feedback signals "derived from predefined evaluation metrics" / "via proxy metrics"),
    4. **Optimiser** (updates the agent via a defined search space and optimisation algorithm until a performance threshold is met).
    The loop terminates on a performance threshold or convergence criterion.
- **What can evolve**: the survey systematically reviews evolving the foundation model itself, prompts, memory, tools, workflows/topologies, and inter-agent communication; plus domain-specific evolution (biomedicine, programming, finance).
- **Cross-cited companion taxonomy**: it names a concurrent survey (Gao et al., "A Survey of Self-Evolving Agents: On Path to Artificial Super Intelligence", arXiv 2507.21046) organised around **what to evolve, when to evolve, how to evolve** (and, per that work, where). The two surveys are complementary axes.
- **Safety framing**: proposes "Three Laws of Self-Evolving AI Agents" (Endure = safety adaptation; Excel = performance preservation; Evolve = autonomous optimisation, subordinate to the first two) and a dedicated discussion of evaluation, safety, and ethics. It flags centralised-coordinator single-point-of-failure risk in multi-agent self-evolution.
- **Reproducibility**: N/A (survey). Names EvoAgentX as the first open-source framework instantiating the MASE loop.

## Interpretation (flagged as inference)

- This survey gives my lane its **map of the design space**: an autoresearch loop can change many things (model, prompt, memory, tools, workflow). For this repo, the *runtime owns truth* rule means only some of these are loop-eligible (prompts, memory/principles, advisory-WAM parameters, skill candidates) - never the verifier, gates, or schemas. The framework's "Environment provides feedback via *proxy* metrics" phrasing is itself a caution: the loop optimizes a proxy, so the proxy's fidelity is everything (consistent with the reward-hacking and collapse cornerstones).
- The "Three Laws" ordering (safety/preservation before autonomy) is a tidy restatement of the repo's advisory constraint: improvement must not violate runtime truth.

## Mechanically useful vs research contribution

- **Mechanically useful for this repo**: (1) the four-component loop is a clean checklist for designing a repo autoresearch loop (Inputs = scenario spec; Agent System = ActorSoul + actor policy + optional advisory WAM; Environment = Mineflayer runtime + verifier; Optimiser = the coding/refinement agent). (2) the what-to-evolve taxonomy tells you which knobs are safe to expose (prompts, memory, skills, WAM params) vs off-limits (verifier, schemas, gates). (3) the safety ordering gives language for "advisory, never overriding."
- **Research contribution NOT to copy**: it is a survey; it contributes a taxonomy, not a method. Do not cite it as a result. Use it to position and to deconflict (it overlaps the wave-3 `research-area-*` files and the H-lanes; cite, do not re-survey).
- **Where it supports/bounds the thesis**: supports - it confirms self-evolving loops are a coherent, active paradigm with a shared structure matching ENPIRE. Bounds - it repeatedly notes the feedback is a *proxy metric*, and devotes a section to safety/evaluation gaps, underscoring that the loop is only as trustworthy as that proxy.
