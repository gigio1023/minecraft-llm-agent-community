## Primary-source facts (what the survey states)

- **Definition of ACL**: "a family of mechanisms that automatically adapt the distribution of training data by learning to adjust the selection of learning situations to the capabilities of DRL agents." Formally, learn a task-selection function D: H -> T (H = history of interactions) that maximizes a target-task performance metric P over T_target after N training steps. ACL is thus a special case of meta-learning (D is learned to improve further learning).
- **Three-axis typology** (the survey's organizing contribution):
  1. **WHY use ACL**: improve performance/sample-efficiency on a restricted task set; solve hard/sparse-reward tasks via auxiliary tasks or organized exploration; train generalist agents (incl. Sim2Real via domain randomization, and multi-agent self-play); train multi-goal agents; organize open-ended exploration when the achievable-goal set is unknown.
  2. **WHAT does ACL control** (the lever): environments, goals, reward functions, or adversarial opponents.
  3. **WHAT does ACL optimize** (the surrogate objective, because the true objective is rarely directly optimizable): learning progress, reward/intermediate difficulty, diversity/novelty, or regret.
- **Scope note**: the survey explicitly *excludes* population-based methods (it names POET and IMGEP) from its DRL-typology, and distinguishes ACL (mechanisms that *explicitly* optimize the organization of training data) from *emergent curricula* (a by-product of, e.g., on-policy learning). It also notes the field "lacks theoretical foundations" and is empirically driven.
- **Related fields**: transfer learning, continual learning (ACL assumes the agent controls the learning scenario; continual learning assumes unforeseen environment change), policy distillation (complementary, for multi-task settings).

## Interpretation (flagged as inference)

- This survey is the *map* for my lane. Its WHAT-does-ACL-control axis is exactly the deconfliction line with sibling lane H3: **I own the `environments` and `goals` levers; H3 owns the `reward functions` lever.** The adversarial-opponent lever is the UED/self-play family (PAIRED, ACCEL).
- Its WHAT-does-ACL-optimize axis enumerates the four surrogate signals my theme file must compare for a social world: learning progress (verifier-friendly: measured success change), reward/difficulty (the POET MC), diversity (QD/MAP-Elites), regret (UED). For the repo, learning-progress and difficulty are the verifier-groundable ones; diversity and regret need a reference (a best policy or a behavior space) that is hard to define socially.
- The ACL-vs-emergent-curriculum distinction matters for the thesis: an ENPIRE-style loop driven by a coding agent is *explicit* ACL on the environment/goal lever, not an emergent by-product; that is what makes it a designable component rather than a hope.

## Mechanically useful vs research contribution

- **Mechanically useful**: the **3-axis vocabulary** (why / what-controlled / what-optimized) is a ready scaffold for the repo to specify any scenario-generation component precisely: state the objective (e.g. probe weak-commons robustness), the lever (generate scenarios = environments+goals), and the surrogate (learning progress, scored by the verifier). The survey's catalog of surrogate objectives tells the repo which ones are verifier-groundable.
- **Not a contribution to copy / overclaim risk**: it is a 2020 survey of DRL ACL; its methods are RL-from-scratch curriculum mechanisms, not LLM-actor scenario generation, and not embodied social tasks. The repo should borrow the *taxonomy*, not assume any specific DRL mechanism transfers. The survey itself flags the field's lack of theory and that surrogate objectives can diverge from the true objective (a Goodhart-adjacent caution echoed by OMNI).
- **Limitation**: pre-LLM; excludes population-based open-endedness (POET) by scope; no social/multi-agent-obligation content; surrogate-objective failure modes are noted but not resolved.
