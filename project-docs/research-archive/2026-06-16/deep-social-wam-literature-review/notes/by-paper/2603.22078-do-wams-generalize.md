# Do WAMs Generalize Better than VLAs? (robustness comparative study)

- **title**: (comparative study) Do World Action Models Generalize Better than VLAs?
- **year**: 2026
- **venue/source**: arXiv
- **arxiv_id**: 2603.22078
- **urls**: LaTeX at `papers/latex/2603.22078/`
- **source availability**: latex (full)

## Primary-source facts

- **Question**: do WAMs generalize/robustify better than VLAs, and is explicit world-model use necessary (vs foundation policies that may already implicitly model dynamics)?
- **Setup**: evaluate SOTA VLAs vs released WAMs on **LIBERO-Plus** (7 perturbation types, single-arm) and **RoboTwin 2.0-Plus** (bimanual) under visual/language perturbations.
- **WAM comparison table** (params / backbone / causal-pred / AR-gen):
  - VPP 1.5B (Stable Video Diffusion)
  - GE-Act 2.2B (LTX-Video-2B)
  - Cosmos-Policy 2B (Cosmos-Predict2-2B)
  - LingBot-VA 5.3B (Wan2.2-5B)
  - DreamZero 14B (Wan2.1-14B)
  - "Pretrain Free" = no task-agnostic embodied pretraining; "Causal Pred." = action conditioned on generated visual state (or vice versa).
- **Results**: WAMs are robust to noise/lighting/layout perturbations (LingBot-VA 74.2% on RoboTwin2.0-Plus; Cosmos-Policy 82.2% on LIBERO-Plus); robustness attributed to spatiotemporal priors from the video backbone. VLAs (π0.5) and hybrids (MOTUS, VLA-JEPA) can match but need curated diverse datasets and/or explicit dynamic-prediction objectives. **WAM advantage = simplicity of the embodied pretraining phase.**
- **Latency**: "a single inference step being at least 4.8x slower than π0.5." Fast-WAM and GigaWorld-Policy mitigate by generating actions only at inference (future prediction during training); Fast-WAM's LIBERO-Plus robustness "collapses sharply when the training data lacks diversity," suggesting **joint-denoising designs depend more on training-data diversity than IDM-style designs that explicitly condition action on a predicted future state.** Inference latency "remains substantial."
- **"World Models as Learned Simulators" framing** (related work): "world models are most operationally useful when treated as learned simulators ... imagined rollouts must remain action-grounded, decision-relevant, and stable under closed-loop distribution shift." Crucially: "**simulation does not need to occur in pixel space. Latent dynamics models - RSSMs, predictive state representations - enable compact rollouts as long as task-relevant structure is preserved.**" And the objective-mismatch caution: "improvements in likelihood or visual fidelity do not necessarily translate to better planning" (cites lambert2020 objective mismatch, action-hallucination).

## Interpretation

- Two findings transfer to the project's argument: (1) the cost gap (4.8x per step) is intrinsic to pixel WAMs; (2) **IDM-style (cascaded, condition-action-on-predicted-future) designs are more data-efficient and less diversity-hungry than joint-denoising designs** - relevant if the project ever learns a structured predictor, favoring a cascaded structure-prediction-then-consequence approach over a monolithic joint model.
- The "learned simulator, not pixel space" passage is independent external support that latent/structured rollouts are legitimate WAMs, and that visual fidelity is the wrong objective for control - central to this lane.

## Mechanically useful vs research contribution

- **Useful**: empirical cost data (4.8x); the data-efficiency argument for cascaded/IDM-style designs; the "simulator value lies in control-reliable prediction, not perceptual realism" framing.
- **Avoid/overclaim**: treating these manipulation-benchmark robustness numbers as evidence about social WAMs.
