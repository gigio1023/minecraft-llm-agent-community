## Primary-source facts (what the paper states)

Formal definitions (Section "def", `020-def.tex`), with `o` = observation, `l` = language instruction, `a` = action, `o'` = next-step observation:

- **VLA**: `L_VLA = E[-log p(a | o, l)]`. Reactive observation->action; no dynamics model.
- **World Model (WM)**: `L_WM = E[-log p(o' | o, a)]`. Predictive transition only; "probabilistic propagator of states." No action selection.
- **WAM**: `L_WAM = E[-log p(o', a | o, l)]`. Two required criteria:
  1. **Forward Predictive Modeling**: forecast physical evolution via a quantifiable representation of `o'` — "explicit visual predictions (pixel video, optical flow) OR implicit physical representations (physics-grounded latent spaces)."
  2. **Coupled Action Generation**: deduce motor commands `a` "by strictly aligning them with the anticipated future states `o'`."
- **Cascaded WAM**: `p(o',a|o,l) = p(a|o',o,l)·p(o'|o,l)` (imagine future, then derive action).
- **Joint WAM**: directly model `p(o',a|o,l)`, state prediction and action co-optimized in a shared representation.

Disambiguation the survey draws (verbatim positions):
- **WAM vs VAM (Video Action Models)**: WAM is "a broader, modality-independent superset." "video is merely one possible proxy for modeling the world." WAMs may use "single-image state transitions, dense point clouds, or multi-sensory modalities like tactile and force feedback." "World" = internalization of physical laws/causal dynamics, not commitment to pixels.
- **WAM vs Video Policies**: a video policy merely inherits a video-generation backbone to map `p(a|o)` directly; a WAM "requires an active predictive commitment — synthesis of the next state `o'` is an explicit component of the model's reasoning and output."
- **WAM vs AWM (Action World Model)**: same math `p(o',a|o,l)`, but WAM repositions the system as a primary Agent category (World + Action co-equal), not an "augmented simulator."

Background lineage (`030-wm.tex`):
- Action-conditioned WMs split into **Explicit** (pixel/video: ACVP, CDNA, SV2P, diffusion WMs) and **Implicit** (latent: PlaNet/RSSM, Dreamer series, TransDreamer/TSSM; predictive-representation JEPA / I-JEPA / V-JEPA 2 / LeWorldModel that "predict future latent embeddings instead of reconstructing pixels with substantially lower reconstruction overhead").
- **World Models for VLA** are used four ways: (a) imitation learning (synthetic trajectories), (b) RL (imagined rollouts as surrogate environment — Dreamer line), (c) reward modeling (VIPER, Diffusion Reward, SRPO from V-JEPA2 latent), (d) **policy evaluation** as data-driven simulators (Ctrl-World, Veo Robotics red-teaming, reproducible closed-loop rollouts).

Data (`050-data.tex`): WAMs need `(o_t, a_t, o_{t+1})` triplets but can also ingest unpaired action-free video. Four data sources: robot teleoperation, portable human demos (UMI), simulation, internet ego-centric video. The entire embodied-data landscape table is robot manipulation. VLA needs paired `(o,a)`; WM needs action-free `(o,o')`; WAM "unified data digestion" sits at the intersection.

Evaluation (`060-eval.tex`): decoupled into (1) world-modeling capability — Visual Fidelity (PSNR/SSIM/LPIPS/DreamSim/DINO/FVD), Physical Commonsense (VideoPhy, PhyGenBench, VBench-2.0, WorldModelBench's 5 physical-law checks, Physics-IQ), Action Plausibility (WorldSimBench Implicit Manipulative Eval; **"Wow, wo, val!" IDM Turing Test** — apply an inverse-dynamics model to generated video, execute inferred actions in the real world; many visually convincing models "collapse to nearly zero success"); and (2) action-policy capability — 40+ manipulation benchmarks (MetaWorld, RLBench, LIBERO, ManiSkill, RoboCasa, COLOSSEUM/LIBERO-Plus perturbations, RoboTwin, etc.). All robot/manipulation; none social or material.

Open challenges (`070-oppo.tex`): architectural coupling has "no systematic controlled study"; "it remains empirically unclear whether explicit visual prediction is strictly necessary for physical grounding." Key passage: "removing the future prediction head at test time does not necessarily degrade downstream control performance," motivating a **latent-predictive (JEPA-style)** approach that "sidesteps the pixel-prediction bottleneck by focusing on causal invariants." Also: "latency tax" (DreamZero 7Hz << 50Hz VLA standard); joint causal-consistency eval is missing (proposes Counterfactual Consistency, Foresight-Conditioned Success); **prediction-integrated safety** — "world predictions can be checked against physical constraints before predicted actions are executed."

## Interpretation (my reading — inference)

- The canonical WAM is robot-manipulation and overwhelmingly pixel/video. But the *definition* explicitly admits "implicit physical representations (physics-grounded latent spaces)" and is "modality-independent." A structured-state Minecraft WAM (typed Minecraft+social `o`, predicted social-material deltas `o'`) sits squarely in the survey's implicit/latent branch — it is an adaptation of the definition, not the mainstream instantiation.
- The survey's own open challenges (latent-predictive over pixel; prediction-integrated safety/verification; joint causal-consistency eval) are the *strongest insider argument* that pixel video is not load-bearing — the field is itself questioning whether pixels are necessary.

## Mechanically useful vs research contribution

- **Mechanically useful**: the exact `p(o',a|o,l)` vocabulary; Cascaded-vs-Joint distinction; the "WM for VLA" quad (esp. (d) policy-evaluation-as-simulator and (c) reward modeling) as the precedent for an advisory predictor/evaluator; the eval taxonomy (visual / physical-commonsense / action-plausibility) which the project should replace with social-material-transition correctness.
- **Would be overclaim**: claiming a structured social WAM is "a WAM" in the canonical pixel sense, or that it inherits the survey's empirical generalization results (those are robot-manipulation, pixel-video). The project's contribution is the *social-material transition target*, not re-deriving WAM theory.

All four (Physical / Material / Social / Institutional) — this is the definitional anchor for the whole review.
