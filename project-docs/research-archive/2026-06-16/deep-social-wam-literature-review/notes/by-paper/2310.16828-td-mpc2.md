## Plain-language: what it introduced and why it mattered

TD-MPC2 is the modern, scalable realization of the value-equivalence idea (MuZero's "predict only
what matters for acting") applied to continuous control, with two practical headlines:

1. It learns an **implicit, decoder-free world model**: it never reconstructs observations. It
   only learns to predict **outcomes (returns) conditioned on a sequence of actions**, via
   joint-embedding latent prediction + reward prediction + temporal-difference (TD) value learning.
2. It is **scalable and robust**: the same single set of hyperparameters works across 104 diverse
   continuous-control tasks and multiple robot embodiments, and performance improves predictably as
   you grow the model (its predecessor TD-MPC got *worse* when naively scaled; TD-MPC2 gets better).

Why it mattered: it is the cleanest large-scale demonstration that a **control-centric latent model
that throws away reconstruction** can be trained robustly on big, mixed-quality (uncurated) datasets
and used to control many embodiments with one config. It also publicly released 300+ checkpoints,
which is unusually open for this corner of model-based RL.

## Definitions (first use)

- **Implicit / decoder-free world model**: a world model with no decoder and no reconstruction
  loss; it predicts the consequences relevant to control (here, future latent embeddings, rewards,
  and returns) rather than future observations.
- **Joint-embedding prediction**: training the latent dynamics by predicting the *encoder's own
  latent* of the next observation (with a stop-gradient on the target), rather than predicting the
  raw next observation. (This is the JEPA family idea, predict in representation space.)
- **Temporal-difference (TD) learning**: updating a value estimate toward a "TD target" made of the
  observed reward plus the discounted value of the next state, rather than the full episode return.
- **Model-Predictive Control (MPC)**: plan a finite action sequence, execute the first action,
  replan after the next observation.
- **Model Predictive Path Integral (MPPI)**: a sampling-based, derivative-free trajectory optimizer
  used as the planner; it samples action sequences from a Gaussian, scores them by rolling them out
  in the latent model, and updates the Gaussian toward the best.
- **Policy prior**: a learned reactive policy that proposes good actions to warm-start and guide the
  MPPI planner and to cut TD-learning cost.
- **SimNorm (simplicial normalization)**: TD-MPC2's latent normalization, projecting the latent into
  several fixed-dimensional simplices via softmax, which biases the representation toward sparsity
  and is reported to be essential for training stability.
- **Behavior cloning (mentioned as the thing being avoided)**: training a policy to imitate
  near-expert demonstrations; the paper argues this limits usable data because it needs near-expert
  trajectories, whereas RL can extract good behavior from uncurated, mixed-quality data.

## Primary-source facts

- **Five components**: encoder `z = h(s)`, latent dynamics `z' = d(z, a)`, reward `r_hat = R(z, a)`,
  terminal value `q_hat = Q(z, a)`, and a policy prior `a_hat = p(z)`. No decoder.
- **Model objective**: jointly minimize joint-embedding prediction loss (latent of next state, with
  stop-grad target) + reward prediction (cross-entropy in log-space) + value prediction (cross-entropy
  to a TD target). Reward and value are predicted as discrete regression in a log-transformed space,
  explicitly citing DreamerV3's transformation trick to handle reward magnitudes that differ across
  tasks.
- **Planning**: MPPI over latent rollouts, bootstrapping with the learned terminal value beyond the
  horizon H (so it estimates the full RL objective, not just within-horizon return); a fraction of
  sampled action sequences come from the policy prior; warm-started across steps.
- **Architecture**: MLPs with LayerNorm + Mish; SimNorm latent normalization; an ensemble of 5
  Q-functions with EMA targets.
- **Why decoder-free (stated)**: "accurately predicting raw future observations ... over long time
  horizons is a difficult problem, and does not necessarily lead to effective control"; the goal is a
  "maximally useful" model that predicts outcomes (returns), not observations.
- **Results**: outperforms model-based and model-free baselines across 104 tasks (DMControl,
  Meta-World, ManiSkill2, MyoSuite) with one hyperparameter set; trains a single 317M-param world
  model spanning 80 tasks, multiple embodiments, and action spaces; capabilities increase with model
  and data size; 300+ checkpoints, datasets, and code released.

## Interpretation (labeled inference)

- TD-MPC2 is the genealogy's "value equivalence, now scalable and open" node, the bridge from MuZero's
  principle to a practical, multi-embodiment, single-config system with public weights. For a newcomer,
  it is the strongest evidence that the decoder-free / value-equivalent / JEPA-style branch is not just
  a clever idea but a robust, scalable engineering reality.
- It also closes a loop in the lineage: it imports DreamerV3's log-space prediction trick, showing the
  two branches (reconstruction-based Dreamer and decoder-free value-equivalent) cross-pollinate.
- For this review's central argument, TD-MPC2 is the cleanest demonstration that "predict outcomes, not
  pixels" works at scale, supporting the case that a structured-state social predictor should target
  consequences (deltas, costs), not reconstruction (cross-ref `wam-foundations.md` sections 3 and 7).

## Mechanically useful vs research contribution

- **Mechanically useful**: the decoder-free, outcome-focused objective is the strongest precedent for a
  lean predictor that targets consequences. SimNorm and log-space discrete regression are reusable
  stabilization tricks. The MPC-with-a-policy-prior pattern shows how a learned model and a cheap
  reactive proposer can cooperate, a useful analogy for an advisory predictor plus the LLM proposer
  (the LLM proposes, the predictor scores), though the repo's predictor must stay advisory.
- **Research contribution (not this repo's)**: scalable model-based control via MPPI planning over a
  learned latent model is a research line. The repo borrows the "predict outcomes, not observations"
  principle and the stabilization tricks; it does not adopt MPPI planning as a runtime action selector.

## Relevance to this repo (one line)

Strongest scalable, open precedent for a decoder-free "predict outcomes not pixels" world model; the
outcome-focused objective and stabilization tricks transfer, the MPPI planner-as-policy does not.
