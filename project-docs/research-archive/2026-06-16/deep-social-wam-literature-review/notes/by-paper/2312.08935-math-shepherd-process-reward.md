## Primary-source facts

- **Research question**: process reward models (PRMs, step-level) are stronger than outcome
  reward models (ORMs, final-answer-level) but PRMs normally need expensive human step
  labels (PRM800K). Can the per-step labels be constructed AUTOMATICALLY?
- **Core mechanism (automatic process supervision)**: a step's quality is defined as "its
  potential to deduce the correct answer" (MCTS-inspired). For a target step s_i, a
  fine-tuned "completer" LLM decodes N subsequent reasoning paths to final answers; the
  step label depends on how many of those N rollouts reach the gold answer a*.
  - **Hard estimation (HE)**: label = 1 if ANY rollout reaches a*, else 0.
  - **Soft estimation (SE)**: label = (count of rollouts reaching a*) / N (empirical
    success frequency).
  - The PRM is then trained with cross-entropy over these auto-generated per-step labels.
- **Two usage scenarios**:
  - **Verification (reranking)**: best-of-N (N=256); solution score = minimum step score
    across the solution; also a self-consistency + RM variant.
  - **Reinforcement**: step-by-step PPO that gives reward at the end of each reasoning step
    (vs ORM-PPO, reward only at the end).
- **Quoted RL numbers (greedy)**: Mistral-7B GSM8K 77.9 -> 84.1, MATH 28.6 -> 33.0;
  LLaMA2-7B GSM8K 66.6 -> 73.2, MATH 19.2 -> 21.6. ORM-PPO baselines are weaker (e.g.
  Mistral ORM-PPO 81.8 / 31.3).
- **Quoted verification-enhanced numbers**: Mistral-7B after step-by-step PPO, then
  self-consistency + Math-Shepherd verification, reaches GSM8K 89.1 and MATH500 43.5.
  Larger generators with Shepherd verification: DeepSeek-67B GSM8K 93.3; LLaMA2-70B 93.2;
  MATH self-consistency + Shepherd in the 45-48 range.
- **PRM vs ORM**: PRM consistently beats self-consistency and ORM across generators; the
  advantage is larger on harder MATH than on GSM8K (GSM8K has few steps, so ORM is already
  efficient there). Data efficiency: PRM beats ORM by ~4 accuracy points at 10k training
  instances. Out-of-distribution Hungarian exam: PRM beats ORM by 9 points. Auto-annotation
  quality beats NLI/rule baselines (85.0% acc / 2.05 loss vs 75.6 / 3.27).
- **Reproducibility**: models and a dataset are on Hugging Face (peiyi9979/math-shepherd-*),
  so partially reproducible at the artifact level; the accuracy numbers are self-reported
  by the authors with no third-party reproduction described in the source.

## Interpretation (labeled inference)

- Math-Shepherd is the founding "auto-label the process without humans" result, and it is
  the natural thing to compare the repo's verifier against. But the comparison reveals a
  sharp difference in KIND, not just degree: Math-Shepherd's per-step label is a MONTE-CARLO
  ESTIMATE of future success probability (HE = any rollout succeeds; SE = fraction that
  succeed), so the label is itself a noisy, learned/estimated signal, and the paper notes
  this introduces false positives, especially as N grows.
- A deterministic runtime verifier produces a per-transition label by CHECKING the world
  state exactly, with zero estimation noise and no rollouts. So the repo already owns the
  thing Math-Shepherd has to approximate: a clean per-step (per-transition) label.

## Relevance to this repo (mechanical import vs research contribution)

- **Mechanical import**: (1) the PRM > ORM lesson transfers as "score per transition, not
  per episode": per-delta checking gives precise credit assignment and catches
  right-outcome-via-wrong-path, which is the repo's stated anti-laundering goal. (2) The
  step-score-is-the-minimum aggregation (a solution is only as good as its worst step) is a
  usable rule for scoring a multi-step plan against per-transition verifier results.
- **Why a learned reward model is the more hackable choice (tie)**: Math-Shepherd's
  estimated PRM labels are gameable in exactly the way a deterministic check is not, an
  intermediate step can look high-potential by chance over N rollouts without being correct.
  The repo's deterministic verifier removes the estimation surface that makes a learned PRM
  hackable; choosing a learned PRM over the verifier would reintroduce it.
- **Research contribution it is NOT**: auto-process-supervision is support tooling; the
  repo borrows the PRM-over-ORM PRINCIPLE and the per-step labeling intuition, not the MC
  estimator (the verifier replaces the estimator with an exact check).
- **What it does not cover**: math-answer domain only; the "gold answer" anchor is a clean
  checkable target, which the repo has at Physical/Material but not at Social/Institutional;
  the MC estimator's noise is a feature of the no-exact-checker setting the repo's verifier
  avoids.
