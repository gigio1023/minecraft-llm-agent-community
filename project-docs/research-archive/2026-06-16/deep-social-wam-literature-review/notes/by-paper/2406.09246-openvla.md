- **arxiv_id**: 2406.09246
- **urls**: paper https://arxiv.org/abs/2406.09246 ; weights https://huggingface.co/openvla/openvla-7b (MIT, ~1.37M downloads) ; LaTeX at `papers/latex/2406.09246/`

- **WAM layer(s)**: Physical (control). Reactive `p(a|o,l)`, no `o'`, so not a WAM. Important as the *open* reproducible VLA reference.

Part of the VLA-canon set missing from wave-1. OpenVLA is the open-weights, fully-
reproducible counterpart to RT-2: the VLA the community can actually run and fine-tune.

## Primary-source facts

- **Research question**: VLAs like RT-2 are closed and there is little public guidance
  on fine-tuning them efficiently. Can an open 7B VLA match or beat closed models, and
  be cheaply adaptable?
- **Model**: 7B-parameter VLA built on the **Prismatic-7B** VLM. Three parts: (1) a
  **fused two-part vision encoder** that concatenates **DINOv2 + SigLIP** features
  channel-wise (DINOv2 adds spatial reasoning useful for control), (2) a 2-layer MLP
  **projector** into the language embedding space, (3) a **Llama 2 7B** LLM backbone.
- **Action space / tokenization**: predicts 7-DoF end-effector control. Follows
  RT-2's "actions as text tokens": each action dimension discretized into **256 bins**,
  but bin edges set by the **1st-99th percentile** of training actions (quantile
  binning, more robust to outliers than RT-2's min-max). The 256 least-used Llama
  tokens are overwritten as action tokens; trained with next-token cross-entropy on
  the action tokens only.
- **Dataset shape**: **970k real robot demonstrations** from the **Open X-Embodiment
  (OXE)** collection (70+ datasets, 2M+ trajectories pooled). Curated to single-arm
  end-effector control with at least one third-person camera; mixture weights borrowed
  from Octo (down-weight low-diversity datasets).
- **Training objective**: behavioral cloning via next-token prediction over action
  tokens. Notable training findings: fine-tuning the vision encoder (not freezing it,
  unlike standard VLM practice) is crucial; train for many epochs until action-token
  accuracy passes 95%; 224x224 resolution suffices (384 gives no gain at 3x cost).
- **Evaluation**: outperforms **RT-2-X (55B)** by **+16.5% absolute** success across
  29 tasks / multiple embodiments with **7x fewer parameters**; beats from-scratch
  Diffusion Policy by +20.4% in fine-tuning settings.
- **Efficiency / availability**: fine-tunable on consumer GPUs via **LoRA**; servable
  via quantization with no measured success-rate hit; **6 Hz on one RTX 4090**.
  Trained on 64 A100s for 14 days (~21,500 A100-hours). Full open release: 7B
  checkpoint, modular PyTorch training codebase (FSDP, FlashAttention, LoRA, quantized
  inference), OXE data loaders, remote inference server.

## Interpretation (inference)

- OpenVLA's contribution is **openness + an efficient adaptation recipe**, not a new
  paradigm: it is RT-2's recipe (web-VLM backbone, actions-as-tokens) made open,
  reproducible, and cheap to fine-tune. Its two notable deltas: the **DINOv2+SigLIP
  fused encoder** (spatial reasoning) and **quantile binning** (robust discretization).
- It is the cleanest **reproducible VLA reference** in the canon (open weights, code,
  data spec, fine-tuning notebooks), which is why it is the standard baseline (e.g.
  LAPA, Do-WAMs-Generalize, FAST all compare to it).
- Still a pure VLA (`p(a|o,l)`). No `o'`. FAST's case study notes OpenVLA "struggled to
  fit the higher-frequency DROID dataset," which is the concrete failure that motivates
  action-tokenization research (see `2501.09747-fast.md`).

## Mechanically useful vs research contribution

- **Useful for THIS repo**: as the canonical *open, runnable* VLA, OpenVLA is the
  reference if the repo ever wants a learned low-level executor baseline to contrast
  against an LLM-Actor-Turn agent (kept isolated, never actor authority). Its quantile-
  binning and "fine-tune the vision encoder" findings are practical if a small local
  model is ever trained on repo transcripts. Its LoRA-on-consumer-GPU result is the
  evidence that VLA fine-tuning is not compute-prohibitive.
- **Avoid / overclaim**: OpenVLA is single-arm manipulation, pixels-to-motor; its
  success numbers do not transfer to social/material state. Do not adopt 256-bin motor
  tokenization for the repo's typed action surface.
