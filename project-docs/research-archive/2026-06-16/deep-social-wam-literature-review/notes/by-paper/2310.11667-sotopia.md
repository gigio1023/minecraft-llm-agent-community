## Primary-source facts (what the paper states/shows)

- Research question: can we evaluate the *social intelligence* of LLM agents interactively, beyond static social-commonsense QA?
- Environment: agents role-play characters and interact (round-robin, dyadic, max 20 turns) to achieve hidden *social goals*. A task = scenario context + characters + social goals. Each agent observes the scenario, its own goal, its own profile; other agents' goals are invisible and their profiles partially observable by relationship type (family/friend/romantic/acquaintance/stranger).
- Action space (per turn, discrete category then free text): `speak`, `non-verbal communication` (e.g. hug, smile), `physical action` (e.g. play music), `none` (silence), `leave` (end episode). All actions resolve to text; there is no physical world that validates them.
- SOTOPIA-Eval: 7 evaluation dimensions scored at the end of an episode (drawn from sociology/psychology/economics):
  1. Goal Completion [0–10]
  2. Believability [0–10] (naturalness + character consistency)
  3. Knowledge [0–10] (new info acquired)
  4. Secret [−10–0] (keeping secrets)
  5. Relationship [−5–5] (preserve/enhance ties; status/reputation)
  6. Social Rules [−10–0] (norms + legal rules)
  7. Financial and Material Benefits [−5–5] (short- and long-term economic payoff)
- Evaluator validity (the key result): GPT-4 evaluator vs human Pearson correlation by dimension — Goal Completion r=0.71 (models)/0.78 (humans), Financial/Material r=0.62, Relationship r=0.56; weak on Secret (0.22), Knowledge (0.33), Social Rules (0.33), Believability (0.45). GPT-4 is "more likely to rate higher than humans when it disagrees." Conclusion: GPT-4 is a usable proxy "with some caution," mainly on Goal Completion, mainly for evaluating models (correlation drops on most dimensions when humans role-play).
- Human vs model: humans beat GPT-4 significantly on Goal Completion. Humans say 16.8 words/turn vs GPT-4's 45.5 (GPT-4 over-uses "active listening"/rephrasing); humans are more strategic in bargaining (anchor lower, settle below GPT-4's target) and more persistent.

## Interpretation (my inference, labeled)

- SOTOPIA is the canonical, widely-reused social-metric vocabulary. Six of its seven dimensions are *purely dialogue-judged plausibility* with no external referent. The single dimension nominally anchored to a verifiable world fact — Financial and Material Benefits — is still scored by an LLM/human reading the transcript, not by a ledger that the environment enforces. So even SOTOPIA's "material" dimension is plausibility, not verified consequence.
- The evaluator-correlation table is itself a validity warning: the dimensions that are easiest to anchor to a concrete outcome (Goal Completion, Financial) correlate best; the diffuse social-construct dimensions (Secret, Knowledge, Social Rules) correlate weakly. This predicts that *the more "social" and less material a construct is, the less reliably an LLM judge measures it.*

## Mechanically useful vs research contribution

- Mechanically useful for this repo:
  - The 7-dimension rubric is a ready checklist of *what social outcomes to name* (goal, relationship, norm, secret, knowledge, material). Adapt the labels.
  - The relationship-gated observability rule (stranger sees nothing, family sees all-but-secrets) is a clean model for asymmetric-knowledge Minecraft scenarios.
  - Hidden-goal, dyadic, bounded-turn episodes map directly to the repo's 2-actor benchmark shape.
- What to avoid / would be overclaim:
  - Do not adopt SOTOPIA-Eval scores as evidence of social *consequence*. They certify plausible dialogue, not a verified change in possession/claim/obligation. The repo's whole differentiator is replacing transcript-judged scores with world-artifact-backed transitions.
  - Do not treat the GPT-4 evaluator as ground truth; the paper itself flags positional bias, over-rating, and dimension-dependent reliability.

## WAM layer(s) informed

- Social WAM (primary): supplies the construct vocabulary (goal, relationship, norm, secret, material benefit).
- Material/Economic WAM (secondary): the Financial/Material dimension is the bridge, but shows the gap — SOTOPIA scores material benefit as text, the repo must score it as inventory/container delta.
