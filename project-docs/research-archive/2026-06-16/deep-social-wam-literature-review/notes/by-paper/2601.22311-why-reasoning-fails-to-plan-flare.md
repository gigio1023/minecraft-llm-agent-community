## Primary-source facts (from the verified abstract)

- **The core thesis**: step-by-step reasoning is strong over short horizons but "induces a form of step-wise greedy policy" that fails on long-horizon planning, where "early actions must account for delayed consequences." The paper frames this as a fundamental mismatch between REASONING and PLANNING.
- **The named failure mode**: "locally optimal choices induced by step-wise scoring lead to early myopic commitments that are systematically amplified over time and difficult to recover from."
- **The study setting (directly matches the repo)**: "deterministic, fully structured environments with explicit state transitions and evaluation signals." This is the repo's exact evaluation posture (a verifier with explicit typed transitions).
- **The proposed minimal fix (FLARE)**: Future-aware Lookahead with Reward Estimation, enforcing "explicit lookahead, value propagation, and limited commitment in a single model, allowing downstream outcomes to influence early decisions."
- **Claimed result**: across multiple benchmarks, agent frameworks, and LLM backbones, FLARE improves task and planning-level behavior, "frequently allowing LLaMA-8B with FLARE to outperform GPT-4o with standard step-by-step reasoning." (claim-only; numbers not read from the source body.)

## Relevance to this repo

- **The sharpest limits statement for this lane, in the repo's own setting**: it analyzes LLM agents in deterministic, fully-structured environments with explicit state transitions and evaluation signals, which is precisely the repo's verifier-grounded world. Its finding, that step-wise self-scoring causes myopic early commitments amplified over the horizon, is a direct caution against a naive "advisory WAM that scores one step at a time" being trusted for long-horizon (Institutional-layer) prediction.
- **Why it matters for the advisory framing**: the repo's WAM is advisory and per-step; FLARE warns that per-step local scoring is myopic. This supports keeping the WAM advisory (it should not drive greedy commitment) and supports the directions report's emphasis that the SHARPENING ceiling is about calibration over horizons, not new capability. A well-calibrated one-step predictor can still mislead a long-horizon plan if its scores are consumed greedily.
- **Honest caveat**: this is a 2026 abstract-only source; treat the mechanism as a well-posed hypothesis aligned with LATS's "performance ceiling" and WebDreamer's "error accumulation," not as an independently verified result here.
- **Research contribution vs support**: not a method the repo builds. It is the citation for the long-horizon myopia risk of step-wise LLM scoring in exactly the repo's structured setting.

## WAM layer(s) informed

Institutional / long-horizon. The clearest statement that step-wise LLM scoring is myopic over horizons even in deterministic structured environments, which bounds how far a per-step advisory WAM can be trusted for settlement-level prediction.
