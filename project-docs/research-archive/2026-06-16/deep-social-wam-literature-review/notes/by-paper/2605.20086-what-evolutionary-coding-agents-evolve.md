## Primary-source facts (LaTeX-verified)

- **What it is**: a diagnostic study of LLM-driven evolutionary coding agents (FunSearch/AlphaEvolve-class) that asks what they ACTUALLY evolve, not just their final scores. Two artifacts: (1) EvoTrace, 121 evolutionary runs across 4 frameworks (incl. OpenEvolve, ShinkaEvolve), 16 tasks (Python math constructions + C++ ALE-bench competitive-programming, ahc008-ahc046), 5 LLMs (with/without diff-based generation), 10,672 unique programs, 18,400 LLM calls, full parent-child graphs. (2) EvoReplay, a replay methodology that reconstructs local search states and reruns controlled interventions (same-prompt replay, BO retuning, static analysis, cycling detection, ablation, repair, model/context substitution).
- **Edit taxonomy (LLM-as-judge, validated against blind human re-annotation, macro kappa=0.77)**: 9 categories. FREQUENCY: Hyperparameter tuning and Local refinement dominate. UTILITY (per-edit odds ratio for positive score change): External dependency 3.58x, Efficiency 1.61x, Architectural change 1.55x. FINDING: the categories that most often improve a single edit are NOT the categories search spends most effort on. Most score gains come from a small subset of rare edit types.
- **Cycling**: across all 121 runs, the median share of ADDED lines that are byte-identical to lines the same lineage already DELETED earlier is ~30%, and this rate grows monotonically over the run in 118 of 121 cases. Cycling is present throughout the trajectory (not a late pathology), dominated by short-span churn (median 5 iterations), stable across all 4 frameworks, both languages, and all 5 generator models. Conclusion: search budget is partly spent re-introducing material the run already discarded.
- **Replay reproducibility**: across 36 best-so-far breakthrough events, re-running the EXACT same prompt 10x almost always yields a runnable, evaluator-accepted program (median 1.00) but essentially NEVER the original program byte-for-byte (median exact-match 0.00); it recovers a median 0.76 of the original score from a DIFFERENT program. The trajectory carries the structural gain; the specific program is one draw from a distribution.
- **The tuning gap (math)**: separate a program into structure s and hyperparameters theta. A 24-call Bayesian-optimization pass over a single intermediate program's exposed knobs improves over the program's original score in 22 of 36 targets, and matches or EXCEEDS the evolutionary run's FINAL-best on 13 of 15 intermediate programs (median delta +0.025). Largest case: evo reached 0.521 in 100 iterations; BO on an intermediate program reached 0.886 (1.70x). Conclusion: on math, late evolutionary iterations are largely substitutable by post-hoc hyperparameter tuning.
- **Overfitting on ALE**: re-scoring public best-so-far chains on the held-out private AtCoder test set, 2 of 4 frameworks overfit on at least 30% of problems; on ahc024 one framework gained +1,606 private rating while another LOST 1,610 despite a positive public score. Public scores are unreliable single-number summaries.
- **Lineage shallowness**: lineages back to the seed are short (median depth 6 math, 4 ALE); the dominant pattern is jackpot-then-flat, most budget spent on dead branches.

## Interpretation (flagged as inference)

- This is the most important BOUND source in the lane. It shows that headline "discovery" scores from evolutionary coding agents conflate at least four mechanisms: genuine new structure, hyperparameter tuning of a known structure, recombination of in-model knowledge, and overfitting to the evaluator. Only some count as discovery.
- Three findings translate directly into guards for a verifier-grounded discovery loop in this repo:
    - The tuning-gap result says: report the single-program tuning ceiling alongside any headline, or you will mistake parameter retuning for structural discovery. For the repo, a "discovered" advisory-WAM rule must be checked against a tuned-baseline rule before claiming it is new.
    - The overfitting result (public vs private) is the program-discovery face of progress laundering by evaluator-overfit: a rule that scores well on the seen transitions may fail on held-out ones. The repo must score discovered rules on HELD-OUT verified transitions, never the ones used to discover them (mirrors ENPIRE's "study failures but never train on the test set").
    - Cycling and lineage shallowness say the loop is inefficient by default and benefits from deletion-aware/lineage-aware curation (which the wave-4 EvolveR principle-curation also argues).
- The replay finding (structural gain reproducible, specific program not) supports treating a discovered rule as a sample from a distribution: re-run and keep what reproduces, do not over-trust a single lucky program.

## Mechanically useful vs research contribution

- **Mechanically useful**: the held-out re-scoring discipline (separate discovery set from judging set for any discovered rule); the tuning-ceiling baseline (BO over a candidate's knobs) as a control before claiming structural discovery; deletion-aware novelty filters / lineage-aware credit assignment to cut cycling; reporting (original score, tuning ceiling) pairs.
- **Not a contribution to copy**: EvoTrace/EvoReplay are diagnostic infrastructure (support tooling, not a research contribution per the shared contract). The repo borrows the DIAGNOSTICS and the discipline.
- **Bound it sets**: even at the Physical/Material layers where the evaluator is exact, a discovery loop will over-report unless (a) discovered rules are judged on held-out transitions and (b) structural gains are separated from retuning. The social layer, with a weaker evaluator, would over-report worse.

## WAM layer(s) informed

Method-level, cross-layer (it is a critique of the discovery method itself). Most directly bounds any claim that a discovery loop "found a new advisory-WAM rule": demands held-out judging and a tuning-gap control.
