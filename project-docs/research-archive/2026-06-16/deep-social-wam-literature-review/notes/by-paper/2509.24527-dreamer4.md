# Dreamer 4 — Training Agents Inside of Scalable World Models

- **title**: Training Agents Inside of Scalable World Models (Dreamer 4)
- **authors**: Danijar Hafner, Wilson Yan, Timothy Lillicrap (Google DeepMind)
- **year**: 2025
- **venue/source**: arXiv
- **arxiv_id**: 2509.24527
- **urls**: LaTeX at `papers/latex/2509.24527/`
- **source availability**: latex (full)

## Primary-source facts

- **Object**: a scalable **agent** that solves control tasks by **reinforcement learning in imagination** inside a learned world model — the Dreamer lineage (learn a world model, train an actor-critic on imagined rollouts). This makes the *world model* a learned simulator and the *agent* a policy trained inside it; together they predict and act, but via RL-in-imagination, not joint per-step `p(o',a)`.
- **Inputs**: "low-resolution images and inventory states" (per appendix) — i.e., **structured inventory state alongside pixels**, not pixels alone. Outputs: mouse, keyboard, and abstract crafting actions.
- **Headline**: **first agent to obtain diamonds in Minecraft purely from offline data**, no environment interaction; choosing sequences of "over 20,000 mouse and keyboard actions from raw pixels." Beats OpenAI VPT's offline agent "despite using 100x less data."
- **Efficiency**: high-capacity world model with **real-time inference on a single GPU** via a "shortcut forcing" objective + efficient transformer (contrast: Genie-3-style video models "often require many GPUs to simulate a single scene in real time").
- **Data**: trainable on large unlabeled video; needs only a small amount of action-paired video to learn action conditioning ("opens up learning general world knowledge from diverse web videos").
- Positions vs neighbors: Dreamer 3 is "fast and accurate for narrow environments" but its architecture "lacks the ability to fit complex real world distributions"; Genie 3 / Matrix are scalable but "struggle to learn precise physics of object interactions and game mechanics."

## Interpretation

- Dreamer 4 is the most capable Minecraft world-model **agent** to date and the key counterpoint to "pixel = the only way": it is a **latent / tokenizer-based** model (the Dreamer RSSM->transformer lineage), uses **structured inventory state** as input, and trains the policy **in imagination** rather than per-step joint prediction. It shows a latent + structured-state world model can do long-horizon Minecraft control single-GPU.
- It is the closest existing thing to "WAM in Minecraft" in capability, but it is still **physical/task** (obtain diamonds), single-agent, and has **no social-material state** (no possession transfer, obligation, claim, trust). Its "inventory state" is the actor's own inventory for crafting, not multi-actor possession/claims.
- DreamerV3 (Nature 2025, arXiv 2301.04104) was first to get diamonds *from scratch without human data* (online RL); Dreamer 4 extends to *offline-only* with 100x less data than VPT. Both are latent world-model agents.

## Mechanically useful vs research contribution

- **Useful**: strongest precedent that a **latent + structured-state** world model (not pixel-primary) achieves hard long-horizon Minecraft control on modest hardware — supports the structured-state feasibility argument. The "structured inventory state as input" design directly parallels feeding typed Minecraft state to a social predictor. RL-in-imagination is a possible *future* training route, but conflicts with the project's "LLM proposes, runtime owns truth; WAM stays advisory" rule, so adopt only as a contrast, not a target.
- **Avoid/overclaim**: Dreamer 4 does not model social/material/obligation state and is single-agent; do not present it as social. The project's runtime is LLM-tool-selection over Mineflayer, not an actor-critic trained in imagination — do not pivot to imagination-trained policies as runtime authority.

## WAM layer(s) informed

Physical (strong); Material (inventory state input, single-actor only). Latent + structured-state feasibility evidence.
