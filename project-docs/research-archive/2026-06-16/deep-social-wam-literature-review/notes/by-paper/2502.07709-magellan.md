## Primary-source facts (what the paper states/shows)

- **Research question**: an LLM agent doing autotelic exploration (choosing its own goals) in a *large, evolving, language-described* goal space must prioritize goals by learning progress (LP). But estimating LP requires knowing one's own competence per goal, and the two existing options are bad at scale: re-evaluating competence on every goal is prohibitive (impossible for continuous spaces), and expert-defined goal groupings are brittle (they assume no transfer across groups and hide variation within). How do you estimate LP sample-efficiently over a huge language goal space?
- **Core proposal (metacognition)**: learn a *goal-conditioned competence estimator* C_theta(g) on top of the agent's own LLM. Feed the goal g into the LLM, take the last-token latent from the final decoder block, and an MLP head predicts success probability. Train the LLM+MLP (LoRA adapters) with binary cross-entropy against observed outcomes. Because the LLM projects semantically similar goals to nearby latents, updating competence for one goal *generalizes* to similar goals - capturing "competence transfer" without any hand-defined groups.
- **Learning progress**: keep a buffer of recent competence-estimator weights; absolute LP = |C_theta_t(g) - C_theta_{t-N}(g)| (tracks both progress and forgetting). Goals are selected by a multi-armed bandit where each arm is a goal and utility = estimated ALP, with annealed epsilon-greedy (epsilon 1 -> 0.2).
- **Testbed**: "Little-Zoo," an interactive environment designed to study commonsense-based generalization over a large language goal space. Agent = SAC-GLAM LLM agent grounded with online RL.
- **Results**: MAGELLAN estimates competence more sample-efficiently than online and evaluation-based ALP baselines and than expert-group baselines; it is the only method that lets the agent *fully master* a large and *evolving* goal space (Q4: it adapts when new goals are added). Ablations: freezing the LLM (static representation) gives poor results; separate LoRA adapters for policy vs estimator give stability.

## Interpretation (flagged as inference)

- MAGELLAN is the recent **LLM-native learnability predictor**: it answers "which goal is at my frontier?" using the LLM's own semantic representation, generalizing across similar goals. This is the closest existing analog to the role the repo's advisory WAM could play in scenario *selection*: predict which social-material scenario is learnable (at the actor's frontier) without exhaustively running every scenario.
- The decisive bridge-and-bound for the thesis: MAGELLAN's competence labels are **verifier-scored outcomes** (the success r in its buffer comes from the environment). It works precisely because the environment returns a clean binary success per episode. In a social world, that binary "did the actor succeed at this social goal?" is the *contested* quantity; MAGELLAN's machinery transfers only as far as the verifier can supply that label.
- MAGELLAN also reframes "interestingness vs learnability" usefully: it is purely a *learnability* (LP) method - no interestingness model. So it is the verifier-groundable half of OMNI, scaled to language goals. For the repo, the safe adoption is the learnability half (MAGELLAN-style), with interestingness left advisory.

## Mechanically useful vs research contribution

- **Mechanically useful**: (1) a concrete recipe to **predict scenario learnability from an LLM latent** instead of running every scenario: a goal/scenario-conditioned competence head trained on verifier outcomes, generalizing across semantically similar scenarios. (2) **ALP = |C_t - C_{t-N}|** with a weight buffer is a cheap, transfer-aware LP signal. (3) The **MAB-over-goals selector** (sample proportional to estimated ALP) is a ready scheduler for "which social-material scenario next." (4) The finding that a *frozen* LLM representation fails (you must adapt the representation online) is a design warning if the repo tries to reuse a static embedding for scenario learnability.
- **Not a contribution to copy / overclaim risk**: MAGELLAN needs a per-episode success label; the repo must source that from the runtime verifier, never the actor's self-report. Its goal space is commonsense language tasks in a synthetic environment ("Little-Zoo"), not embodied multi-actor social-material situations; transfer of the *method* is fine, transfer of the *results* is not. Estimating LP is not the same as scoring whether a *social* outcome (a kept promise, a respected claim) actually occurred - that remains the verifier's job and the repo's contribution.
- **Limitation (theirs + spotted)**: single synthetic testbed; success is environment-supplied binary; LP is a proxy for future progress (uses past progress); no possession/obligation semantics; the competence head is only as honest as the success labels feeding it.
