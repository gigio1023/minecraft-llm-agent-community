# GROOT - Learning to Follow Instructions by Watching Gameplay Videos

- **authors**: Cai, Zhang, Wang, Ma, Liu, Liang (Team CraftJarvis: PKU / UCLA / BIGAI)
- **year**: 2023 - **venue**: ICLR 2024 (Spotlight)
- **arxiv_id**: 2310.08235
- **urls**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2310.08235 ; code https://github.com/CraftJarvis/GROOT ; weights https://huggingface.co/CraftJarvis/MineStudio_GROOT.18w_EMA

## Primary-source facts

- **Research question**: how to specify open-ended goals to a low-level Minecraft
  controller without expensive trajectory↔text pairs, while staying more
  expressive than task-id or single goal-image conditioning?
- **Core idea**: specify the goal as a **reference gameplay video clip**. Learn a
  video-instruction *encoder* that induces a structured **goal space**, plus a
  policy, *simultaneously*, by casting the problem as **future-state prediction
  from past observations**. The variational objective reduces to a behavioral
  cloning loss + a KL regularization loss.
- **Architecture**: encoder-decoder of causal transformers. The **video encoder**
  is a *non-causal* transformer that reads the reference video and maps it to a
  latent goal; the **policy decoder** is a causal transformer that autoregressively
  emits actions conditioned on the latent goal and the current state.
- **Observation / action space**: same VPT-family pixel obs (128×128) and
  camera/buttons action space; built on the VPT visual backbone lineage.
- **Benchmark introduced - Minecraft SkillForge**: 30 tasks across 6 groups
  (`collect`, `build`, `survive`, `explore`, `tool`, `craft`). Scored by **Elo**
  from human pairwise evaluations among GROOT, baselines, and human players.
- **Result**: closes the human-machine gap; **+150 Elo (≈70% win rate)** over the
  best generalist baseline. Qualitative: emergent goal composition.

## Interpretation (inference)

- GROOT is the VPT-family member **closest to a WAM-adjacent objective**: it is
  trained by predicting future states. But the prediction is used to *induce a
  goal space*, NOT to forecast consequences for evaluation or planning - at
  inference it is still `p(a | o, z_video)`, a goal-conditioned visual policy. So
  it is a **VLA / visual policy**, not a WAM under the survey's two criteria
  (no explicit predictive commitment to `o'` at decision time).
- SkillForge's reliance on **human Elo** is the honest admission that open-ended
  Minecraft behavior is hard to score programmatically - directly relevant to why
  this repo insists on structured verifiers for the *physical* layer and reserves
  judgment-style scoring for the social layer.

## Mechanically useful vs research contribution

- **Mechanically useful**: (a) "goal-as-video" is a reusable idea for *demoing*
  desired actor behavior in a separate visual lane; (b) SkillForge's 6 task
  groups are a clean competence-gate taxonomy (collect/build/survive/explore/
  tool/craft) to label this repo's physical gates.
- **What would be overclaim**: treating GROOT's future-state prediction as a
  consequence model. It predicts to *encode goals*, not to evaluate "Bob can now
  mine." And Elo-from-humans is not a runtime truth source.

## WAM layer(s) informed

Physical-WAM (executor + a near-miss on Forward Predictive Modeling, used for goal
induction not consequence forecasting). Reproducible: weights + code public.
