## Primary-source facts

- Research question: can a *structured* representation of social-world state let LLMs reason about and predict social dynamics (others' beliefs, intentions, actions) better than free-form text?
- **Social World Model formulation** (Dec-POMDP variant). State space S, action space A = A_1×...×A_N, observation O = O_1×...×O_N, transition T: S×A->Δ(S), observation Ω: A×S->Δ(O). Observation per agent splits into **external observations** O^ex (environment + others' acts) and **introspective observations** O^in (own beliefs, goals, moral values, emotions). Action space includes introspective ops (recall memory, reflect, update belief). A social world model computes:
  - (1) `p(A_t^{-i} | S_t)` - predict OTHER agents' actions from social state
  - (2) `p(S_{t+1} | S_t, A_t^{-i}, a_t^i)` - predict next state given all agents' actions
- **S3AP** = an LLM-parsed structured representation of social-world state. A free-text narrative at time t is parsed into a tuple with `state` (overall environment), per-agent `observations`, per-agent `actions`. Symbols like `<same_as_state>` mark an agent whose observation equals full state. Predicting the S3AP representation = configuring a social world model.
- **Foresee and Act** algorithm (inference-time): the agent samples a candidate action, then the SWM simulates how social state would evolve (how others interpret it, how environment responds), then refines the action. One LLM predicts the next social state; another generates and refines the action. (This is structurally an imagine-then-execute / cascaded loop.)
- Results (evaluation): on 5 social-reasoning tasks S3AP improves understanding (+51% on FANToM ToM with o1). On **SOTOPIA-hard** (100 sims, GPT-4o fixed partner, one-step SWM rollout), integrating an S3AP SWM consistently boosts goal-completion; larger gains in competitive vs cooperative settings. Key finding: **stronger social agent ≠ better social world model** - GPT-4.1 beats Llama-4 Maverick as an agent (6.01 vs 4.52 goal completion) but SWM performance is nearly identical (6.34 vs 6.36); and a useful SWM (o1) paired with a weak agent (Llama-4) can still lower performance. "Social reasoning involves two parts; strength in constructing social representations doesn't imply strength in reasoning over them and acting correctly."

## Interpretation (my inference, labeled)

- This is the single most important translation source for the repo's "Social WAM" thesis. Its formulation is **exactly the canonical WAM definition (`p(o',a|o,l)`) applied to social state**: equation (2) is the forward predictive model `p(S_{t+1}|S_t, actions)`, and "Foresee and Act" is the Cascaded WAM `p(a|o',o,l)-p(o'|o,l)` (imagine social consequence, then act). It proves the structured-state branch is real and beats free text.
- The decomposition into external vs introspective observation is a clean template for the repo: external = Minecraft world facts (verifiable), introspective = beliefs/obligations/trust (the social overlay). The repo can make external observation *verified* (Mineflayer) while keeping introspective as advisory predicted state - this is precisely the advisory-WAM line the SPEC draws.
- The "stronger agent ≠ better world model" finding is a direct validity caveat that generalizes the SimBench / MineExplorer "more reasoning ≠ better" pattern: world-modeling capability and acting capability are separable, so the repo must score *prediction accuracy* and *acting outcome* as different axes.
- The decisive limitation for the repo: S3AP's `state` is still **LLM-parsed from free-form narrative**, and evaluated only via SOTOPIA goal-completion (a dialogue score). It measures "does a structured social-state guess help win a dialogue," NOT "does a predicted social-material delta match a verified world change." The repo's contribution is to make S' a *verified Minecraft+social delta* checked against inventory/container/ledger artifacts, not a narrative parse scored by an LLM judge.

## Mechanically useful vs research contribution

- Mechanically useful:
  - The S3AP `state / observations / actions` tuple is a ready schema for the repo's per-cycle social-world-state record (rename to Minecraft typed fields).
  - "Foresee and Act" = the exact shape of the repo's proposed advisory predictor lane (predict deltas before acting), with the hard rule it stays advisory.
  - The two-LLM split (one predicts state, one acts/refines) is a concrete decomposition for an advisory predictor that does not touch Actor Turn selection.
- Avoid / overclaim:
  - Do NOT evaluate the social WAM only on a dialogue benchmark; that is what S3AP does and is the gap the repo fills.
  - Do not let the predicted next-state become authority (S3AP's SWM feeds the agent; the repo's must only advise, never fill args / mark progress / override verifiers).

## WAM layer(s) informed

- Social WAM (primary, defines the predictive object), Institutional WAM (other-agent action prediction across a group). This is the paper that most directly justifies a structured-state (non-pixel) social WAM for the repo.

## Lane 30 extension: scoring the predictor of deltas SEPARATELY from the action outcome

Added by lane 30 (calibration and predictor evaluation). The S3AP paper supplies the SEPARATION
principle this lane builds the measurement protocol around.

- The separation, restated as a measurement rule: S3AP proves "strength in constructing social
  representations doesn't imply strength in reasoning over them and acting correctly," with the
  numeric witness that GPT-4.1 beats Llama-4 Maverick as an AGENT (goal completion 6.01 vs 4.52) yet
  their social-world-MODEL performance is nearly identical (6.34 vs 6.36), and a strong world model
  (o1) paired with a weak agent (Llama-4) can LOWER end performance. World-modeling capability and
  acting capability are EMPIRICALLY DISSOCIABLE. Therefore an honest evaluation of an advisory
  social-material WAM must score the PREDICTOR of deltas on its own axis (predicted next-state vs the
  observed next-state), not let a downstream task-success number stand in for it.
- What S3AP scores its predictor on, and the gap: S3AP's predictive quality is measured only through
  SOTOPIA goal-completion (does feeding the predicted social state to the agent help it win the
  dialogue) and ToM-task understanding (FANToM +51 percent with o1). It never scores
  "predicted social-material delta vs a verified world change." That is the cell the repo fills: with
  the runtime verifier auto-labeling (state, action, next-state), the repo can score the predicted
  delta DIRECTLY against the verified delta, with the calibration and coverage machinery this lane
  imports (ECE [[1706.04599-calibration-modern-neural-networks]], distribution-free coverage
  [[2107.07511-conformal-prediction-tutorial]], proper scores [[2304.15004-emergent-abilities-mirage]]).
- The concrete two-axis protocol this implies (lane 30 contribution, labeled inference):
  1. PREDICTOR axis (advisory, the lane's focus): for each held-out transition, the predictor emits a
     distribution / set / point over the next typed delta; the verifier supplies the observed delta;
     score with a proper scoring rule (Brier / log loss), calibration (ECE + reliability diagram),
     and, where a set is emitted, distribution-free coverage (conformal). Aggregate over scenarios and
     seeds with rliable's IQM + stratified-bootstrap CIs
     [[2108.13264-rliable-statistical-precipice]], NOT a single mean.
  2. ACTING axis (separate): whether the actor completed the social-material goal, scored against the
     same verifier artifacts. Reported alongside but NEVER substituted for axis 1.
  The two must be reported as different numbers precisely because S3AP shows they dissociate.
- The hard line preserved: S3AP's "Foresee and Act" FEEDS the predicted state to the agent. The
  repo's predictor stays ADVISORY (it never fills action args, marks progress, gates serving, or
  overrides the verifier), so the predictor axis above is a pure measurement of forecast quality, with
  no path for the prediction to launder its own success into the acting axis. This is the
  proposer-is-never-the-scorer rule from the directions report, applied at the evaluation boundary.
