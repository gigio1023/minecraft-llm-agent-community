- authors: Mou, Liu, et al.
- year: 2025
- venue: NAACL 2025 (2025.naacl-long.257)
- arxiv_id: 2410.19346
- urls: paper https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.19346 ; code https://github.com/ljcleo/agent_sense
- source read: LaTeX (papers/latex/2410.19346/latex/tabs/010_intro.tex, 030_benchmark.tex)

## Primary-source facts

- Research question: can LLM agents navigate *diverse, complex, multi-party* social interactions, including concealing/inferring private information — beyond SOTOPIA's mostly two-party, single-goal, top-down scenarios?
- Method (grounded in Goffman's Dramaturgical Theory): **bottom-up** scenario extraction from scripts. 245 scenario templates → expanded to **1,225 scenarios**, mitigating data leakage; goals span ERG theory (existence → relatedness → growth). Agents have social goals and private information to protect.
- Evaluation, two aspects: (1) **Goal Completion** (did agents achieve social goals?); (2) **Implicit Reasoning** (can agents deduce others' private information?). Measured via interviews + multiple-choice; a **PSI** (profile-sensitivity) metric assesses how much performance depends on the character profile. Third-party (GPT-4) judges.
- Findings: LLMs struggle with complex scenarios and especially **high-level growth goals**; social intelligence is affected by **profiles, interaction partners, and the goal-vs-privacy balance.**

## Interpretation (my inference, labeled)

- AgentSense generalizes SOTOPIA along three axes the repo cares about: multi-party (>2 actors), multiple simultaneous goals, and information-concealment/inference (private info). The implicit-reasoning aspect (deduce others' hidden info) is the closest dialogue-benchmark analog to the repo's `asymmetric_knowledge_v1` scenario.
- Like SOTOPIA, evaluation is dialogue/MCQ-grounded, not world-grounded: "goal completion" is judged from the conversation, and "implicit reasoning" is an MCQ about a persona's hidden field, not a verified world consequence. PSI (profile sensitivity) is again partner/profile-dependence — the recurring lane-wide finding that a social score is conditional on the counterpart.

## Mechanically useful vs research contribution

- Mechanically useful: bottom-up scenario sourcing (from scripts) to avoid the narrow top-down "persuasion + collaboration" set; the ERG goal taxonomy (existence/relatedness/growth) as a way to vary social-goal difficulty; the private-info concealment/inference design → Minecraft asymmetric-knowledge scenarios (one actor knows a chest location / recipe / hazard).
- Avoid / overclaim: do not treat MCQ "did the agent infer the hidden field" as a verified social consequence; in Minecraft the analog must be a *world action* taken on the inferred fact (going to the chest, avoiding the hazard), verified by Mineflayer.

## WAM layer(s) informed

- Social WAM (goal completion, implicit reasoning about others), partner-dependence. Scenario-design source for asymmetric knowledge and multi-party interaction.
