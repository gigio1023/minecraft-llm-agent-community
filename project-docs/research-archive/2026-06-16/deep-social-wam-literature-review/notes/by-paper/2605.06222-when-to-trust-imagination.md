- **title**: (When to Trust Imagination) — Future–Reality Verification for adaptive WAM execution; module = Future Forward Dynamics Causal Attention (FFDC)
- **year**: 2026

- **arxiv_id**: 2605.06222

## Primary-source facts

- **Research question**: current WAMs execute a fixed-size action chunk per inference and are "blind to whether the imagined future remains consistent with the actual physical rollout." When should the WAM's imagined future still be trusted during execution?
- **Formulation**: adaptive WAM execution as a **future–reality verification** problem. WAMs uniquely "predict not only what action to take, but also what future visual observations should occur if the action rollout remains valid" — enabling **self-verification** by comparing real vs predicted observation at the corresponding timestep.
- **Method (FFDC)**: a **lightweight verifier** using structured causal attention over {predicted future actions, predicted visual dynamics, real observations, language instruction} to estimate whether the remaining action segment is still executable. Effective chunk size becomes an emergent consequence of prediction–observation consistency (execute long when predictable, replan early when reality deviates). Plus Mixture-of-Horizon Training for long-horizon coverage.
- **Verifier training data**: a **binary verification dataset** — valid segments from demonstrations and successful rollouts (positive) vs failure-prone segments from failed rollouts + **synthetic action corruptions** (negative). Trained to predict executability of the remaining segment.
- **Results**: RoboTwin — reduces WAM forward passes 69.10%, execution time 34.02%, +2.54% success vs short-chunk baseline; real world +35% success.
- **Related-work facts**: WAMs capture forward dynamics `p(o'|o,a)`, inverse dynamics `p(a|o,o')`, marginal action `p(a|o)`, marginal image `p(o'|o)` in one framework. "Since pixel-level video decoding is expensive, several works perform future video prediction mainly during training, while relying on latent world features or even skipping explicit future rollout at inference time" (cites Fast-WAM, GigaWorld). Prior adaptive-execution methods (entropy, ensemble disagreement, verifier-based replanning) work on action-only/VLA policies and "cannot directly compare the policy's internal future expectation with the actual physical rollout."

## Interpretation

- This is the most direct primary-source instantiation of **WAM-as-evaluator/verifier** (not actuator, not planner). The predicted future is used as "an internal expectation of how the physical world should evolve," consumed by a *separate lightweight module* that gates execution. The WAM does not fill arguments or override anything — it provides a consistency signal.
- This maps almost exactly onto the project's hard rule that a WAM "must stay advisory: it predicts/evaluates consequences; it must not fill missing args, mark progress true, override verifiers." FFDC is a precedent for a runtime that compares predicted-vs-observed and decides trust/replan, where physical truth still comes from observation (the project's verifiers/artifacts), not from the prediction.

## Mechanically useful vs research contribution

- **Useful**: (1) the "predict expected future state, then compare to observed evidence to decide trust/replan" loop — directly adaptable to "predict social-material delta, then compare to verifier/inventory/transcript evidence"; (2) the verifier-training recipe (positives from successful rollouts, negatives from failures + **synthetic corruptions**) — adaptable to building a social-transition verifier with negative controls; (3) explicit evidence that future *generation* can be skipped at inference while keeping the future *signal* (efficiency).
- **Avoid/overclaim**: FFDC verifies pixel-future-vs-pixel-observation for contact-rich manipulation; the project verifies typed social-material deltas vs structured evidence. Borrow the *control structure*, not the perceptual mechanism. Do not frame a verifier as the research contribution (per shared contract: evidence tooling is support).
