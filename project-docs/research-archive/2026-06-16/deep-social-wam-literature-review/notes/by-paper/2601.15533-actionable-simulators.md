# From Generative Engines to Actionable Simulators (physical-grounding survey)

- **title**: From Generative Engines to Actionable Simulators: The Imperative of Physical Grounding in World Models
- **year**: 2026
- **venue/source**: arXiv (Springer Nature journal template)
- **arxiv_id**: 2601.15533
- **urls**: LaTeX at `papers/latex/2601.15533/`
- **source availability**: latex (full)

## Primary-source facts

- **Thesis**: current world models suffer from **"visual conflation"** - "the mistaken assumption that high-fidelity video generation implies an understanding of physical and causal dynamics." Modern models "excel at predicting pixels" but "frequently violate invariant constraints, fail under intervention, and break down in safety-critical decision-making." "Visual realism is an unreliable proxy for world understanding."
- **Reframe**: world models as **"actionable simulators" rather than visual engines**, emphasizing **"structured 4D interfaces, constraint-aware dynamics, and closed-loop evaluation."** "the world state is not merely rendered, but explicitly exposed for long-horizon reasoning."
- Organizes the field around four challenges, including "**From Generative to Structural Interfaces**: evolution from 2D pixel-level extrapolation toward structured 4D dynamic meshes, persistent memories, and causal interaction graphs."
- **Epistemic stress test**: uses **medical decision-making** - "where trial-and-error is impossible and errors are irreversible" - to argue a world model's value "is determined not by how realistic its rollouts appear, but by its ability to support counterfactual reasoning, intervention planning, and robust long-horizon foresight."
- Notes latent-space physics (V-JEPA "operates entirely in latent space," WISA injects physical priors) as paths to grounding "without symbolic physics engines."
- **Defining line**: "a true world model is defined not by how realistically it predicts the future, but by how reliably it supports intervention, reasoning, and decision-making."

## Interpretation

- This survey, from a neighboring field (general world models / medical decision-making), independently arrives at the exact thesis the Minecraft-social-WAM project holds: pixel fidelity is the wrong target; the right target is a structured, causally-grounded, constraint-aware state that supports counterfactual reasoning. It is the strongest external "someone else said it" endorsement for choosing structured state over pixels.
- The medical-stress-test framing is a methodological parallel for the project: social obligations are a domain where "errors are costly and not freely reversible" (a broken promise, a violated claim has durable social consequence) - so the value of a social WAM is in supporting intervention/foresight, not in producing plausible-looking dialogue.

## Mechanically useful vs research contribution

- **Useful**: ready-made citations and vocabulary ("visual conflation," "actionable simulator," "structured 4D interface," "structured state explicitly exposed") to justify, in the project's own write-ups, why a structured-state Minecraft WAM is the defensible path. Strengthens the framing without being a method to import.
- **Avoid/overclaim**: it is a survey/position paper, not an empirical result; cite it as framing support, not as proof that a structured social WAM works. Do not over-borrow the "4D mesh" specifics - the project's structure is typed social-material state, not 4D geometry.
