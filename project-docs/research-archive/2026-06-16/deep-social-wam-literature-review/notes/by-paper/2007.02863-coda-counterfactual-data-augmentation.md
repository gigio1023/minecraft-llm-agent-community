## Research question
Many control / RL processes are made of interacting subprocesses that are not globally independent but interact only SPARSELY in time. Can you exploit the fact that AT A GIVEN STEP the subprocesses are usually locally causally independent, to manufacture extra, causally-valid training transitions WITHOUT a learned forward dynamics model, and thereby improve sample efficiency?

## Primary-source facts (what the paper states/shows)

Formalism (the causal vocabulary this lane needs, taught from the source):
- Structural Causal Model (SCM), Pearl Ch. 7: per time slice (t, t+1), nodes V are the decomposed state/action variables {S^1_t..S^n_t, A^1_t..A^m_t, S^1_{t+1}..S^n_{t+1}}; noise variables U (one per node, independent at t+1); structural equations F = {f^i} mapping a node's parents + its noise to the node. A DAG G encodes which variable directly causes which.
- Structural minimality (Assumption): V^j is a parent of V^i iff changing V^j can have a NONZERO DIRECT effect on V^i through f^i. This is the precise definition of a causal edge (vs a mere correlation).
- Factored MDP: when G is sparse, P(S^i_{t+1} | S_t, A_t) = P(S^i_{t+1} | parents(S^i_{t+1})), and S^i_{t+1} is independent of every non-parent given its parents. Sparse factored MDPs are more efficient to solve than monolithic ones.
- The do-operator / intervention (taught directly): to answer "what would the transition have looked like if component S^i_t had value x instead of y?", you apply Pearl's do-calculus to get the submodel M_{do(S^i_t = x)}: replace f^i with the constant S^i_t = x and delete incoming edges to S^i_t, then re-evaluate every structural equation that depends on S^i_t. Cost scales with the number of children of S^i_t.
- THE KEY INSIGHT, Local Causal Models (LCMs): globally the DAG is almost always fully connected ("there are (almost) no true zeros", Gelman), so a globally-factored model rarely beats a monolithic one. BUT for each edge (V^i_t -> S^j_{t+1}) there is usually a large SUBSPACE L of state-action space on which that edge is INACTIVE (e.g. two robot arms too far apart to touch). Conditioning on L induces a local DAG G^L strictly sparser than the global G. On L, counterfactual reasoning decomposes into independent components.

The CoDA algorithm:
- If two observed transitions share the same LOCAL factorization, you can SWAP their independent subprocess slices to mint a new transition that is causally valid in the global model, WITHOUT any forward model. (Fig 1: given 3 factual samples, mix-and-match factored sub-blocks; reject proposals whose sources are not locally factored or whose result is not factored; accept the valid one as extra RL training data.)
- It works on the agent's TRAINING DATA only, so it is compatible with any agent architecture (even unfactored ones).
- Local structure is discovered with an attention-based method over a disentangled / object-oriented state representation; they also show that goal relabeling (HER) and visual augmentation are special cases of CoDA using GLOBAL independence relations.

Where this naturally applies (direct project hook, verbatim spirit):
- "this decomposition will emerge naturally in any simulated process or game that is defined using a high-level programming language (e.g., the commonly used Atari or MINECRAFT simulators)." The paper explicitly names Minecraft (Malmo) as a setting where the object-hierarchy + global-context state decomposition exists for free.

Evaluation + metrics:
- CoDA "significantly improves the sample efficiency" of model-free RL in standard, batch-constrained, and goal-conditioned settings on locally factored tasks. (Quantitative tables are in the body/appendix, not re-extracted here; mark "headline = qualitative sample-efficiency improvement, exact numbers not transcribed".)

Limitations (theirs + spotted):
- Theirs: assumes a SINGLE, KNOWN top-level state decomposition with fixed factor count n; extensions to hierarchical decomposition, dynamic n, and learned latent factors are left to future work. Local-factorization discovery needs a disentangled state.
- Spotted: CoDA is for generating training DATA for a policy; it is not itself an action-selecting model. Its counterfactual validity rests on the local-independence test being correct; a wrong "they don't interact here" call mints a causally INVALID sample.

## Interpretation (my reading, labeled inference)
- CoDA is the most mechanically relevant counterfactual work in this lane for THIS repo, more so than the heavier causal-RL machinery, for one reason: it operates on STRUCTURED, object-decomposed transitions WITHOUT a forward dynamics model, and it explicitly names Minecraft as such a setting. The repo's verifier already emits exactly the kind of object-decomposed (o, a, o') transitions CoDA consumes.
- The local-factorization idea is the right mental model for a Minecraft social-material WAM's data problem: most actor pairs do not interact most of the time (Alice mining alone does not change Bob's possessions), so logged transitions are LOCALLY factored, and a counterfactual like "what if Alice had instead lent the pickaxe to Bob" can in principle be assembled by swapping the possession/obligation sub-blocks of two factual transitions that share the same local structure.
- The do-operator framing is the exact formal object behind the query's "predict and evaluate how actions transform state": predicting the effect of a candidate action IS computing the post-do submodel's next-state distribution. CoDA shows that when structure is local, this is cheap.

## Mechanically useful vs research contribution (for this repo)
- Mechanically useful (engineering to borrow):
  - Treat logged verifier transitions as object-decomposed SCM samples and use a local-independence test (which actors/items/containers actually interacted this step) to (a) cheaply enlarge a training set for any learned predictor and (b) reason about a candidate action's effect by re-evaluating only the affected sub-blocks.
  - Structural minimality as the discipline for the advisory WAM's predicted-delta schema: a state field is a "parent" of a delta only if changing it has a nonzero direct effect, so the WAM should not condition a possession-delta prediction on time-of-day.
  - The insight "knowledge of whether a local interaction occurs is useful in itself, even without a forward model" justifies a LIGHTWEIGHT advisory WAM: the runtime owns the forward truth, so the WAM's job can be the cheaper local-structure + delta call rather than a full simulator.
- Research contribution (novel if done here):
  - Applying CoDA-style counterfactual augmentation to SOCIAL/material transitions (swapping obligation/claim sub-blocks) to evaluate "what if Alice lends the pickaxe?" is unproven; CoDA only swapped physical subprocesses (robot arms, bouncing balls). The social local-independence test is itself a research object.
  - Reporting an advisory social WAM's counterfactual answers against verifier ground truth is a structured-state analogue of the survey's proposed Counterfactual-Consistency metric (see `wam-training-evaluation-and-open-problems.md`), not something CoDA itself measures.
- Avoid / overclaim guard:
  - CoDA generates training data; it does NOT act, fill args, or select. Keep it on the data/analysis side of the "WAM stays advisory" line.
  - Its causal validity depends on the local-independence call. For social state, "did these two actors interact this step" is far less crisp than "are the two arms far apart"; a wrong call mints invalid social counterfactuals. Do not assume the physical result transfers to social transitions without validation.

## WAM layer(s) informed
- Physical WAM (primary, where CoDA is proven): swapping independent physical subprocess slices.
- Material + Social WAM (as a research extension, unproven): the local-factorization + sub-block swap is the natural template for possession/obligation counterfactuals, but transporting it there is the repo's contribution.
- Evaluation: CoDA's do-operator framing is the formal backbone for a structured-state Counterfactual-Consistency check.
