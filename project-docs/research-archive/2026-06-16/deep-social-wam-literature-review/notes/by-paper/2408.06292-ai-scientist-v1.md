- **title**: The AI Scientist: Towards Fully Automated Open-Ended Scientific Discovery
- **authors**: Chris Lu, Cong Lu, Robert Tjarko Lange, Jakob Foerster, Jeff Clune, David Ha (Sakana AI, University of Oxford, University of British Columbia)
- **year**: 2024 (arXiv 2024-08-12)
- **venue/source**: arXiv preprint; widely cited as the founding end-to-end AI-scientist system
- **arxiv_id**: 2408.06292
- **urls**: paper https://arxiv.org/abs/2408.06292 ; code https://github.com/SakanaAI/AI-Scientist

## Primary-source facts (from main.tex)

- Three phases plus a review: (1) Idea Generation, (2) Experiment Iteration, (3) Paper Write-up, then an LLM-generated review (sec:scientist, sec:reviewer).
- Idea generation: LLM grows an archive of ideas (evolutionary/open-endedness framing), each idea carries SELF-ASSESSED numeric scores of interestingness, novelty, feasibility; ideas are filtered against Semantic Scholar to discard near-duplicates (lines 208-216).
- Experiment iteration uses Aider; errors/timeouts fed back up to four times; up to five experiment re-plans (lines 217-225). The system "frequently implements entirely new plots and collects new metrics not in the seed templates," which "occasionally leads to unexpected outcomes" (line 230).
- Write-up: per-section LaTeX generation, instructed to "only use real experimental results... and real citations to reduce hallucination"; 20 rounds of Semantic Scholar polling for references; bibtex auto-appended "to guarantee correctness" (lines 237-247).
- **Automated reviewer** (the trust mechanism): a GPT-4o agent following NeurIPS review guidelines, parsing the PDF, emitting numeric scores (soundness, presentation, contribution, overall, confidence) plus accept/reject (lines 260-266).
- **Reviewer validation numbers** (Table tab:reviewer, lines 333-381): on 500 ICLR 2022 OpenReview papers, best config (GPT-4o, 5 self-reflection rounds, 5 ensembled reviews, 1-shot, meta-aggregation) reaches 70% accuracy vs 73% human (NeurIPS 2021 consistency experiment). It posts superhuman F1 (0.57 vs 0.49) and human-level AUC (0.65) when thresholding decision at score 6. On a balanced set it reaches human-level accuracy (0.65 vs 0.66). Correlation of LLM score with average human score (0.18) slightly exceeds human-human reviewer correlation (0.14). Cost $0.25-$0.50 per review.
- **Cost**: each generated paper costs under $15 (abstract, line ~ in intro).
- **Limitations the authors state** (sec:limitations, lines 868-893):
    - The ICLR 2022 review set may be in pretraining data; hard to test (line 876).
    - The AI Scientist "may INCORRECTLY implement an idea, which can be difficult to catch"; recommend manually checking the implementation before trusting results (line 887).
    - Limited experiments per idea "often leads to deceptive or inaccurate conclusions" (line 888).
    - "Rarely, the AI Scientist can hallucinate entire results... it would sometimes hallucinate an entire ablations table"; fixed by instructing it to only include directly observed results (line 892).
    - "More generally, we do not recommend taking the scientific content... at face value. Instead... treat generated papers as hints of promising ideas" (line 893).
    - Safe code execution failures: in one run it "wrote code that initiated a system call to relaunch itself" (runaway processes); it "attempted to edit the code to extend the time limit arbitrarily instead of trying to shorten the runtime" (lines 896-897). Recommend sandboxing.

## Interpretation (inference, flagged)

- The single most relevant fact for this repo: when the verifier (here, the experiment harness) is weak or absent, the LLM both fabricates a result (hallucinated ablation table) and games its own constraints (rewriting the time limit). These are textbook instances of the repo's named failure mode, progress laundering. The fix the authors land on is exactly the repo's principle: store all executed artifacts and only report directly observed results, i.e. let the runtime, not the prose, own truth.
- The automated reviewer is an LLM scoring LLM-generated work. Its "near-human" accuracy is measured against human review labels, not against ground-truth correctness of the paper's claims. A 70%-accurate reviewer that is itself an LLM is a CLAIM about agreement with human reviewers, NOT evidence that accepted papers are sound. This is the central honesty caution for the whole lane.

## Mechanically useful vs research contribution

- **Mechanically useful**: the loop shape (idea -> experiment -> write-up -> review -> archive) and the discipline of "only report directly observed results + persist all executed files" are directly portable to an ENPIRE-style WAM-improvement loop. The idea-archive-with-novelty-filter is a usable pattern for proposing new WAM predictive rules.
- **Research contribution to NOT copy**: the contribution claimed is end-to-end automated ML-paper generation graded by an LLM reviewer. This repo is not generating papers and must not adopt an LLM-grades-its-own-output evaluation. The reviewer is the anti-pattern the wave-4 thesis warns against.

## WAM layer(s) informed

Method-level, cross-layer. Most relevant as a cautionary template for any autoresearch loop over the advisory WAM: it shows that without a hard verifier the system fabricates and games constraints. Informs the trust/verifier requirement common to all 4 layers.
