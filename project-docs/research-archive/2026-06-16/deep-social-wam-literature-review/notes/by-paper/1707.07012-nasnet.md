- **year**: 2017 (arXiv 2017-07; CVPR 2018)
- **venue/source**: CVPR 2018
- **arxiv_id**: 1707.07012
- **urls**: paper https://arxiv.org/abs/1707.07012
- **source availability**: latex (tarball extracted, papers/latex/1707.07012/; abstract + intro + experiments + appendix read directly)

## Primary-source facts (LaTeX-verified)

- **Research question**: NAS via RL is too expensive to run directly on large datasets (ImageNet). Can one search a CELL on a cheap proxy (CIFAR-10) and TRANSFER it by stacking, so the architecture's complexity is independent of depth/input size? (intro)
- **Method**: a one-layer LSTM controller (RL, trained with PPO, not REINFORCE) samples cell structures; child networks are trained and their validation accuracy is the reward. The "NASNet search space" searches a cell (normal + reduction cells) that is stacked into the full network. ScheduledDropPath is introduced as regularization (intro, appendix, experiments).
- **Search cost (the cost fact)**: 500 GPUs for 4 days = **2,000 GPU-hours**; 20,000 child models trained, top 250 retrained to convergence (experiments, appendix). Stated as ~7x faster than the prior NAS (Zoph & Le 2017), which "used 800 GPUs for 28 days resulting in 22,400 GPU-hours" (experiments footnote).
- **Results**: 2.4% error on CIFAR-10; the transferred NASNet reaches 82.7% top-1 / 96.2% top-5 on ImageNet, 1.2% better top-1 than the best human architecture with 9B fewer FLOPS (abstract).

## Interpretation (flagged as inference)

- NASNet is the "proxy-task + transfer to cut cost" milestone in the NAS cost curve: search a small cell cheaply, stack it. Even so, 2,000 GPU-hours and 20,000 trained child models is a lab-scale spend. It is the upper anchor of the cost trajectory that ENAS (0.5 GPU-day) and DARTS (1.5-4 GPU-days) later collapsed.
- The proxy-then-transfer pattern is the reusable idea: an autoresearch loop should search a cheap surrogate (small fixture of cycles) and transfer the improved module, not search at full fidelity.

## Mechanically useful vs research contribution

- **Mechanically useful**: the cheap-proxy-search + transfer-the-building-block pattern (reference). Search a WAM-prediction module on a small cycle fixture; reuse it.
- **Research contribution (not ours)**: RL-based transferable NAS is a Google-Brain research result.
- **What to avoid / overclaim**: do not treat NAS-style search as affordable; even the "cheap" cell-transfer version is 2,000 GPU-hours. The repo's edge is a near-$0 verifier signal, not a GPU-fleet search.
