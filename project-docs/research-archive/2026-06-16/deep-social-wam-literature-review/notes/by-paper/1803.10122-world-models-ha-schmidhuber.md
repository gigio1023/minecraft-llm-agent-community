# World Models (Ha and Schmidhuber, 2018)

The seminal modern world-model paper. Source: LaTeX deep-read at `papers/latex/1803.10122/main.tex`.

- **title**: World Models
- **authors**: David Ha (Google Brain), Jurgen Schmidhuber (NNAISENSE, Swiss AI Lab IDSIA)
- **year**: 2018
- **venue/source**: NeurIPS 2018 (also ICML 2018 workshop / arXiv); interactive version at worldmodels.github.io
- **arxiv_id**: 1803.10122
- **urls**: paper arxiv.org/abs/1803.10122 ; project worldmodels.github.io
- **code availability**: reference TensorFlow code released by authors; many public reimplementations
- **data availability**: environments are public (OpenAI Gym CarRacing-v0, VizDoom Take Cover); data is self-collected rollouts
- **model-weight availability**: small models (under 10M params); weights are trivially retrainable, not a gated release

## Plain-language: what it introduced and why it mattered

This is the paper that made "let the agent learn a model of its world, then train a policy by acting inside that learned model" a concrete, working recipe in deep RL. It split an agent into three pieces with a memorable V-M-C naming:

- **V (Vision)**: a Variational Autoencoder (VAE) that compresses each raw image frame into a small **latent vector** `z`. (A VAE is a neural network that learns to encode an input into a low-dimensional code and decode it back, so the code keeps the essence of the image. "Latent" just means the compressed internal representation, not the raw pixels.)
- **M (Memory)**: a recurrent neural network with a Mixture Density Network output (MDN-RNN) that predicts the next latent `z_{t+1}` given the current latent `z_t`, the action `a_t`, and its own hidden state `h_t`. (A recurrent neural network, RNN, is a network with memory of past steps. A Mixture Density Network outputs a probability distribution over outcomes, here a mixture of Gaussians, rather than a single point, so the model can represent a stochastic, uncertain future.)
- **C (Controller)**: a tiny linear policy `a_t = W_c [z_t h_t] + b_c` (only 867 parameters in CarRacing) that maps the compressed perception+memory to an action.

Why it mattered: it showed you can push almost all the agent's capacity into the world model (V and M, trained by self-supervised prediction, cheap on a GPU) and keep the decision-maker (C) tiny enough to optimize with a black-box evolutionary method, **Covariance-Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy (CMA-ES)**. (CMA-ES is a gradient-free optimizer that perturbs and re-weights a small parameter vector; it dodges the RL credit-assignment problem by not needing per-step gradients.) And, most influentially, it demonstrated that the controller could be **trained entirely inside the world model's "dream"** and then transferred back to the real environment.

## Primary-source facts

- **Architecture**: V = VAE (z in R^32, ~4.3M params), M = MDN-RNN modeling `P(z_{t+1} | a_t, z_t, h_t)` (~422K params), C = single linear layer (867 params for CarRacing).
- **Training procedure**: (1) collect 10,000 rollouts from a random policy; (2) train V to encode frames to `z`; (3) train M to predict the next-`z` distribution given action and state; (4) optimize C with CMA-ES to maximize expected cumulative reward. V and M are trained separately and self-supervised (no reward); only C sees reward.
- **Result 1 (CarRacing-v0)**: first reported agent to "solve" the task (average reward 900 over 100 trials).
- **Result 2 (VizDoom Take Cover, "training in the dream")**: the controller is trained entirely inside the MDN-RNN's hallucinated latent environment (no real pixels rendered during dream training, since the agent acts on `z` directly), then transferred to the real game. Best agent: ~1092 +/- 556 over 100 rollouts (the OpenAI Gym leaderboard reference was 820 +/- 58).
- **Temperature `tau`**: a knob on the MDN-RNN sampling that injects extra uncertainty into the dream. Higher `tau` makes the dream harder and less exploitable; the paper finds agents trained at higher `tau` transfer better to the real environment.
- **The model-exploitation failure mode (stated explicitly)**: "our agent can easily find an adversarial policy that can fool our dynamics model -- it'll find a policy that looks good under our dynamics model, but will fail in the actual environment, usually because it visits states where the model is wrong because they are away from the training distribution." The authors observed the controller learning to make monsters never fire in the dream (an exploit that does not exist in the real game). They mitigate this by (a) the stochastic MDN-RNN (vs a deterministic LSTM, which suffers mode collapse and is trivially exploited) and (b) raising `tau`.
- **Lineage acknowledged**: the paper frames itself as distilling RNN-world-model + controller ideas from Schmidhuber's 1990-2015 line ("Making the World Differentiable", "On Learning to Think"), and connects to PILCO (Bayesian dynamics) and action-conditional video prediction.

## Interpretation (labeled inference)

- This is the conceptual root of the whole "predict the world to act" genealogy that later produces PlaNet, the Dreamer series, and (as a model-based-RL neighbor) the WAM survey's framing. The V-M-C split anticipates "perception encoder + latent dynamics model + policy/controller," which is exactly the skeleton every later latent-dynamics agent refines.
- The 2018 "model exploitation / adversarial policy / out-of-distribution model error" passage is, in plain terms, the **compounding-error** problem named before the term was standard: a policy optimized against an imperfect simulator drifts into regions where the simulator is wrong. Lane 10 owns the deep treatment; this note flags that the failure mode is as old as the modern field, and that injecting stochasticity (`tau`) was the first mitigation.
- Two design choices later got revised by the field: (1) training V, M, C in three separate stages was simple but lossy; later agents train the world model end-to-end and the policy jointly (Dreamer). (2) A reconstruction-based VAE latent is not the only option; MuZero and TD-MPC2 later drop reconstruction entirely (value-equivalent models).

## Mechanically useful vs research contribution

- **Mechanically useful**: the V-M-C decomposition is a clean teaching scaffold for "encoder + dynamics + controller." The `tau` insight (a learned stochastic simulator is less exploitable than a deterministic one, and you can dial its uncertainty) is a reusable guardrail principle for any advisory predictor: a predictor that is honest about uncertainty is safer to act against.
- **Research contribution (not this repo's)**: training a policy by RL inside a learned dream is a research line, not an engineering borrow, and it directly conflicts with this repo's hard rule (the LLM proposes, the runtime owns physical truth, a WAM stays advisory). Adopt the *idea* of a learned forward model used cautiously; do not adopt "train the actor inside the dream" as runtime authority.

## Relevance to this repo (one line)

Founding ancestor of latent forward models; teaches the encoder-dynamics-controller split and the first statement of model-exploitation, but its "train the policy in the dream" recipe is out of scope for an advisory WAM.

## Which WAM layer(s) it informs

Physical (foundational). It is the historical seed; it models pixel/latent dynamics of single-agent arcade environments and has no material/social/institutional content.
