## Primary-source facts

- **Research question**: existing video world models are single-agent; build a **multiplayer** video world model that simulates consistent multi-view observations (each player's first-person view) under multi-agent interaction.
- **Model**: video diffusion model adapting a single-agent pretrained video DiT to multiple perspectives with minimal modifications; autoregressive diffusion over past observations + actions -> future observations. Uses **Self Forcing** + a memory-efficient variant ("Checkpointed Self Forcing") for long-horizon autoregressive training.
- **Output/observation**: RGB first-person frames per player (pixel video). Action = WASD movement + mouse/camera + block place/dig (Minecraft mouse/keyboard).
- **Dataset**: **12.64M multiplayer frames** (6.32M per player) collected via **`solaris-engine`, pre-programmed bots** doing mining/attacking/building/navigating. NOT human social interaction; bot-scripted.
- **Eval framework**: held-out episode types for **Movement, Grounding, Memory, Building, Consistency**, scored by FID (visual quality) + **VLM-as-a-judge** answering verifiable questions about generated video (e.g., "is the observer seeing a built structure?", "are co-visible regions consistent across the two views?").

## Interpretation

- Solaris is the closest existing work to "multiplayer/social Minecraft world model," and that is exactly why the gap is sharp: it models **what each player visually sees**, not **social-material state**. Its "multi-agent interaction" is visual co-presence and view consistency, not possession, obligation, claims, trust, request/lend/return, or reputation. Its eval (Movement/Grounding/Memory/Building/Consistency) is about perceptual/spatial coherence, not social consequence.
- "Memory" here = remembering whether an object/player is still where it was (visual persistence), not memory of commitments or interaction history. "Building" = the observer pixel-sees a structure, not who owns/claimed it.

## Mechanically useful vs research contribution

- **Useful**: the **multiplayer data-collection engine pattern** (pre-programmed bots producing synchronized multi-agent gameplay) is a methodological precedent for generating social-scenario fixtures; the **VLM-as-judge verifiable-question** eval is adaptable to checking social-state claims (though the project should prefer structured verifier evidence over VLM judgment for physical truth). Open weights/data mean it could serve as a *visual sidecar* if pixel rollouts were ever wanted for human review.
- **Avoid/overclaim**: do not present Solaris as a social world model; it has no social-material state. Do not adopt its pixel-video output as the project's `o'`. Do not rely on VLM-as-judge as runtime authority (project rule: runtime owns physical truth via verifiers/artifacts).
- This is the single clearest "none predicts social-material state" datapoint.
