## Primary-source facts

- Framing: "Machine learning models always make a prediction, even when it is likely to be
  inaccurate," which is unsafe in decision-support settings where mistakes are costly. ML with a
  REJECT OPTION lets a model ABSTAIN ("I do not know") when it is likely to be wrong. The idea dates
  to 1970 (Chow) and recently regained interest.
- Two formalized types of rejection:
  - AMBIGUITY rejection: abstain near a decision boundary, where classes overlap and the model is
    genuinely uncertain about WHICH label (in-distribution uncertainty).
  - NOVELTY rejection: abstain on inputs unlike the training data (out-of-distribution / new regime),
    where the model has no basis to predict at all.
- The survey reviews and categorizes strategies to EVALUATE both the PREDICTIVE quality (accuracy on
  accepted predictions) and the REJECTIVE quality (whether the model abstains on the right cases), and
  catalogs architectures for models with rejection (separated, dependent, integrated rejectors) and
  standard learning techniques.

## Interpretation (my inference, labeled)

- This is the conceptual home of the repo's "advisory predictor that says I do not know." It supplies
  a distinction the repo needs and that a single confidence threshold blurs: AMBIGUITY rejection
  (the social situation is genuinely two-way: the partner might return the pickaxe or keep it) vs
  NOVELTY rejection (this is a scenario or relationship configuration the predictor has never seen,
  e.g. a multi-actor coalition it was never trained on). For a social-material WAM these have
  different fixes: ambiguity is reducible with more/cleaner data; novelty is a coverage gap that
  abstention should flag and that fresh held-out scenarios (lane 35) should probe.
- The dual evaluation (predictive AND rejective quality) is the right scoring frame for an advisory
  predictor: it is not enough that the predictor is accurate on what it emits; it must abstain on the
  cases it would get wrong, and BOTH must be measured. This complements the conformal selective-
  prediction guarantee in [[2107.07511-conformal-prediction-tutorial]] (which gives the threshold a
  formal accuracy guarantee) by naming what a good rejector should reject and how to score it.

## Mechanically useful vs research contribution

- Mechanically useful:
  - The ambiguity-vs-novelty split is a ready taxonomy for the repo's abstention behavior and for
    reporting WHY the predictor abstained (boundary uncertainty vs unfamiliar regime).
  - The predictive-plus-rejective evaluation pairing is a checklist item for the predictor-eval
    protocol: report accuracy-on-accepted and the abstention rate / what was abstained on, together.
- Research contribution (not the repo's to claim): the survey organizes decades of reject-option
  theory. The repo borrows the taxonomy and evaluation framing; the novelty is applying it to a
  verifier-grounded social-material predictor, where "novelty rejection" maps to unseen social
  configurations and the verifier supplies the ground truth that tells you whether an abstention was
  warranted.
