## Primary-source facts (LaTeX-verified)

- **The phenomenon.** Training generation after generation of generative models on (some mixture including) the previous generation's synthetic outputs forms an **autophagous ("self-consuming") loop**. The paper's analogy is mad cow disease; the named condition is **Model Autophagy Disorder (MAD)**.
- **Primary conclusion (verbatim).** "Without enough fresh real data in each generation of an autophagous loop, future generative models are doomed to have their quality (precision) or diversity (recall) progressively decrease." Quality maps to **precision**, diversity to **recall** (standard generative-model metrics).
- **Three loop families studied.**
    1. **Fully synthetic loop**: each generation trains only on the previous model's synthetic data. Result: either precision or recall (or both) decreases over generations.
    2. **Synthetic augmentation loop**: a fixed set of real data plus accumulating synthetic data. Models the standard practice of scraping the internet (which now contains both). Collapse is slowed but a fixed real set is not enough to fully prevent degradation.
    3. **Fresh data loop**: enough NEW real data each generation. Result: "with enough fresh real data, the quality and diversity of the generative models do not degrade over generations."
- **Sampling bias is decisive.** Users and models "cherry-pick" high-quality samples (or use guidance/truncation that trades diversity for quality). The paper shows: without sampling bias, autophagy causes a rapid decline of BOTH quality and diversity; WITH a quality-favoring bias, quality (precision) can be maintained but diversity (recall) degrades even more rapidly. The bias is parameterized by a single universal "biased sampling parameter" lambda.
- **Empirical breadth.** Analytic results on Gaussians and Gaussian mixtures; empirical confirmation across DDPM (diffusion), StyleGAN-2, WGAN, and Normalizing Flows, on MNIST and FFHQ. A succession of StyleGAN-2 models trained on the prior generation's images showed progressively amplified cross-hatch artifacts (an architectural "fingerprint" reinforced by the loop). LAION-5B (the Stable Diffusion training set) already contains synthetic images from earlier generative models, so the loop is "all but inevitable" in practice.

## Interpretation (flagged as inference)

- MAD is the second independent confirmation (after the Curse of Recursion 2305.17493) of the same limit, with a cleaner operational framing: collapse splits into a quality axis (precision) and a diversity axis (recall), and a cherry-picking bias can hide the collapse on one axis (quality stays high) while accelerating it on the other (diversity dies). This is the single most transferable nuance of the paper for a self-improvement loop: a loop that keeps only its high-confidence successes can look like it is improving (precision up) while its behavioral diversity silently collapses.
- The "fresh real data each generation prevents MAD" result is the same lesson as 2305.17493's 10%-real-anchor and 2404.01413's accumulation: the loop needs a continuing external information source. The repo's runtime verifier supplies fresh externally-checked labels every cycle, which is the strongest form of "fresh data."
- The cherry-picking warning maps directly onto a self-improvement loop that filters by its own success signal: filtering by quality is exactly the bias that preserves precision and kills diversity. A loop that re-trains a Physical/Material WAM only on verifier-PASSED transitions risks narrowing diversity (it stops predicting failure modes). This is why H5's note insists the loop keep verifier-FAILED transitions too.

## Mechanically useful vs research contribution

- **Mechanically useful**: the precision-vs-recall decomposition gives the repo two distinct collapse detectors to monitor in any loop (is predictive accuracy holding? is the diversity of predicted/attempted behaviors holding?); the sampling-bias result is the formal reason a confidence-filtered loop needs an explicit diversity guard.
- **Not a contribution to copy**: MAD is a failure mode, not a method. The mitigation ("inject enough fresh real data") is the borrowable principle, instantiated in the repo as "re-ground every cycle with the runtime verifier."
- **Honest bound it supplies**: a self-improvement loop that filters by its own quality signal will preserve apparent quality while collapsing diversity unless fresh external data keeps entering. Watch both axes.

## WAM layer(s) informed

Cross-layer (generative-model limit). Most relevant to any WAM-predictor self-training loop at the Physical/Material layers, where a quality-filtered loop could narrow the diversity of predicted outcomes.
