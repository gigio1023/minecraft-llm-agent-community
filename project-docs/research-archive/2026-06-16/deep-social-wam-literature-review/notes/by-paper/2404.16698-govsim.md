# GovSim, Cooperate or Collapse: Emergence of Sustainable Cooperation in a Society of LLM Agents

- title: Cooperate or Collapse: Emergence of Sustainable Cooperation in a Society of LLM Agents
- authors: Giorgio Piatti, Zhijing Jin, Max Kleiman-Weiner, Bernhard Schölkopf, Mrinmaya Sachan, Rada Mihalcea
- year: 2024
- venue/source: NeurIPS 2024 Datasets & Benchmarks; arXiv 2404.16698v4
- arxiv_id: 2404.16698
- urls: paper https://arxiv.org/abs/2404.16698 ; code https://github.com/giorgiopiatti/GovSim ; hf https://huggingface.co/papers/2404.16698
- source availability: read via `hf papers read 2404.16698` (HTML); open-source sim + prompts + web interface.

## Primary-source facts (what the paper states)

- Research question: can LLM agents achieve and maintain *sustainable cooperation*
  when collectively governing a shared regenerating resource (a common-pool
  resource / public-goods problem)? Explicitly inspired by Hardin's "Tragedy of
  the Commons" and Ostrom-style bottom-up norm formation.
- Three scenarios, all mathematically equivalent regeneration dynamics: `fishery`
  (shared lake, fish double each month up to capacity 100), `pasture` (sheep on
  shared grass), `pollution` (factories pollute a shared river). Resource
  collapses when stock falls below C=5.
- Formalized as a partially observable Markov game interleaving actions,
  observations, rewards with free natural-language dialogue. Two phases per month:
  (1) harvesting (private simultaneous actions, then made public), (2) discussion
  (free NL communication).
- Sustainability threshold f(t): the max that can be extracted at t without
  reducing stock at t+1 given growth multiplier g.
- Metrics suite (they cite Perolat et al.; "no single scalar metric can track the
  entire state of a mixed-incentive repeated game"):
  - **Survival time** m: longest period the resource stays above collapse C.
  - Total gain, **efficiency** (vs optimal sustainable use), **equality** (Gini
    over agents' gains), **over-usage** (fraction of actions above sustainability
    threshold).
- Headline results: all but the most powerful LLMs fail; best agent survival rate
  < 54%. Failure mechanism: inability to mentally simulate the long-term effect of
  greedy actions on the group equilibrium (i.e., weak forward prediction of
  social-material consequences).
- Mechanism findings:
  - Communication is critical: ablating dialogue raises resource over-use by 21%.
  - Negotiation is the dominant dialogue type (62% of dialogues).
  - Ability to form beliefs about other agents correlates 0.83 with community
    survival time.
  - "Universalization" reasoning (prompt the agent to consider "what if everyone
    did this?", a moral-philosophy heuristic) significantly improves survival.
  - Robustness probe: introduce a **greedy newcomer** who has not observed the
    norm history -> increases inequality and can collapse cooperation. This is a
    direct operational test of norm robustness to an uninformed entrant.

## Interpretation (my reading, labeled inference)

- GovSim is the closest existing computational analogue to Ostrom's IAD on the
  commons, but it deliberately strips the *physical substrate*: the "resource" is
  an abstract integer that doubles. There is no inventory, no tool, no container,
  no place, no movement, so the social claims rest entirely on declared numbers
  and dialogue, not on world-verifiable possession.
- The 0.83 belief-accuracy/survival correlation is essentially evidence that a
  **Social WAM capability** (predicting others' future actions) is the bottleneck,
  not dialogue fluency. This is the single most transferable empirical claim for
  this lane.

## Mechanically useful vs research contribution

- Mechanically useful for this repo:
  - The **metric decomposition** (survival, efficiency, equality/Gini,
    over-usage) maps cleanly onto the repo's `weak_commons` /
    `public_furnace_v1` scenario family.
  - The **greedy-newcomer perturbation** is a ready-made norm-robustness test for
    the repo's `weak_commons_surplus_use_and_dispute_v1` scenario.
  - Universalization-as-prompt is a cheap intervention worth an ablation.
- What this repo should avoid / overclaim risk:
  - Do NOT adopt GovSim's abstract-number commons as the core economy. The repo
    explicitly demotes shared-commons economy to `weak commons`; GovSim's whole
    world IS the commons, which is the framing the repo rejects.
  - GovSim's "cooperation" is scored from declared harvest numbers; in Minecraft
    the analogue must be inventory/container/block-verified, or it regresses to
    dialogue plausibility, exactly the failure mode the repo guards against.

## WAM layer(s) informed

- Primarily **Institutional / Settlement WAM** (norm formation, commons
  governance, robustness to newcomers) and **Material / Economic WAM** (resource
  flow, over-extraction cost to others). Secondary **Social WAM** (belief about
  others predicts survival).
