## What it introduced and why it mattered (plain language)

V-JEPA 2 is the non-generative camp's flagship world model: it learns to predict the
future in representation space (not pixels) from a million hours of internet video, then,
with only ~62 hours of robot data, adds an action-conditioned predictor (V-JEPA 2-AC) and
uses it to actually control a real robot arm zero-shot by planning. It matters because it
is the strongest demonstration that you can build a usable, controllable world model
without ever generating a pixel of the future, directly answering the generative camp's
"video models are world simulators" with "predict latents and plan in them instead." It is
the most direct counter-exhibit in the whole debate, from LeCun's own group.

Newcomer terms:
- Action-conditioned world model: a predictor `p(z' | z, a)` that forecasts the next latent
  state given the current latent state and an action.
- Planning by energy minimization / Model Predictive Control (MPC): pick an action sequence
  that drives the model's imagined future latent toward a goal latent, execute the first
  action, observe, re-plan (receding horizon).
- Cross-Entropy Method (CEM): a sampling-based optimizer used here to search action sequences.
- Mask-denoising feature prediction: the self-supervised task, predict the features of masked
  video patches in latent space.

## Primary-source facts

- Two stages: (1) action-free pretraining of the V-JEPA 2 encoder (up to 1B params) on >1M
  hours of internet video + 1M images, via mask-denoising feature prediction (L1 loss in
  latent space, EMA target-encoder), with progressive resolution training beyond 16-frame
  clips. (2) freeze the encoder, train V-JEPA 2-AC, a 300M-param block-causal transformer that
  autoregressively predicts the next-frame representation conditioned on past states, actions,
  and end-effector states, from ~62 hours of unlabeled Droid robot video.
- Planning: at each step, optimize an action sequence minimizing a goal-conditioned energy
  `E = || P(actions; state) - z_goal ||_1` (L1 distance between imagined future latent and goal
  latent) via CEM; execute first action, re-plan (MPC). Goals given as images.
- Results: Understanding, 77.3 top-1 on Something-Something v2 (motion); aligned with an LLM,
  SOTA at the 8B class on multiple physical-reasoning VideoQA benchmarks (PerceptionTest 84.0,
  TempCompass 76.9, etc.). Prediction, 39.7 recall-at-5 on Epic-Kitchens-100 action
  anticipation (44% relative gain). Planning, zero-shot Grasp and Pick-and-Place with novel
  objects in new labs on a Franka arm, no task-specific training or reward, no robot data from
  those labs.
- Verbatim debate-relevant claims (their intro):
  - On the generative camp: action-conditioned video-generation world models "only demonstrate
    limited results in robot execution" and "often emphasizes the evaluation of the faithfulness
    of the predictions and visual quality instead of planning capabilities, perhaps due to the
    computational cost of planning by generating video."
  - On why JEPA: it "focuses on learning representations for predictable aspects of a scene
    (e.g., the trajectory of an object in motion) while ignoring unpredictable details that
    generative objectives emphasize, since they make pixel-level predictions (e.g., the precise
    location of each blade of grass in a field, or each leaf on a tree)."
- Stated limitations (theirs, explicit): (1) sensitivity to camera position (no explicit
  calibration; they hand-tuned camera placement); (2) long-horizon planning limited by
  autoregressive error accumulation AND exponential growth of the action search space;
  (3) goals must be given as images, not language (language-goal alignment is future work).

## Interpretation (inference, labeled)

- V-JEPA 2 is the best evidence that "do not generate pixels, predict latents and plan" is not
  just a slogan, it closes the loop to real robot control. For the project's structured-state
  bet, this is the strongest "a non-pixel world model can actually drive behavior" citation.
- Its limitations are the honest ones the project inherits if it builds any latent/structured
  WAM and rolls it forward: compounding error and search-space blowup over long horizons, and
  the awkwardness of image-only goals. A structured social WAM that predicts a few typed deltas
  one or two steps ahead (advisory, short-horizon) sidesteps the worst of these, which is a
  point worth making explicitly rather than assuming the V-JEPA-2 robot results transfer.
- Caveat on "understanding": the VideoQA / anticipation numbers are benchmark scores, not proof
  of human-like causal understanding. They are strong, but the debate (and 2407.10311) argues
  even V-JEPA-class models have not learned a complete world model. Present as strong-but-bounded.

## Mechanically useful vs research contribution

- Mechanically useful: the "freeze a representation, then train a small action-conditioned
  predictor on top, then plan by minimizing distance-to-goal in latent space" recipe is a
  concrete, low-data pattern. For this repo the analog is: take typed Minecraft+social state as
  the representation, train a predictor of next typed state given an action, and use it
  advisorily to compare candidate actions against a desired social-material outcome, never to
  execute or override the runtime. The honest limitation list is a ready-made risk section.
- Avoid / overclaim: V-JEPA 2-AC is a learned-latent robot controller using MPC; adopting MPC
  control as runtime authority conflicts with "LLM proposes, runtime owns truth, WAM advisory."
  Do not claim a social WAM will plan like V-JEPA 2 on robots; borrow the principle and the
  limitation taxonomy, not the control role.

Cross-cutting; the central non-generative counter-position. Primary support for structured/
