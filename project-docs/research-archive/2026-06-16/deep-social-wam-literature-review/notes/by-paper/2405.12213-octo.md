# Octo: An Open-Source Generalist Robot Policy

- **title**: Octo: An Open-Source Generalist Robot Policy
- **authors**: Octo Model Team (Ghosh, Walke, Pertsch, Black, Mees, Dasari, Hejna, Kreiman, Xu, Luo, ... Levine, Finn) (UC Berkeley / Stanford / CMU / Google DeepMind)
- **year**: 2024
- **venue/source**: arXiv 2405.12213; RSS 2024; project octo-models.github.io
- **arxiv_id**: 2405.12213
- **urls**: paper https://arxiv.org/abs/2405.12213 ; weights https://huggingface.co/rail-berkeley/octo-base-1.5 (MIT) ; LaTeX at `papers/latex/2405.12213/`
- **source availability**: latex (full, `sections/03_approach.tex` etc.)
- **WAM layer(s)**: Physical (control). Reactive policy (no `o'` prediction), so not a WAM. Architecturally important: diffusion action head + modular tokenizers.

Part of the VLA-canon set missing from wave-1. Octo is the modular, fine-tune-friendly
generalist transformer policy, and (with pi-0) one of the two canon members that uses a
**diffusion/flow action head** instead of discrete action tokens.

## Primary-source facts

- **Research question**: build an open generalist robot policy that is *flexible* -
  one model that can be fine-tuned to **new sensors and new action spaces** (not just
  new tasks), unlike monolithic VLAs whose input/output shape is locked at pretraining.
- **Model**: a transformer policy `pi(a_{1:H}|o)` with three modular parts:
  1. **Input tokenizers** (modality-specific): language via a pretrained **t5-base
     (111M)** encoder; image observations/goals via a shallow conv stack split into
     flattened patches. Tokens for absent modalities are masked out.
  2. **Transformer backbone** with **block-wise causal masking** (observation tokens
     attend to same/earlier timesteps + task tokens). Learned **readout tokens** (like
     BERT's `[CLS]`) passively read internal embeddings without being attended to.
  3. **Readout heads**: a lightweight **diffusion action head** on the readout-token
     embeddings predicts a chunk of consecutive actions.
- **The flexibility property (its main idea)**: because inputs/outputs are modular
  tokens + heads, you can **add or remove a camera, a proprioception stream, or swap
  the action space at fine-tune time while retaining all pretrained transformer
  weights**, only adding a small encoder / positional embeddings / new head. Monolithic
  designs (RT-1 style) would need to re-initialize large components.
- **Action head / training objective**: **conditional diffusion decoding** (DDPM).
  Crucially, only **one forward pass of the transformer backbone per action chunk**;
  the multi-step denoising runs entirely inside the small diffusion head. This
  diffusion head beat both MSE heads and discretized-token action heads (RT-1 style) in
  zero-shot and fine-tuning. Trained with the standard DDPM noise-prediction loss.
- **Observation / conditioning**: 2 frames of history (diminishing returns beyond
  one extra frame); conditions on **either** a language instruction **or** a goal image
  (randomly dropped per example), with hindsight goal relabeling for unlabeled data.
- **Dataset shape**: **800k trajectories** curated from the Open X-Embodiment dataset
  (the largest robot manipulation dataset at the time); heterogeneous embodiments,
  sensors, and labels. Curation removes no-image / non-delta-EE-control / low-res /
  niche datasets; "more diverse" datasets get 2x weight.
- **Sizes / cost**: Octo-Small (ViT-S backbone) and Octo-Base (ViT-B). Octo-Base
  trained 300k steps, batch 2048, on a TPU v4-128 pod in **14 hours**; fine-tune on one
  24GB A5000 in ~5 hours.
- **Evaluation**: tested on 9 real robot setups across 4 institutions, including
  fine-tuning to **new observations** (force-torque), **new action spaces** (joint
  position control), and **new embodiments** (bimanual). Serves as a versatile
  initialization.
- **Availability**: fully open: Octo-Small / Octo-Base checkpoints, JAX fine-tuning +
  pretraining pipelines, standalone OXE data loaders.

## Interpretation (inference)

- Octo's two distinctive contributions vs RT-2/OpenVLA: (1) a **diffusion action
  head** that models multimodal continuous action distributions and beats discrete-token
  heads (the same direction pi-0 takes with flow matching), and (2) **modular tokenizers
  + readout heads** so the *interface* is reconfigurable at fine-tune time. The latter is
  the property that makes it a "generalist" usable across heterogeneous robots.
- Reactive policy, no `o'`. Octo does not predict the future; it is the "act" lineage.

## Mechanically useful vs research contribution

- **Useful for THIS repo**: the **modular-interface principle** ("add/remove an
  observation channel or swap the action representation without retraining the core")
  is a good architectural lesson for any future learned helper the repo might add: keep
  the predictor's I/O modular so the typed state schema can evolve without a full
  retrain. The "one backbone pass, denoise in a small head" pattern is a cheap-inference
  template if a learned predictor is ever wanted.
- **Avoid / overclaim**: Octo is single-task-horizon manipulation from pixels; nothing
  social/material. Do not present its diffusion head or generalist claims as relevant to
  a structured social predictor; the takeaway is the *interface modularity*, not the
  manipulation results.
