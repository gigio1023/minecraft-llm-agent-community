Social intelligence is the ability to understand others and act wisely in social situations \cite{doi:10.2466/pr0.1973.33.3.839}.
While LLMs show potential in simulating human behavior \cite{xie2024largelanguagemodelagents}, their social intelligence remains underexplored \cite{zhou2024real}.
Current research on evaluating social intelligence in LLMs falls into two main categories.
The first involves static, non-interactive assessments that evaluate models through Q\&A tasks based on commonsense reasoning about social situations \cite{sap2019socialiqacommonsensereasoningsocial,Zadeh_2019_CVPR}.
The second involves interactive benchmarks, where LLMs are assessed through goal-oriented interactions in role-playing scenarios~\cite{zhou2024sotopiainteractiveevaluationsocial,chen2024socialbenchsocialityevaluationroleplaying,wang2024objectivelybenchmarkingsocialintelligence,liu2023agentbenchevaluatingllmsagents}.
AgentSense uses a bottom-up approach to create scenarios from scripts, allowing evaluating agents’ capabilities in more diverse settings.
