LLMs are increasingly been used to construct role-playing agents (RPAs)~\cite{chen2024personapersonalizationsurveyroleplaying}, which enable efficient simulation of typical representatives, from individuals~\cite{shao2023characterllmtrainableagentroleplaying,argyle2023out} to demographic groups~\cite{li2023camel,jiang2024evaluating}.
These agents can embody various personas to coordinate, collaborate, exchange information, and compete with one another~\cite{chen2024personapersonalizationsurveyroleplaying, zhou2024sotopiainteractiveevaluationsocial}.
Recently, RPAs have been applied in various domains, e.g., psychotherapy~\cite{article}, economics~\cite{fu2023improvinglanguagemodelnegotiation}, and social research~\cite{doi:10.1126/science.adi1778}.
AgentSense leverages this by simulating social interaction scenarios through agents with diverse personalities and social goals.
