# Benchmark Metrics Matrix (Lane 3: LLM Social Simulation and Social Benchmarks)

Each row is a social benchmark or simulator. Columns:

- **Social construct measured**: what social thing the work claims to measure.
- **Grounded vs dialogue-only**: is the metric anchored to *verified environment state* (G), or to *judged text/role-play* (D), or a mix?
- **Metric form**: how the number is produced.
- **Environment**: where it runs.
- **Repro**: code/data availability for independent re-run.
- **Minecraft-transition translation**: the verified social-material change this would become in the repo's runtime, using repo vocabulary (`personal possession`, `material claim`, `public affordance`, `weak commons`, `obligation/credit`, `memory`).
- **Validity caveat**: the strongest reason the metric is *plausibility, not consequence* (or the boundary it respects).

Legend: **D** = dialogue/role-play/text-judged. **G** = grounded in verified environment state. **D→G** = judged today, but the construct could be grounded. Repo's whole point is the **G** column.

## How to read this matrix (one-paragraph synthesis)

Almost every social benchmark in the field sits in the **D** column: the number is produced by an LLM (or human) judging text or role-play, with no environment state that could refute the claim. The few partial exceptions (GLEE, Melting Pot, parts of MultiAgentBench, Concordia's grounded variables) either abstract the "world" to a number, use a simplified gridworld, or trust a *generative* Game Master to enforce grounding. The validity instruments (SimBench, Don't Trust, belief-behavior, 1000 People) independently converge on one message: plausible output is not validity; the best simulators are modest even at the easiest static task; more reasoning/compute does not help; and stated disposition does not predict enacted behavior. The repo's contribution is to move the whole table into the **G** column for an embodied substrate, every social score backed by a Mineflayer-verified material/claim/obligation/memory delta, with prediction accuracy and acting outcome scored as separate axes, and partner/seed generalization always specified.
