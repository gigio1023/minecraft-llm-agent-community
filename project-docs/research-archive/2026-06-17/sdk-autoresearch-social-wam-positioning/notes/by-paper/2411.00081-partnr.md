# PARTNR: A Benchmark for Planning and Reasoning in Embodied Multi-agent Tasks

- title: PARTNR: A Benchmark for Planning and Reasoning in Embodied Multi-agent Tasks
  (PARTNR = Planning And Reasoning Tasks in humaN-Robot collaboration)
- authors: Chang, Gervet, et al. (Meta FAIR)
- year: 2024 - venue: arXiv / ICLR 2025 - arxiv_id: 2411.00081
- urls: paper https://arxiv.org/abs/2411.00081 ; code https://github.com/facebookresearch/partnr-planner ; hf https://huggingface.co/papers/2411.00081
- source read: abstract + secondary summaries via WebSearch (no body deep-read; figures
  below are claim-only from the abstract/summaries).

This is the lane's "even the biggest embodied multi-agent robotics benchmark scores task
completion, not material consequence" anchor. It runs on the Habitat stack
(`2310.13724-habitat-3.md`).

## Primary-source facts (abstract + summaries, claim-only)

- Scale: **100,000 natural-language tasks**, **60 houses**, **5,819 unique objects**. The
  largest human-robot-collaboration benchmark of its kind.
- Task character: everyday household activities with **spatial, temporal, and
  heterogeneous agent-capability constraints** (some tasks need both agents, some have
  ordering constraints).
- Generation: a semi-automated pipeline uses LLMs to author tasks, with **simulation in
  the loop for grounding and verification** (a task is checked against the simulator state,
  not just judged as text).
- Evaluation axes: planning, perception, skill execution. Reported findings: SoTA LLMs
  show **poor coordination, failures in task tracking, and weak recovery from errors**.
- Human pairing (claim-only): an LLM paired with a real human needs **1.5x** the steps of
  two humans and **1.1x** the steps of a single human; a fine-tuned small planner can match
  a model 9x larger while being 8.6x faster.

## Interpretation (labeled, distinction-relevant)

- PARTNR's "collaboration" is **joint task completion under constraints** (move these
  objects to these places, in this order, with two agents). Its truth source is good (it
  verifies against simulator state, simulation-in-the-loop, which is the right stance, the
  same stance Minecraft benchmarks like MineNPC-Task take, see the old archive's
  `minecraft-agent-benchmarks.md`). But the **scored outcome is task success and step
  efficiency**, not a durable social-material delta.
- There is no possession that persists, no obligation created by a transfer, no trust or
  credit between the two agents, no claim over a contested resource, and no continuation
  past the task. The "collaboration" is coordination, not consequence.
- So PARTNR demonstrates the same gap on the robotics-sim side that the social benchmarks
  show on the dialogue side: a clean, verifiable, large benchmark that nonetheless scores
  **competence and coordination**, not material-grounded social consequence. The substrate
  (Habitat) could in principle carry possession state, but the benchmark does not, and the
  per-humanoid cost (Habitat 3.0) caps the agent count well below society scale.

## Mechanically useful vs research contribution

- Useful: PARTNR's **simulation-in-the-loop verification** of LLM-authored tasks is a
  strong methodology precedent for autogenerated-scenario validity (a deterministic check
  that the generated task is actually grounded), directly relevant to an autoresearch loop
  that proposes scenarios. Its heterogeneous-capability constraints are a good reference
  for designing asymmetric-capability Minecraft scenarios.
- Avoid / overclaim: do not cite PARTNR as social-consequence modeling. It is a
  human-robot **coordination/competence** benchmark. Its value to the lane is as evidence
  that robotics-sim multi-agent work, even at 100k tasks, stops at coordination.
