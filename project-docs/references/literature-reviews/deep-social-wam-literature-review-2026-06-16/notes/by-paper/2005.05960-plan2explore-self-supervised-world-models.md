## Primary-source facts (what the paper states)

- Research question: can an agent learn a TASK-AGNOSTIC world model by self-supervised exploration that seeks EXPECTED future novelty (via planning, before reaching states), then adapt zero-shot or few-shot to downstream tasks from images?
- Architecture: a Dreamer-style latent world model (RSSM: image encoder, latent prior/posterior transition, reward and image decoders, trained by ELBO) PLUS an **ensemble of K=5 lightweight one-step models** that each predict the next image-embedding from `(latent state, action)`.
- Exploration signal: the intrinsic reward is the **disagreement (variance) among the ensemble's predictions**, `Var({mu(w_k, s_t, a_t)})`. This approximates the **expected information gain** / model epistemic uncertainty about the next observation; aleatoric (irreducible) noise is excluded because it cancels in the variance over ensemble MEANS. Crucially the exploration policy is trained IN IMAGINATION (Dreamer rollouts) to maximize this reward, so the agent plans toward states it EXPECTS to be novel, rather than retrospectively rewarding novelty after arrival.
- Loop (reward-free): train world model + ensemble on the replay buffer; train exploration policy in imagination to maximize disagreement; execute it; expand the buffer; repeat. Observation: 64x64 RGB; action: continuous control (DM Control Suite); no proprioception.
- Adaptation: after reward-free exploration, distill a task reward into a reward head and train a task policy in imagination, zero-shot (no new interaction) or few-shot (~100-150 episodes).
- Results: zero-shot, Plan2Explore reaches ~81% of the Dreamer oracle (which trained WITH task rewards); few-shot (150 episodes) ~92% of oracle. It generalizes across tasks (explore once on Cheetah, solve run-forward/backward/flip zero-shot) where the task-specific Dreamer fails on the other tasks. Outperforms ICM-style curiosity and MAX adapted to images, especially on hard-exploration tasks.
- Limitations (theirs/spotted): depends on an accurate Dreamer latent world model; fixed ensemble size; non-stationary intrinsic reward decays as the model improves (exploration can saturate); homoscedastic-noise assumption.

## Interpretation (flagged as inference)

- Plan2Explore is the cleanest classical statement of the lane's core idea: explore specifically to IMPROVE THE WORLD MODEL, and measure where to go by the model's own epistemic uncertainty (ensemble disagreement = expected information gain), planning toward expected novelty inside the model. It is the model-based, look-ahead version of ICM/RND curiosity.
- For the thesis, it supplies the principled exploration objective (information gain about dynamics) that WorldLLM approximates with prediction error, and it cleanly separates epistemic (reducible, worth chasing) from aleatoric (irreducible noise, not worth chasing). That separation is the technically correct form of the lane's social bound: chase uncertainty you can resolve, not the irreducible unpredictability of another actor.

## Mechanically useful vs research contribution

- **Mechanically useful**: the disagreement/expected-information-gain objective is a better-targeted exploration signal than raw prediction error for improving an advisory WAM: an ensemble of cheap WAM-delta predictors, steer the actor toward `(state, action)` where the ensemble DISAGREES (the WAM is uncertain, not merely the world is noisy), and let the runtime verifier label the outcome to shrink that disagreement. Planning toward expected novelty maps onto using the advisory WAM's own uncertainty to prioritize which Action Card to try when the goal is model improvement.
- **What to avoid / overclaim**: Plan2Explore is pixel-based continuous control on DM Control with a learned latent simulator the repo does not have; the repo's WAM is structured-state and advisory, not a Dreamer simulator the policy is trained inside. Do not import the imagination-training pipeline; import the EPISTEMIC-vs-ALEATORIC distinction and the disagreement-as-information-gain signal. Also note the saturation limitation: a Minecraft WAM-improvement loop driven by disagreement will stop being informative once the WAM is confident, which is fine for the Physical layer but means the loop will not, by itself, keep probing the open-ended social layers.
