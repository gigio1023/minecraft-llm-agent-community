# GLEE: A Unified Framework and Benchmark for Language-based Economic Environments

- title: GLEE: A Unified Framework and Benchmark for Language-based Economic Environments
- authors: Eilam Shapira, Omer Madmon, Itamar Reinman, Samuel Joseph Amouyal, Roi Reichart, Moshe Tennenholtz (and Jeffrey S. Rosenschein per OpenReview)
- year: 2024
- venue: ICLR 2025 / OpenReview
- arxiv_id: 2410.05254
- urls: paper https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.05254 ; code+data https://github.com/eilamshapira/GLEE
- source read: LaTeX in full (papers/latex/2410.05254/paper.tex), intro + game families + metric definitions

## Primary-source facts

- Research question: a standardized, richly parameterized benchmark for **two-player, sequential, language-based economic games**, so LLM strategic/economic behavior is comparable across studies.
- Three game families (each formally defined, game-theoretically grounded):
  1. **Bargaining** (Rubinstein 1982): alternating offers to split a sum M, discount factors δ_A, δ_B make delay costly.
  2. **Negotiation** (bilateral trade): seller and buyer alternate price proposals over an indivisible good.
  3. **Persuasion** (info asymmetry): an informed seller sends messages to persuade an uninformed buyer; repeated, reputation matters.
- Degrees of freedom: game **horizon** (finite/infinite, known/unknown), **information structure** (know opponent's preferences or not), **communication form** (free natural language vs structured messages), plus payoffs (δ, M, T).
- Metrics (normalized to [0,1], higher = better), defined exactly:
  - **Efficiency** = normalized sum of discounted payoffs at agreement (e.g. bargaining: δ_A^{t-1}-p + δ_B^{t-1}-(1−p); 0 if no agreement).
  - **Fairness** = 1 − 4-(p − ½)² at agreement (1 if no trade, since equal-zero is "fair"); explicit fairness-efficiency tradeoff (no-trade is maximally fair but minimally efficient).
  - **Self-gain** (individual agent payoff).
- Data: 587K LLM decisions across 80K+ games, 13 LLMs; plus a human-vs-LLM dataset via oTree.
- Findings:
  - Economic outcome (efficiency/fairness/self-gain) is significantly shaped by market parameters (information structure, communication form, horizon).
  - **No absolute best-performing model on any measure; one LLM's performance strongly depends on its competitor's LLM choice.**
  - Human behavior is more extreme than LLMs': humans either outperform all LLMs or fall behind entirely, depending on game and role.

## Interpretation (my inference, labeled)

- GLEE supplies the repo's **material/economic WAM** with formal, defensible metrics. Efficiency and fairness as defined here translate almost directly to a Minecraft material-claim ledger: efficiency of a tool/resource trade (did the exchange leave both better off vs no-deal), fairness of a split (how far from an equal division). Crucially, in GLEE these are computed from the *agreed outcome* (a verifiable terminal state), not from transcript sentiment - that is the property the repo wants.
- The "no absolute best model; depends on the competitor" finding is the economic-game version of S3AP's and AgentSense's partner-dependence: it strongly motivates the repo's *partner/seed generalization matrix* (same actor vs different partner model). A social/economic score is meaningless without specifying the counterpart.
- The limit for the repo: GLEE is still pure text - the "good" being divided is a number, not a Minecraft item with durability, location, and physical access. GLEE measures whether agents *agree on a number*; the repo measures whether a *physical item actually moved and what social obligation that created*. GLEE's outcome is verifiable within its abstract game; the repo's is verifiable in a physical world.

## Mechanically useful vs research contribution

- Mechanically useful:
  - Efficiency / fairness / self-gain formulas -> directly adaptable scoring for `borrowed_tool_v1`, `scarce_food_v1`, trade scenarios (compute over verified item transfers, not text).
  - The three families (bargaining/negotiation/persuasion) -> scenario templates for resource division, tool sale/lending, and asymmetric-knowledge persuasion.
  - Degrees-of-freedom parameterization (horizon, information, communication form) -> ablation axes for the repo's benchmark.
- Avoid / overclaim:
  - Do not reduce the project to abstract text bargaining (the repo's research frame explicitly warns against "abstract text-only bargaining" and "shared-resources ideology").
  - Do not report a single per-model economic score; report it conditioned on the partner.

## WAM layer(s) informed

- Material/Economic WAM (primary): formal efficiency/fairness/self-gain over exchange outcomes. Social WAM (secondary): persuasion/negotiation as social actions with reputational consequence.
