# ICM: Curiosity-driven Exploration by Self-supervised Prediction (with RND comparison)

- **title**: Curiosity-driven Exploration by Self-supervised Prediction (Intrinsic Curiosity Module, ICM)
- **authors**: Deepak Pathak, Pulkit Agrawal, Alexei A. Efros, Trevor Darrell (UC Berkeley)
- **year**: 2017
- **venue/source**: ICML 2017
- **arxiv_id**: 1705.05363
- **urls**: paper https://arxiv.org/abs/1705.05363 ; code https://pathak22.github.io/noreward-rl/
- **source availability**: latex (tarball extracted, `papers/latex/1705.05363/`)
- **also covered here for comparison**: RND (Random Network Distillation), Burda et al., ICLR 2019, arxiv 1810.12894 (LaTeX at `papers/latex/1810.12894/`), the next major curiosity method.

## Primary-source facts (ICM)

- Research question: with no or sparse extrinsic reward, can an agent explore and learn useful skills using **curiosity** = the error in predicting the consequence of its own actions?
- Intrinsic Curiosity Module: two learned models. (1) An **inverse dynamics** model encodes states into a feature space phi via predicting the action `a_t` from `phi(s_t), phi(s_{t+1})`. This forces phi to represent only the parts of the world the agent can affect. (2) A **forward dynamics** model predicts `phi_hat(s_{t+1})` from `phi(s_t), a_t`. The **intrinsic reward** is the forward prediction error `r^i_t = (eta/2) || phi_hat(s_{t+1}) - phi(s_{t+1}) ||^2` in that learned feature space. Policy = A3C.
- Critical design point: because phi is learned via inverse dynamics, prediction-error curiosity in phi-space "ignores the aspects of the environment that cannot affect the agent." The paper explicitly raises the **noisy-TV problem** (a screen of white noise makes every state novel and unpredictable) and argues the inverse-dynamics feature space mitigates it (a pixel-space forward model, ICM-pixels, does not and breaks under added visual noise).
- Observation: 42x42 grayscale frames (4 stacked). Action space: discrete (4 in VizDoom, 14 in Super Mario Bros). Data is generated by the agent's own interaction; exploration runs reward-free (except a sparse goal reward in some VizDoom settings).
- Results: in sparse/very-sparse VizDoom navigation, ICM+A3C reaches the goal where vanilla A3C fails; in reward-free Mario it crosses ~30% of level 1 with no reward; pre-training with curiosity then transferring to new levels explores faster than from scratch.
- Limitations (theirs): stalls at long-horizon bottlenecks needing precise 15-20 action sequences (Mario pit); applicability is best where interaction opportunities are not too rare.

## Primary-source facts (RND, for comparison)

- RND replaces the learned-feature forward model with a simpler novelty signal: the intrinsic reward is the error of a trainable predictor network trying to match a **fixed, randomly-initialized target network** applied to the observation. Where the predictor has seen similar observations, error is low; novel observations give high error.
- Because the target is deterministic and inside the predictor's model class, RND sidesteps the noisy-TV / stochasticity trap that prediction-error curiosity (ICM) suffers from (no aleatoric noise to chase). Uses PPO with separate intrinsic (non-episodic) and extrinsic (episodic) value heads.
- Headline result: state of the art on Montezuma's Revenge (a famously hard sparse-reward Atari game) without demonstrations or privileged state, finding ~22 of 24 first-level rooms on average and occasionally clearing level 1. Limitation (theirs): strong at LOCAL exploration but weak at coordinated long-horizon strategy (e.g., saving a key for a distant door).

## Interpretation (flagged as inference)

- ICM and RND are the canonical intrinsic-motivation signals the rest of the lane builds on (WorldLLM's RL-LogP IS ICM-style prediction-error curiosity; Plan2Explore generalizes the idea to expected/future novelty). For the lane's thesis, they answer "how does an agent decide WHICH transitions to collect to improve its model?" The honest nuance: ICM's curiosity is reward-as-prediction-error, which directly DRIVES the agent toward where its model is wrong, but it is value-free about what those transitions COST.
- The noisy-TV problem and RND's fix are the technical core of the lane's safety bound. In a SOCIAL world the "noisy TV" is not a literal screen; it is any high-entropy social outcome (another actor's unpredictable response) that a naive prediction-error signal would chase by repeatedly poking that actor. RND's lesson (suppress aleatoric novelty) and ICM's lesson (only model controllable features) both translate into: do not reward the WAM-improvement loop for probing irreducibly unpredictable or socially costly outcomes.

## Mechanically useful vs research contribution

- **Mechanically useful**: ICM-style prediction-error and RND-style novelty are cheap, label-free signals for prioritizing which `(state, action)` to try when the goal is to improve a predictor. In the repo, a curiosity signal could steer the actor toward Action Cards whose verifier outcome the advisory WAM predicts poorly (high predicted-vs-verifier-observed mismatch), gathering exactly the transitions that would correct the WAM, at near-$0.
- **What to avoid / overclaim**: these are pixel/Atari RL methods that train a policy from scratch for millions of frames; the repo neither trains from pixels nor runs that many steps, and must not adopt a value-free curiosity objective in a social setting. The borrow is the SIGNAL (model-error-as-priority), constrained by the repo's material-claim and weak-commons vocabulary so exploration does not become anti-social probing.
