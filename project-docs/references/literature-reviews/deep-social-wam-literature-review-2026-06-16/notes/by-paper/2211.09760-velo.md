## Primary-source facts (LaTeX-verified)

- **Research question**: can a learned optimizer be scaled up enough to be VERSATILE (work across many neural-net training tasks) and need NO per-problem hyperparameter tuning? (abstract, intro)
- **Compute scale (the central cost fact)**: VeLO was "meta-trained with approximately four thousand TPU-months of compute on a wide variety of optimization tasks." Versus its own prior work, VeLO meta-trains on "three orders of magnitude more tasks" and "two orders of magnitude more compute" (abstract, intro Section 4).
- **Architecture**: a per-parameter MLP optimizer (extremely small: 2 hidden layers, 4 hidden units) operating on hand-selected fast features, with a per-tensor LSTM producing weights/context. Meta-trained with Evolution Strategies (ES) gradients rather than backprop-through-unrolls (Section 4.2, Section problem setting). Batched async meta-training with increasing batch size 10K-40K tasks.
- **Headline result (no tuning)**: "VeLO ... with no hyperparameters optimizes models dramatically faster than learning rate-tuned baselines, and usually surpasses the performance of NAdamW with one thousand trials of per-problem hyperparameter tuning." On the VeLOdrome 83-task eval set, VeLO outperforms all learning-rate-tuned optimizers on all problems, and on >85% of tasks also beats the 1000-trial NAdamW search with a single training run (abstract, Section 5).
- **Failure cases (authors' own, Section "Limitations and Failure Cases")**: they define failure broadly (not comparable to a tuned baseline). All failures "occur when VeLO is asked to optimize tasks which are very unlike tasks in its meta-training distribution":
  - **Large models (>500M params)**: performance lags/decreases as model size grows past ~500M. ViT-H/14 (650M) "notably underperformed." An 8B-param Transformer on 160B tokens of C4 was unstable and underperformed an untuned Adafactor baseline with 1/4 the batch, "even on a step-for-step basis." These sizes are "far out of the meta-training distribution," which contains few tasks even 5% that size (Section limitations).
  - **Long training (>200K steps)**: meta-trained on <=20K inner steps, then finetuned to <=200K; performs less well on longer runs (Section long-training-failure; also the GNN example: VeLO beat the baseline at 46K steps but underperforms on longer runs).
  - **A re-introduced hyperparameter**: a 100M Transformer on 20B tokens diverged from unbounded activation growth; the fix was adding weight decay 1e-6, "which unfortunately adds a tunable hyperparameter to VeLO" (Section 717).

## Interpretation (flagged as inference)

- VeLO is the strongest single piece of evidence in my lane for BOTH halves of the thesis. The "real" half: a learned optimizer, given enough scale, beats a 1000-trial tuned baseline with zero tuning on >85% of a broad task set. Self-improvement of the learning algorithm clearly works. The "expensive + bounded" half: it cost ~4000 TPU-months, and it fails precisely when asked to leave its meta-training distribution (bigger models, longer training), even re-introducing a hyperparameter to stay stable. The capability is trained-distribution-bound, not open-ended.
- This is the cleanest analog to the wave-4 thesis caveat: a meta-learned self-improvement component is a specialist for the distribution it was trained on. Push it out-of-distribution and it degrades or breaks. For the repo, that means a meta-learned WAM/recipe component would be valid only near the cycle/scenario distribution it was tuned on.

## Mechanically useful vs research contribution

- **Mechanically useful**: VeLO is reference context, not a borrow. The transferable lesson is the evaluation discipline: VeLO explicitly includes "an investigation of problems in which VeLO fails or underperforms baselines" and defines failure as "not comparable to a tuned baseline." Any self-improvement loop in the repo should report its own failure regime (which scenarios the improved WAM does NOT help), not just its wins.
- **Research contribution (not ours)**: training a versatile learned optimizer at TPU-month scale is a frontier-lab research program. The repo cannot and should not attempt it.
- **What to avoid / overclaim**: do not assume a meta-learned improvement generalizes to scenarios bigger/longer/unlike its training set. VeLO, the most-scaled learned optimizer to date, still breaks out of distribution. Also do not equate "beats tuned baseline on a benchmark" with "needs no compute": the no-tuning win was bought with 4000 TPU-months up front.

## WAM layer(s) informed

Method-level, cross-layer (reference context). The out-of-distribution failure pattern bounds any meta-learned Physical/Material WAM component and, more strongly, any attempt to meta-learn the contested Social/Institutional layers.
