# FeUdal Networks for Hierarchical Reinforcement Learning (Vezhnevets et al., 2017)

The deep-learning realization of the manager/worker (feudal) hierarchy: a Manager sets directional
goals in a learned latent at a slow time scale, a Worker follows them at the fast clock rate, trained
by a transition policy gradient. Source: LaTeX deep-read at `papers/latex/1703.01161/` (`00_abstract.tex`,
`01_intro.tex`, `02_model_02.tex`).

## Plain-language: what it introduced and why it mattered

FeUdal Networks (FuN) took the 1993 feudal RL idea (Dayan and Hinton: levels of an agent communicate
by explicit goals, the higher level says *what* and not *how*) and made it a single end-to-end deep
network. It has a Manager and a Worker:
- The **Manager** runs at a slower temporal resolution, computes its own latent state, and emits a
  *goal* that is a **direction in that latent space** (a normalized vector), not an absolute target.
- The **Worker** runs at every environment tick and produces primitive actions, modulated by the
  Manager's pooled recent goals; it gets an **intrinsic reward** for moving in the goal direction.
- Crucially, **no gradients flow between Manager and Worker**. The Manager learns only from
  environment reward; the Worker learns only to follow the Manager's directions.

Why it mattered: it showed a fully-differentiable two-level hierarchy could improve long-term credit
assignment and memory on hard sparse-reward games (the canonical example is Montezuma's Revenge),
using two ideas that later HRL kept reusing: (1) goals as *directions* in a learned latent, and (2) a
*transition policy gradient* that trains the Manager on the direction the agent actually moved, so the
Manager's goal carries semantic meaning rather than being an arbitrary vector.

## Definitions (first use)

- **Feudal reinforcement learning (FRL)**: Dayan and Hinton's 1993 proposal that an agent is a
  hierarchy of managers; each level sets a goal for the level below and is rewarded for the goal being
  achieved, decoupling goal-setting from goal-achievement.
- **Manager / Worker**: the two modules; Manager sets goals at low temporal resolution, Worker emits
  primitive actions at high temporal resolution.
- **Directional goal**: the Manager outputs a unit vector g_t in latent space; the goal is "move in
  this direction from the current latent state," not "reach this absolute coordinate."
- **Intrinsic reward**: a reward internal to the agent (here, cosine alignment between the latent state
  change and the Manager's goal direction) used to train the Worker, separate from environment reward.
- **Transition policy gradient**: the Manager's update rule; it treats the *observed latent-state
  transition direction* as the Manager's effective action, so the gradient pushes the Manager toward
  goal directions that led to reward. This is what gives the goals "semantic meaning."
- **Dilated LSTM**: the Manager's recurrent network design, which preserves memory across long time
  spans and lets gradients flow through large time hops (hundreds of steps).

## Primary-source facts

- **Architecture (`02_model_02.tex`)**: a shared perceptual module produces z_t from the observation;
  the Manager maps z_t to a latent state s_t and emits a normalized goal g_t; the Worker emits an
  action embedding matrix U_t; the last c goals are sum-pooled and linearly embedded into w_t (no
  bias, so the Manager's input can never be ignored), and the policy is softmax(U_t w_t).
- **Decoupling**: "no gradients are propagated between Worker and Manager; the Manager receives its
  learning signal from the environment alone" (`01_intro.tex`).
- **Four stated contributions (`01_intro.tex`)**: (1) an end-to-end differentiable model embodying
  feudal RL; (2) the approximate transition policy gradient for the Manager; (3) directional rather
  than absolute goals; (4) the dilated LSTM for long-horizon credit assignment.
- **Ablation result**: transition policy gradient and directional goals are both confirmed crucial for
  best performance.
- **Domains**: ATARI (including Montezuma's Revenge) and 3D DeepMind Lab memory tasks; FuN improves
  long-term credit assignment and memorization over a strong LSTM baseline (but see the caveat below).

## Interpretation (labeled inference)

- FuN is the *deep-RL bridge* between the 1993 feudal idea and modern hierarchical world models like
  Director (2206.04114): Director keeps the manager/worker shape and the latent-goal idea but replaces
  FuN's directional goals with discrete codes in a *world model's* latent and adds explicit
  temporally-abstract exploration. Director's own related-work explicitly notes FuN "did not
  demonstrate clear benefits over an LSTM baseline" and that both FuN levels maximized task reward with
  the low level only regularized by a goal reward, "calling into question the benefit of their
  high-level controller." So FuN is genealogically central but empirically contested, a good honest
  data point about how finicky HRL benefits are.
- For this repo, FuN's most transferable idea is conceptual: a higher level naming a *direction of
  desired change* in a typed state, evaluated against the direction the state actually moved. That is
  almost exactly the advisory-WAM evaluation pattern (predict a delta, score against the verified
  delta), minus FuN's RL training and minus any control authority.

## Mechanically useful vs research contribution

- **Mechanically useful**: the manager/worker vocabulary; the notion of a goal as a *direction of
  change in a typed latent* rather than an absolute target (maps cleanly to the repo's predicted
  social-material *deltas*); the transition-policy-gradient insight that a goal is meaningful only when
  scored against the transition that actually occurred (the repo's predicted-vs-verified-delta scoring).
- **Research contribution (NOT to adopt)**: the end-to-end RL training of a Manager policy and the
  dilated-LSTM credit-assignment machinery are research artifacts for *acting* agents; the repo's WAM
  is advisory and the LLM does the proposing, so these are cited as lineage, not adopted.

## Relevance to this repo (one line)

The deep-RL root of the manager/worker hierarchy and of "a goal is a direction of change scored against
the transition that happened," which is the conceptual shape of advisory delta-prediction, not a control
loop to adopt.
