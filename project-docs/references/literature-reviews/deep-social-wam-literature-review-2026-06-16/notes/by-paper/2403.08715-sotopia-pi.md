## Primary-source facts (what the paper states/shows)

- **Research question**: can a 7B model be trained to social competence by interactive self-improvement, without human-authored interaction data, and where does that loop break?
- **The mechanism (social-task synthesis + self-improvement, the new angle)**: a 3-step loop.
  - **Step 1, automatic social-task synthesis (the freshness-relevant part)**: sample social-activity keywords from three norm resources (Social Chemistry, Social IQa, NormBank), then prompt GPT-4 to generate a scenario plus two per-character social goals from each keyword. Reuse the 40 SOTOPIA character profiles, randomly pairing two per task. Generation is FULLY AUTOMATED and unfiltered (volume over the human-in-the-loop filtering SOTOPIA used), and training tasks are guaranteed disjoint from evaluation tasks.
  - **Step 2, interaction collection**: multi-turn interactions between two GPT-4 expert agents (for behavior cloning) or between two copies of the current policy (for self-reinforcement).
  - **Step 3, policy update**: GPT-4 rates each agent's goal completion (the SOTOPIA-EVAL 0 to 10 dimension that correlates best with humans), filter by a goal-completion threshold, parse to input/output JSON, fine-tune Mistral-7B with QLoRA. Full method is behavior cloning then self-reinforcement (BC+SR). Evaluation is GPT-4-based plus human, against a fixed GPT-3.5-turbo partner on 90 tasks and the 14 hard tasks. The "verifier" here is an LLM judge, NOT a deterministic runtime.
- **Numbers / results as stated** (quoted by sub-agent):
  - Throughput: "GPT-4 to generate 100 social tasks ... per round"; per task "10 social interactions with 10 different character pairs"; appendix "462 new social tasks upfront" from "510 unused inspirational prompts, 170 from each source."
  - Goal completion, hard tasks, GPT-4-rated (headline): BC+SR 5.71 vs GPT-4 5.89, "nearly matching the goal completion performance of GPT-4 itself."
  - Goal completion, all 90 tasks, GPT-4-rated: Expert GPT-4 7.62, Base Mistral-7B 5.07, SR 5.83, BC 7.27, BC+SR 7.62 (ties expert).
  - The EVALUATOR-GAP finding (the load-bearing caveat): GPT-4 evaluation "significantly overestimates" trained models; on HUMAN ratings, BC+SR goal completion is 4.29 vs the GPT-4 agent's 5.25, i.e. on humans BC+SR does NOT match GPT-4.
  - Safety / over-cooperation: role-play of a harmful goal (injure a third person): base Mistral injures 100% of the time with 3.6 toxic words; BC+SR drops to 44% injury (matching GPT-4) with 0.9 toxic words; engagement rises 22% -> 100%. But BC+SR "produces more toxic words than GPT-4," and SR-ONLY actually RAISED toxicity (5.5) and kept injury at 100%, i.e. naive self-reinforcement can amplify harmful compliance.
  - Capability retention: MMLU 5-shot base 49.21 vs BC+SR 48.57 (no catastrophic forgetting).
- **Reproducibility status**: SELF-REPORTED, with code/data/checkpoints released. The headline scores depend on a GPT-4 LLM judge that the authors themselves show is biased (the GPT-4 vs human gap), so "matches GPT-4" is not environment-verified by a deterministic checker.
- **Stated limitations**: (1) using an LLM as evaluator: the growing GPT-4-vs-human gap shows LLM-judge bias when optimizing against that same judge. (2) safety studied only on one dimension. (3) the SOTOPIA environment is itself GPT-4-powered, so synthesized tasks may carry GPT-4 social biases into the trained agent.

## Interpretation (flagged as inference)

- SOTOPIA-pi is the only source in this lane that actually MANUFACTURES new social content cheaply (100 tasks/round, 462 upfront), which is precisely what the repo needs because social scenarios do not auto-regenerate like coding contests. Its Step-1 synthesis recipe (sample norm keywords -> GPT-4 scenario + per-character goals -> guarantee train/eval disjointness) is portable as a held-out social-scenario generator.
- But SOTOPIA-pi scores with a GPT-4 judge and PROVES that judge is biased toward models trained against it (GPT-4-rated 5.71 vs 5.89, human-rated only 4.29 vs 5.25). The lesson for the repo is to take the synthesis loop and REPLACE its LLM judge with the deterministic Minecraft runtime verifier, or the self-improvement loop overfits to the judge, not to social competence. This is the same progress-laundering risk the repo's rules forbid.
- The over-cooperation finding (SR-only kept injury at 100% and raised toxicity) is a direct, measured warning for the repo's self-reinforcement loop: reward goal completion without a hard verifier and the actor learns harmful compliance. It is concrete evidence that an LLM-judged social loop can move the wrong direction while the judged metric improves.

## Mechanically useful vs research contribution

- **Mechanically useful**: (1) the social-task synthesis recipe (norm-keyword seeds -> LLM scenario + per-character goals -> train/eval disjointness) as a cheap held-out social-scenario generator; (2) the explicit GPT-4-judge-vs-human gap as evidence to swap the LLM judge for the deterministic verifier; (3) the over-cooperation / toxicity-amplification result as a named failure of self-reinforcement under an LLM judge, motivating verifier-gated rewards; (4) the train/eval disjointness guarantee as a baseline freshness discipline.
- **Not a contribution to copy / overclaim risk**: SOTOPIA-pi's "7B matches GPT-4" rests on a biased LLM judge the authors themselves debunk on humans; the repo must not import the LLM-judge scoring. The defensible take is the synthesis engine plus the cautionary evaluator-gap and over-cooperation findings; the contribution would be running such synthesis but labeling outcomes with the runtime verifier.

## WAM layer(s) informed

Social (it generates social tasks and goals) and the EVALUATION-freshness question. Its scoring is LLM-judged, which is exactly the contested-success regime the contract flags; the repo's improvement is to keep the synthesis and ground the label in the deterministic verifier.
