## Primary-source facts (what the paper states/shows)

- **Research question**: massive data and high-capacity models drove vision/NLP; can a platform that procedurally generates large, diverse, interactive 3D houses do the same for Embodied AI, removing the bottleneck of hand-authored scenes?
- **The mechanism (procedural generation pipeline)**: built on AI2-THOR (inherits assets, physics, robotic agents). Input is a manually authored room-spec tree (e.g. "1 bedroom + 1 bathroom"). Multi-stage conditional sampling then: generates a floor plan (iterative boundary cutting plus the Lopes et al. 2010 recursive layout subdivision), creates external walls, samples lighting and doors, then places large/small/wall objects, colors, and textures. Room connectivity is constrained by the spec tree (a path connects every child of a parent; door counts are limited). Doors between adjacent rooms are sampled by type with annotated probabilities (kitchen-living-room: 0.375 open, 0.375 door frame, 0.25 doorway). Object placement uses soft annotations (room assignments, "fridge along a wall, TV not on floor") and Semantic Asset Groups (SAGs, co-occurring sets like "dining table with 4 chairs") via dependent sampling, placed iteratively "ensuring that rooms continue to have adequate floor space for agents to navigate." Scenes serialize to JSON and load into AI2-THOR.
- **Validity / solvability gate (explicit, the load-bearing tie)**: after generation, "we use a validator to make sure that the agent can successfully navigate to each room in the house, without modifying the scene." It teleports the agent in, runs BFS over a 0.25 x 0.25 m grid for all reachable positions, and checks every room has at least 5 reachable grid cells; "If the validator fails, we resample a new house using the same room spec." Result: "All houses are fully navigable." A separate reachability/visibility raycast decides valid ObjectNav target objects. (PySAT is acknowledged but the described gate is BFS reachability, not a SAT solver.)
- **Numbers / results as stated** (quoted by sub-agent from the tex):
  - ProcTHOR-10K = "a sample of 10,000 generated houses"; plus "1,000 validation and 1,000 testing houses."
  - Data release statement: "the initial static dataset of 1 million procedurally generated house JSON files."
  - Asset/material library: "database of 1633 household assets across 108 categories"; "40 solid colors or one of 122 wall textures"; "55 floor materials"; "18 different Semantic Asset groups"; "19.3 million combinations of group instantiations."
  - Scale framing: "16 different scene specifications ... can result in over 100 billion layouts."
  - Generation cost: "10K houses was generated in 1 hour on a local workstation with 4 NVIDIA RTX A5000 GPUs." Houses span 1 to 10 rooms.
  - SOTA (zero-shot rows): RoboTHOR ObjectNav zero-shot Success 55.0% / SPL 0.237 (baseline EmbCLIP 47.0% / 0.200), fine-tune 65.2% / 0.288; AI2-iTHOR zero-shot 75.7% / 0.644 (baseline 68.4% / 0.516); Habitat 2022 HM3D-Semantics ProcTHOR+Large+fine-tune 54.4% / 0.318; ArmPointNav zero-shot 37.9% / 74.8. "ranked first on three public leaderboards as of 10am PT, June 14th 2022."
  - Scale ablation (train on 10/100/1,000/10,000 houses, each to 80% train success): RoboTHOR 0-shot Success 8.53% -> 20.9% -> 33.1% -> 44.5%; AI2-iTHOR 28.7% -> 42.0% -> 53.0% -> 64.9%.
- **Reproducibility status**: ENVIRONMENT-VERIFIED. Results come from inside AI2-THOR with a runtime navigability validator, across 6 benchmarks and 3 live leaderboards. Code/dataset stated as future open-source release (Apache 2.0) at submission time; the simulator is the verifier.
- **Stated limitation**: "ProcTHOR-10K only uses 1-floor houses. We plan to support multi-floor houses in ProcTHOR-v2.0." Asset databases to be scaled via external 3D sources.

## Interpretation (flagged as inference)

- ProcTHOR is the strongest structural analog in this lane for COMPARABLE SEEDED SCENARIOS plus a runtime validity gate. The room-spec tree plus seeded conditional sampling is a parameterized, reproducible scenario generator: fix the spec and RNG and you get a resettable scene serialized to JSON, the direct counterpart to seeding a Minecraft world/ledger and replaying it.
- Its BFS-reachability validator with reject-and-resample (>=5 reachable cells per room, else resample under the same spec) is a true deterministic verifier on generated state, the closest published mirror of the repo's transition verifier. But it certifies only the Physical layer (navigability/collision); it has no Material-Economic, Social, or Institutional admissibility notion.
- To reach the repo's 4 layers, the lesson is to stack analogous reject-and-resample gates per layer (economic feasibility, role/relationship consistency, institutional-rule satisfaction) on top of the physical one ProcTHOR demonstrates. ProcTHOR proves the pattern (generate, verify, resample-on-fail under a fixed seed-spec) works at industrial scale and near-zero per-scene cost.

## Mechanically useful vs research contribution

- **Mechanically useful**: (1) the seed-spec + conditional-sampling recipe for resettable, comparable scenarios; (2) reject-and-resample-under-the-same-spec as the admission loop that pairs a generator with a deterministic gate; (3) the per-room reachability criterion as a template for typed per-layer admissibility checks; (4) the demonstrated cheapness (10K verified scenes in 1 hour) as evidence the generate-verify loop is affordable.
- **Not a contribution to copy / overclaim risk**: "ProcTHOR for Minecraft social scenarios" is a method import (procedural house generation), not a contribution. ProcTHOR's validity is purely physical navigability, so it cannot certify social/institutional admissibility; the repo should borrow the seed-and-resample-against-a-deterministic-gate pattern, not claim a procedural social-scene generator as a result.

## WAM layer(s) informed

Method-level, Physical (the validator is navigability/reachability). The seed-spec generation idea generalizes across layers, but the demonstrated verifier is physical-only; Material/Social/Institutional gates are the repo's to add.
