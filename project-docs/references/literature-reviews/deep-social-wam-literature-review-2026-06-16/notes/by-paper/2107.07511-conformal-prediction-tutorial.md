## Primary-source facts

- Object: conformal prediction wraps ANY pre-trained model fhat and a small held-out CALIBRATION set
  to emit a PREDICTION SET C(X) (a set of candidate labels, or an interval) instead of a single
  guess. The set is valid in a DISTRIBUTION-FREE sense: a guarantee that holds with no assumption on
  the data distribution or on the model being any good.
- The marginal coverage guarantee (the central theorem, Vovk-Gammerman-Saunders): for a
  user-chosen miss rate alpha,
  1 - alpha <= P(Y_test in C(X_test)) <= 1 - alpha + 1/(n+1),
  where n is the calibration-set size. So with alpha = 0.1 the set contains the true label with
  probability ~90 percent. The probability is MARGINAL: averaged over the randomness in calibration
  and test points, not conditional on a given X.
- The recipe (3 steps): (1) define a score function s(x,y) where larger = worse agreement between x
  and y; (2) compute qhat = the ceil((n+1)(1-alpha))/n empirical quantile of the calibration scores
  s_i = s(X_i, Y_i); (3) form C(x) = { y : s(x,y) <= qhat } for a new x. Validity holds for ANY score
  function and ANY distribution.
- The key caveat, verbatim: "although the guarantee always holds, the usefulness of the prediction
  sets is primarily determined by the score function." If the score is random noise the set is a
  random subset of labels large enough to still cover at the nominal rate; the guarantee is on
  coverage, the INFORMATIVENESS (set size) is on you.
- The standing assumption: EXCHANGEABILITY (a weakening of i.i.d.) of calibration and test points.
  When that breaks (distribution shift / drift), bare coverage breaks too.
- Distribution drift extension (load-bearing for a sequential predictor): use WEIGHTED conformal,
  discounting old calibration scores (e.g. rolling window w_i = 1[i >= n-K], or decay
  w_i = 0.99^(n-i+1)). Theorem (Barber et al. 2022): with eps_i = total-variation distance between
  the i-th data point and the test point, weighted conformal satisfies
  P(Y_test in C(X_test)) >= 1 - alpha - 2 * sum_i (wtilde_i * eps_i).
  Coverage degrades by twice the weighted average shift. The eps_i "are never known exactly in
  advance"; the discount is a heuristic.
- Selective prediction (abstention) is covered directly. A SELECTIVE ACCURACY guarantee
  P(Y_test = Yhat(X_test) | Phat(X_test) >= lambdahat) >= 1 - alpha makes the model say
  "I do not know" below a confidence threshold lambdahat and guarantees the requested accuracy on the
  predictions it does emit. Because accuracy is NOT monotone in the cutoff, the threshold is chosen by
  the Learn-then-Test framework: scan lambda, upper-bound the selective misclassification rate with a
  Binomial CDF bound Rhat+(lambda) at failure rate delta, and pick the smallest lambda whose upper
  bound stays under alpha. Guarantee holds with probability 1 - delta under i.i.d.
- Extensions cataloged (abstract/section level, not deep-read): class-conditional and group-balanced
  coverage; conformal risk control (control a general monotone loss, not just miscoverage);
  conformal prediction under covariate shift; outlier detection; full (transductive) conformal and
  Jackknife+/CV+; the conformalized-quantile-regression and APS score functions.

## Interpretation (my inference, labeled)

- This is the distribution-free counterpart to [[1706.04599-calibration-modern-neural-networks]].
  Calibration (ECE) tells you whether a stated probability is honest on average; conformal prediction
  gives a hard, finite-sample COVERAGE guarantee on a set/interval with no distributional assumption.
  For an advisory social-material WAM scored on held-out scenarios, conformal lets the predictor emit
  a SET of plausible next deltas (e.g. {returns pickaxe, keeps pickaxe}) with a guaranteed
  ~(1-alpha) chance of containing the verifier-observed delta, computed purely from a held-out
  calibration set of verifier-labeled transitions. Because the verifier auto-labels (state, action,
  next-state) at ~$0, the calibration set the method needs is exactly the cheap thing the repo owns.
- The drift theorem is the single most important caveat for THIS repo. Social/settlement state is a
  SEQUENTIAL, non-exchangeable process (trust accrues, norms form, a settlement matures), so the
  i.i.d./exchangeability assumption is the first thing that breaks. Weighted conformal with a decay or
  rolling window is the principled response, and the 2*sum(w_i*eps_i) slack is the honest statement
  of how much coverage you lose. Reporting conformal coverage on a multi-cycle social run WITHOUT
  acknowledging non-exchangeability would be an overclaim.
- Selective prediction is the formal home of the SPEC's "advisory" stance and the repo's
  "I do not know" posture: an advisory predictor that abstains below threshold can hold a target
  accuracy on what it does emit, and abstention is a first-class, guaranteed behavior rather than an
  ad-hoc dropout. This connects to [[2107.11277-machine-learning-reject-option]].

## Mechanically useful vs research contribution

- Mechanically useful:
  - The 3-step split-conformal recipe is directly runnable: pick a score function over typed deltas,
    calibrate on held-out verifier-labeled transitions, emit prediction sets with guaranteed coverage.
  - The selective-prediction (Learn-then-Test) threshold procedure is a ready abstention controller
    for an advisory predictor.
  - Weighted conformal (decay / rolling window) is the off-the-shelf fix for the sequential,
    non-exchangeable social setting, with an explicit coverage-loss bound to report.
- Research contribution (not the repo's to claim): conformal prediction is established theory; the
  tutorial does not invent it. The repo's contribution would be the FIRST application of
  distribution-free coverage to verifier-grounded, structured social-material deltas, and an honest
  treatment of the non-exchangeability that a maturing settlement induces. That gap is the surface,
  not a citable result.

## WAM layer(s) informed

- Cross-cutting (measurement / uncertainty quantification). Most pointed at the Social and
  Institutional layers, where the horizon is long and the process is non-exchangeable, so the drift
  theorem and weighted conformal matter most. Physical/Material deltas (more nearly exchangeable
  per-step) are the easy case where plain split conformal applies. Companions:
