# DreamerV2: Mastering Atari with Discrete World Models (Hafner et al., 2020)

The first world-model agent to reach human-level Atari, by switching the latent from
Gaussian to discrete (categorical) variables. Source: LaTeX deep-read at
`papers/latex/2010.02193/` (sections/intro.tex, method.tex).

## Plain-language: what it introduced and why it mattered

Through DreamerV1, world-model agents worked on continuous control but had never matched
the best model-free agents on the competitive Atari benchmark, where pixel inputs are
discrete and gameplay is more varied. DreamerV2 closed that gap with a small but important
change: it **replaced the continuous (Gaussian) latent state with a vector of discrete
(categorical) variables**, trained with straight-through gradients, plus a stabilizing
trick called KL balancing.

- **Discrete / categorical latent**: instead of representing the compressed state as
  continuous numbers from a Gaussian, represent it as a set of categorical ("one of K
  choices") variables. The authors argue categorical latents fit the often discrete,
  multi-modal structure of game frames better and are easier to predict.
- **Straight-through gradients**: you cannot backpropagate through a hard discrete sample
  directly (sampling is not differentiable). The straight-through estimator passes gradients
  as if the sample were the underlying probabilities, so you keep a discrete forward pass but
  a differentiable backward pass.
- **KL balancing**: the world-model loss has a KL term that both (a) trains the prior
  (transition predictor) toward the encoder's representations and (b) regularizes the
  representations toward the prior. KL balancing uses different learning rates for the two
  directions so the model learns an accurate prior without being dragged toward a
  poorly-trained one.

Why it mattered: it was **the first RL agent to reach human-level performance on the
55-game Atari benchmark purely by learning behaviors inside a separately learned world
model**, on a single GPU with a single environment instance. That a policy trained entirely
in imagination could match model-free agents (Rainbow, IQN) built on years of tuning was the
proof that the latent world model was capturing the environment accurately enough to learn
in. The categorical latent then carried forward into V3 and Dreamer 4.

## Primary-source facts

- **World model**: PlaNet/Dreamer RSSM, but with the stochastic latent `z_t` changed from a
  diagonal Gaussian to a vector of categorical variables (optimized with straight-through
  gradients). Components: recurrent model `h_t = f(h_{t-1}, z_{t-1}, a_{t-1})`,
  representation model `z_t ~ q(z_t | h_t, x_t)` (posterior, sees the image), transition
  predictor `z_hat_t ~ p(z_hat_t | h_t)` (prior, does not see the next image), plus image,
  reward, and discount predictors. The transition predictor not needing the next image is
  what lets behavior learning run in pure latent imagination.
- **Loss**: image + reward + discount log-likelihoods + KL term (interpreted as a
  sequential-VAE ELBO / variational free energy), with KL balancing (prior learning rate
  alpha=0.8). 20M trainable parameters.
- **Behavior learning**: actor-critic in imagination (inherited from V1), imagination
  horizon H=15, "the world model is fixed during behavior learning," ~2500 latent
  trajectories simulated in parallel on a single GPU (no images decoded during imagination).
- **Result**: first agent to reach human-level mean performance on the Atari 55-game
  benchmark by learning purely within the world model; outperforms single-GPU model-free
  agents Rainbow and IQN. The paper also argues for reporting both human-gamer-normalized and
  human-world-record-normalized scores.
- **Compute contrast (intro)**: explicitly contrasts MuZero (strong on board games and
  deterministic Atari but with huge compute, "over 2 months ... on a GPU," and no public
  implementation at the time) to position DreamerV2 as the practical single-GPU option.

## Interpretation (labeled inference)

- DreamerV2 is the "what fixed the prior step" node: PlaNet/Dreamer's Gaussian latent was the
  limiter on the discrete, multi-modal Atari domain; categorical latents + straight-through
  gradients + KL balancing removed it. This is a clean teaching example of how a
  representation choice (continuous vs discrete latent) gates which domains a world model can
  master.
- The MuZero contrast (line in intro) is genealogically useful: MuZero and DreamerV2 reached
  Atari competence at nearly the same time from opposite directions, MuZero by value-equivalent
  planning with MCTS at large compute, DreamerV2 by reconstruction-based latent imagination on
  a single GPU. The genealogy is not a single line; it is two converging branches (see the
  theme file).

## Mechanically useful vs research contribution

- **Mechanically useful**: the categorical-latent + straight-through-gradient pattern is the
  standard modern way to represent a discrete structured latent; directly relevant if a
  structured-state predictor ever needs differentiable discrete state. KL balancing is a
  reusable stabilization trick for any sequential-VAE-style predictor.
- **Research contribution (not this repo's)**: reaching a benchmark by imagination-trained RL
  is a research result, not an engineering borrow. The repo borrows representation lessons,
  not the imagination-trained policy.

## Relevance to this repo (one line)

Shows a discrete/categorical latent unlocks discrete, multi-modal domains; the
representation lesson (and KL-balancing stabilization) transfers, the imagination-RL policy
does not.
