## What it introduced and why it mattered (plain language)

IRIS (Imagination with auto-Regression over an Inner Speech) is the canonical
"token-based world model." Its idea: treat learning the dynamics of an environment
exactly like a language model treats text. First compress each game frame into a
small set of discrete tokens (a learned visual vocabulary), then train a GPT-style
transformer to predict the next tokens given past frame-tokens and actions. The
agent then learns its policy entirely "in imagination" (inside the world model's
rollouts), touching the real game only to collect data. This mattered because it
showed a plain autoregressive transformer over discrete tokens is extremely
sample-efficient: superhuman on 10 of 26 Atari games from only ~2 hours of play.

Newcomer terms (defined once):
- World model: a learned model `p(o' | o, a)` that predicts the next observation
  given the current one and an action. A learned simulator.
- Learning in imagination: training a policy on trajectories generated by the world
  model instead of the real environment, so data cost drops.
- Discrete autoencoder / VQ-VAE: an encoder that maps an image to a fixed number of
  tokens drawn from a finite codebook, plus a decoder that maps tokens back to pixels.
- Autoregressive prediction: generating the next token conditioned on all previous
  tokens, one at a time.

## Primary-source facts

- Architecture: two parts. (1) a discrete autoencoder (VQ-VAE-style) `E: image ->
  K tokens from a vocabulary of size N`, with decoder `D`; trained with L1
  reconstruction + commitment loss + perceptual loss, straight-through estimator.
  (2) a GPT-like autoregressive transformer `G` over an interleaved sequence
  `(z_0^1..z_0^K, a_0, z_1^1..z_1^K, a_1, ...)`. `G` predicts three things per step:
  transition (next-frame tokens, autoregressive at the token level), reward, and
  termination.
- Training objective: self-supervised next-token cross-entropy for transition and
  termination; MSE or cross-entropy for reward. The policy + value are trained with
  an actor-critic in imagination, reusing DreamerV2's objectives/hyperparameters.
- Observation/state representation: pixels, encoded to discrete tokens. Action space:
  discrete Atari actions.
- Evaluation protocol + metrics: Atari 100k benchmark (26 games, 100k env actions ~=
  2 hours human play). Mean human-normalized score (HNS) 1.046; superhuman on 10/26;
  new state of the art among methods without lookahead search.
- Stated limitations (theirs): the policy learns from reconstructed frames rather than
  the world model's internal representations; combining with MCTS is left as future
  work; scaling to harder tasks left open.

## Interpretation (inference, labeled)

- IRIS is the reference point for the "discretize-then-transformer" branch of world
  models. DIAMOND (2405.12399) was written largely as a rebuttal to it: IRIS's
  discretization can throw away small but decision-relevant visual details, which is
  exactly what DIAMOND's diffusion approach tries to keep. So IRIS is best understood
  as one pole of the "what should a world model predict, and in what representation?"
  axis, not a dead end.
- The interleaved frame-token / action-token sequence is the direct technical ancestor
  of MineWorld's tokenized Minecraft WM (already covered in
  `minecraft-world-models.md`) and of GAIA-1's tokenized driving WM (2309.17080).

## Mechanically useful vs research contribution

- Mechanically useful for this repo: the "interleave typed state-tokens with
  action-tokens and predict the next state with a sequence model" template is a clean
  mental model for a structured-state social WAM, where the tokens would be typed
  social-material facts rather than VQ image codes. The reward/termination heads are a
  reminder that a WAM can also predict scalar consequence signals (e.g. a social-cost
  scalar) alongside the next-state.
- Avoid / would be overclaim: IRIS is a pixel WM that selects actions only via a
  separate actor-critic trained in imagination. Importing "RL-in-imagination as the
  controller" conflicts with the repo rule that the LLM proposes and the runtime owns
  truth and a WAM stays advisory. IRIS is an architecture analogy, not a control
  recipe to adopt.
