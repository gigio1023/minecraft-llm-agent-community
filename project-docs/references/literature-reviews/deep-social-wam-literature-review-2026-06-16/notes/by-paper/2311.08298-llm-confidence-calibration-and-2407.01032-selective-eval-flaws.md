# LLM Confidence/Calibration Survey (2311.08298) and Selective-Classification Evaluation Flaws (2407.01032)

Two breadth sources for lane 30, read at abstract level (verified via hf papers info, not LaTeX
deep-read). Logged together because each fills one specific gap for the repo's predictor-evaluation
protocol.

## A. A Survey of Confidence Estimation and Calibration in Large Language Models

- title: A Survey of Confidence Estimation and Calibration in Large Language Models
- authors: Jiahui Geng, Fengyu Cai, Yuxia Wang, Heinz Koeppl, Preslav Nakov, Iryna Gurevych
  (MBZUAI / TU Darmstadt)
- venue/year: NAACL 2024 (arXiv 2311.08298, Nov 2023)
- arxiv_id: 2311.08298
- source read: abstract (hf papers info). reproducibility status: n/a (survey).
- Primary-source facts (abstract): LLMs "can be unreliable due to factual errors in their
  generations." Assessing their CONFIDENCE and CALIBRATING them across tasks can mitigate risk and
  improve generations. The survey organizes recent work on confidence estimation and calibration for
  LLMs, which "has been no comprehensive overview" before, and outlines the main lessons.
- Why it matters for the repo: the repo's advisory predictor is, in practice, an LLM emitting a
  predicted typed delta and (optionally) a stated confidence. The classic calibration result
  [[1706.04599-calibration-modern-neural-networks]] is about softmax classifiers; this survey is the
  bridge to the LLM-specific case, where confidence may be a verbalized number ("I am 80 percent
  sure"), a token-probability, or a sampled-agreement estimate, each with its own miscalibration
  pattern. It signals that the repo must measure calibration of WHATEVER confidence channel its
  predictor uses, and not assume a verbalized confidence is honest.

## B. Overcoming Common Flaws in the Evaluation of Selective Classification Systems

- title: Overcoming Common Flaws in the Evaluation of Selective Classification Systems
- authors: Jeremias Traub, Till J. Bungert, Carsten T. Lueth, Michael Baumgartner,
  Klaus H. Maier-Hein, Lena Maier-Hein (German Cancer Research Center / Heidelberg)
- venue/year: 2024 (arXiv 2407.01032, Jul 2024)
- arxiv_id: 2407.01032
- source read: abstract (hf papers info). reproducibility status: claim-only here (abstract-level;
  methods not re-run).
- Primary-source facts (abstract): selective classification (a model that can REJECT low-confidence
  predictions) is usually evaluated "at fixed working points based on pre-defined rejection
  thresholds." The paper argues methodological progress needs a THRESHOLD-INDEPENDENT metric, "akin
  to the AUROC in standard classification," and defines 5 requirements for multi-threshold metrics
  (task alignment, interpretability, and others) to benchmark the general performance of selective
  systems rather than a single operating point.
- Why it matters for the repo: it is the direct evaluation-methodology source for the abstention angle
  [[2107.11277-machine-learning-reject-option]]. It warns the repo NOT to report an advisory
  predictor's accuracy at one hand-picked abstention threshold (which can flatter or sandbag the
  system), but to report a threshold-independent curve (accuracy vs coverage, the analogue of an ROC)
  so the predictor's selective behavior is judged across all operating points. This is the abstention
  twin of rliable's "do not report a single point estimate."

## Combined relevance to this repo

- Together these two say: (1) measure calibration of the predictor's actual confidence channel
  (LLM-specific, not just softmax), and (2) when the predictor abstains, evaluate it across thresholds,
  not at one cherry-picked cutoff. Both are reporting-discipline items for the lane-30 protocol, not
  new science the repo would claim. They sit under the same overclaim-boundary umbrella as
  notes/by-theme/benchmark-validity-and-evaluation.md.

## WAM layer(s) informed

- Cross-cutting (measurement). Calibration-of-LLM-confidence and threshold-independent selective
  evaluation apply to any layer where an LLM predictor emits a confidence and may abstain.
