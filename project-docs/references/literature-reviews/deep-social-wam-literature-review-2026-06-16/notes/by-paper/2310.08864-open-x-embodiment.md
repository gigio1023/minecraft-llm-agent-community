# Open X-Embodiment: Robotic Learning Datasets and RT-X Models

Lane 11 depth-extra by-paper note. Source-backed. This is the cross-embodiment dataset
that made generalist VLAs trainable; F3 owns RT-1/RT-2 notes, this note owns the dataset.

- **title**: Open X-Embodiment: Robotic Learning Datasets and RT-X Models
- **authors**: Open X-Embodiment Collaboration (21 institutions; lead coordination Google
  DeepMind, UC Berkeley, Stanford, and many others; hundreds of authors)
- **year**: 2023 (arXiv 2310.08864); ICRA 2024
- **venue/source**: ICRA 2024 / arXiv
- **arxiv_id**: 2310.08864
- **urls**: LaTeX deep-read at `papers/latex/2310.08864/` (root.tex); project

- **source availability**: latex (full)

## Primary-source facts

- **Research question**: can robotics get a "consolidation of pretrained models" like
  NLP/CV, i.e. one generalist X-robot policy adaptable to new robots/tasks/environments,
  instead of one model per application/robot/environment?
- **Dataset shape**: assembled from **60 datasets** across **21 institutions**, **22
  robot embodiments**, demonstrating **527 skills** over **160,266 tasks**; the pooled
  corpus exceeds **1 million real robot trajectories**. Released in a single standardized
  data format (RLDS) so heterogeneous robot logs become co-trainable.
- **Row shape**: each datum is a `(observation, action)` step within a teleoperated
  demonstration trajectory; observations are camera images (+ proprioception where
  available); actions are the source robot's native end-effector / joint commands,
  harmonized into a common action representation for cross-embodiment training.
- **Models**: RT-X = RT-1 and RT-2 architectures retrained on the pooled OXE data.
  Empirical result: **positive cross-embodiment transfer** - "a high-capacity model
  trained on this data ... improves the capabilities of multiple robots by leveraging
  experience from other platforms," and model capacity matters more in the data-rich
  regime. (9 manipulators used in the headline training mix.)
- **Action / observation representation**: pixels-in, motor-actions-out; entirely robot
  manipulation. No symbolic/structured social or material state anywhere.
- **Availability**: dataset, data loaders, and RT-1-X checkpoints released; RT-2-X
  weights not fully open (RT-2 base is closed).

## Interpretation (clearly labeled)

- OXE is the *data substrate* of the whole VLA canon: OpenVLA trains on a 970K-trajectory
  OXE subset; Octo on 25 OXE datasets; pi_0 on "OXE Magic Soup." The distinction-relevant
  point is what kind of data a VLA needs: **paired `(o, a)` teleoperation demonstrations**,
  expensive to collect and modality-poor. This is the data axis on which a WAM differs: a
  WAM can additionally ingest **action-free video** `(o, o')`, which is cheaper and more
  abundant (the WAM survey's "unified data digestion"; DreamZero's 12-20 min video-only
  cross-embodiment transfer).
- OXE is the concrete reason the VLA survey can say robot data is "1/200,000" of LLM
  corpus scale even after this massive pooling effort: 1M trajectories is still tiny next
  to web text.

## Mechanically useful vs research contribution

- **Useful**: the standardized-format idea (harmonize heterogeneous logs into one schema
  so they are jointly usable) is a transferable data-engineering lesson; a structured
  social-material transition dataset would similarly need one typed schema across actors.
- **Avoid / overclaim**: OXE is pixel/motor manipulation data with no social, material,
  or possession semantics; it is not a source for the social/material WAM layers and
  must not be cited as evidence about them. For this repo it is a contrast point (what
  data a VLA needs) and a feasibility caution (data scale is the field's hard limit).
