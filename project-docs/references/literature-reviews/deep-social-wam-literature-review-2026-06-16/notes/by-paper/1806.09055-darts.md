# DARTS: Differentiable Architecture Search

- **title**: DARTS: Differentiable Architecture Search
- **authors**: Hanxiao Liu (CMU, then Google Brain), Karen Simonyan (DeepMind), Yiming Yang (CMU)
- **year**: 2018 (arXiv 2018-06; ICLR 2019)
- **venue/source**: ICLR 2019
- **arxiv_id**: 1806.09055
- **urls**: paper https://arxiv.org/abs/1806.09055 ; code https://github.com/quark0/darts
- **source availability**: latex (tarball extracted, papers/latex/1806.09055/main.tex; intro + method + experiments read directly)

## Primary-source facts (LaTeX-verified)

- **Research question**: NAS is computationally demanding because it treats architecture search as black-box optimization over a discrete, non-differentiable domain (RL, evolution, MCTS, SMBO, Bayesian optimization), requiring "a large number of architecture evaluations." Can the search be made differentiable so gradient descent can do it cheaply? (intro)
- **The cost numbers DARTS cites (the central cost narrative for this lane)**: "obtaining a state-of-the-art architecture for CIFAR-10 and ImageNet required 2000 GPU days of reinforcement learning (RL) [NASNet/Zoph 2017] or 3150 GPU days of evolution [AmoebaNet/Real 2018]." DARTS reduces "the cost of architecture discovery to a few GPU days" (intro, contributions).
- **Method**: search for a computation CELL (a DAG of N nodes; each edge carries an operation) that is stacked (conv) or recursively connected (recurrent) into the final network. Continuous relaxation: replace the categorical choice of operation on edge (i,j) with a softmax over all candidate ops weighted by a learned vector alpha. Architecture search then becomes learning the continuous alpha. At the end, take argmax over alpha to get a discrete architecture (Section 2, eq. softmax).
- **Bilevel optimization**: find alpha* minimizing the VALIDATION loss, where the weights w*(alpha) minimize the TRAINING loss. Solved with an approximation that interleaves a weight step and an architecture step. First-order approximation (xi = 0) is cheaper; second-order (xi > 0) is more accurate (Sections 2.2-2.3).
- **Results / measured cost (Table, CIFAR-10)**: DARTS (second order) + cutout: 2.76 +/- 0.09% test error, 3.3M params, **4 GPU-days** search; DARTS (first order) + cutout: 3.00 +/- 0.14%, **1.5 GPU-days**. Reference points in the same table: NASNet-A 2000 GPU-days, AmoebaNet-A 3150 GPU-days, ENAS 0.5 GPU-day. The DARTS search cost EXCLUDES the selection cost (1 GPU-day) and final from-scratch retrain (1.5-3 GPU-days) (experiments, Table).
- **Transfer**: the CIFAR-10 cell transfers to ImageNet (mobile, 26.7% top-1); the PTB recurrent cell transfers to WikiText-2.

## Primary-source facts: limitations stated by the authors

- DARTS is "initialization-sensitive": "the optimization outcomes can be initialization-sensitive" (Section, near Fig. progress). They repeat each search 4x with different seeds and report median + best.
- The reported search cost is not the whole cost: selection + from-scratch retrain add several more GPU-days per result.

## Interpretation (flagged as inference)

- DARTS is the canonical "make the expensive search cheap by making it differentiable" result. The lineage is the whole point of my lane: NAS started at thousands of GPU-days (NASNet 2000, AmoebaNet 3150), then dropped 1000x with parameter sharing (ENAS 0.5 GPU-day) and with continuous relaxation (DARTS 1.5-4 GPU-days). Self-improvement of a learning system is real, and the field's main work for years was driving its cost down.
- The initialization sensitivity is an early signal of a known follow-on problem (later literature documents DARTS "collapsing" to all-skip-connection cells and needing many random restarts). The honest read: cheapening the search traded compute cost for brittleness and a clean-validation-metric dependency.
- For the autoresearch thesis: DARTS is the kind of inner-loop method an autoresearch coding agent could SELECT and tune (it is "differentiable recipe search"), but it assumes a clean differentiable validation objective. The repo's social-WAM does not have a clean differentiable validation loss at the social layer; it has a discrete, partly contested verifier signal. So DARTS transfers as reference framing, not as a drop-in.

## Mechanically useful vs research contribution

- **Mechanically useful**: the bilevel framing (optimize the recipe/config against a held-out validation signal while the inner weights fit the training signal) is exactly the shape of "tune the advisory-WAM training recipe against verifier-held-out cycles." The cell-search idea (search a small reusable building block, then stack/transfer it) maps to searching a small reusable WAM-prediction module and reusing it across scenarios.
- **Research contribution (not ours)**: differentiable NAS is a NAS-community contribution. The repo is not doing NAS.
- **What to avoid / overclaim**: do not assume gradient-based recipe search is cheap or stable for the repo. DARTS is cheap ONLY because it has a differentiable validation loss and a well-behaved cell space; it is still initialization-sensitive even there. The repo's validation signal (the runtime verifier) is non-differentiable, so a DARTS-style method does not transfer mechanically.

## WAM layer(s) informed

Method-level, cross-layer (reference context). Physical/Material WAM recipe tuning could borrow the bilevel + reusable-building-block framing; not applicable as-is to the non-differentiable social verifier.
