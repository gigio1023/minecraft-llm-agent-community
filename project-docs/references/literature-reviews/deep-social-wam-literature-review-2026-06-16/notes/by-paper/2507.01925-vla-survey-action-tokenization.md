## Primary-source facts

- **Formal VLA definition (verbatim sense)**: "models that generate actions conditioned
  on visual and linguistic inputs, and are built upon at least one large-scale vision or
  language foundation model." VLAs intersect digital AI, robotics, and hardware; framed
  as "the next frontier" of embodied AI on the path to AGI.
- **Unifying framework**: every VLA is a chain of *VLA modules* (maximal differentiable
  subnetworks, or non-differentiable functional units like motion planners) emitting
  *action tokens* that "progressively encode more grounded and actionable information,
  ultimately generating executable actions." Action tokens are framed as "generalized
  counterparts to language tokens in LLMs."
- **The eight action-token types** (the survey's central taxonomy): (1) language
  description (language plan vs language motion), (2) code, (3) affordance (keypoint /
  bbox / mask / affordance map), (4) trajectory, (5) **goal state** (a *predicted future
  observation*: image / point cloud / video clip, "serving as an intermediate target for
  planning and execution"), (6) latent representation, (7) raw action (low-level commands
  directly executable), (8) reasoning (an externalized decision process; a meta-token
  that enhances the others).
- **Raw-action lineage** (Sec. "Raw Action as Action Tokens"): LangLfP / BC-Z (feature
  fusion) -> VIMA / Gato / LEO / JARVIS-VLA (transformer generalists) -> RT-1 (130K demos,
  700+ tasks) -> RT-2 (co-fine-tune web-scale VLM, discretized action bins, emergent
  generalization, CoT) -> OXE/RT-X (1M+ trajectories, 22 robots, cross-embodiment gains)
  -> RoboFlamingo / OpenVLA (open 7B, 970K OXE subset) -> Octo (diffusion head, block-wise
  attention) -> pi_0 (flow matching + action chunking, **50 Hz vs RT-2's 5 Hz**) -> RDT,
  CogACT, GR00T N1, pi_0-FAST (DCT tokenization, 13.2x token compression),
  pi_0.5 (knowledge insulation). Framed via the "bitter lesson": scaling over engineering.
- **Goal-state action token = a forward-prediction step inside a VLA** (Sec. "Goal State
  as Action Tokens"): "we often engage in a mental simulation, envisioning the desired
  outcomes before executing." High-level generative model (DiT/CVAE) synthesizes a goal
  image/video from current obs + instruction; a low-level policy conditions on it. Lines:
  UniPi (text->video then IDM), AVDC (synthesize frames -> optical flow guidance), SuSIE,
  CoT-VLA, VPP, FLIP, Gen2Act. Advantages: data scalability via hindsight relabeling,
  use of action-free video, interpretability. Limitations: overspecification /
  inaccuracy of generated goals, and **high inference latency** (AVDC ~10 s for 8 frames;
  Gen2Act 3 Hz; VPP 7-10 Hz even with single-step denoising).
- **Acknowledged VLA structural limits** (across the discussion):
  - Imitation-learning ceiling: "an inherent upper bound imposed by the capabilities of
    human demonstrators," suboptimal/idiosyncratic demos; motivates RL on VLAs.
  - Data scarcity: OXE token count is "only about 1/200,000 of that in large-scale
    language model corpora"; raw robot data is costly, modality-poor, embodiment-fragmented.
  - Raw actions "cannot directly generalize across embodiments"; fine-tuning causes
    "catastrophic forgetting of pretrained visual-language knowledge."
  - "Most existing VLA models lack mechanisms for incorporating history" beyond a few
    frames; insufficient for long-horizon progress tracking / subtask dependencies.
- **Where it points toward world models (primary-source)**: "planning and online
  exploration in VLA agents could also be substantially enhanced by integrating advances
  in world models"; the goal-state synergy "is powerfully supported by world models,
  which can predict visual goal states to ground the generation of both [affordance and
  trajectory] token types." So the survey itself nominates predictive world modeling as
  the next ingredient VLAs lack.

## Interpretation (clearly labeled)

- The survey is not a WAM paper, but two of its eight token types (goal state; and the
  goal-based subtype of latent representation) are *exactly* the VLA literature reaching
  toward forward prediction `p(o' | o, l)`. The WAM-vs-VLA boundary is therefore a
  spectrum, not a wall: a "goal-state VLA" predicts a future observation but does not
  necessarily *couple* the action to it under a world-modeling objective (the WAM
  survey's second criterion). This is the cleanest source-backed way to state that
  "predicting a future" alone does not make a WAM; the *coupling commitment* does.
- pi_0.5's decomposition predicts a *language subtask* as the intermediate token, which is
  the survey's "language description" type, not a predicted state `o'`. So hierarchical
  VLAs (pi_0.5, Hi Robot, SayCan) are still VLAs: their intermediate token is semantic,
  not a forecast of the world.

## Mechanically useful vs research contribution

- **Useful to borrow**: the action-token vocabulary gives a precise way to locate this
  repo's LLM-actor: the Actor Turn emits a *typed tool selection* (closest to "code" /
  "raw action" action tokens under strict schemas), with no goal-state token. Adding an
  advisory social-material predictor would add a *goal-state-like* token used advisorily,
  not as authority. The survey's "VLA agent" direction (memory, planning, reflection,
  world models) is the right framing for where an LLM-actor + advisory-WAM project sits.
- **Avoid / overclaim**: do not import the manipulation-centric benchmark or hardware
  framing; this repo is symbolic-state Minecraft, not gripper manipulation. The survey is
  background/positioning, not a contribution to cite as a result.
