# Institutional AI: Governing LLM Collusion in Multi-Agent Cournot Markets via Public Governance Graphs

- title: Institutional AI: Governing LLM Collusion in Multi-Agent Cournot Markets via
  Public Governance Graphs
- authors: M. Bracale Syrnikov, F. Pierucci, M. Galisai, M. Prandi, P. Bisconti,
  F. Giarrusso, O. Sorokoletova, V. Suriani, D. Nardi (DEXAI Icaro Lab; Sapienza;
  Sant'Anna; VU Amsterdam)
- year: 2026
- venue/source: arXiv 2601.11369
- urls: paper https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.11369 ; hf https://huggingface.co/papers/2601.11369 (verified)
- source availability: LaTeX read (`papers/latex/2601.11369/main.tex`).

## Primary-source facts (what the paper states)

- Core reframing: alignment for deployed multi-agent LLM systems is moved "from
  preference engineering in agent-space to mechanism design in institution-space".
  Treat agents as black-box, bounded-rational optimizers that respond to incentives,
  constraint sets, and access rights, not to internal norm adherence.
- The governance graph: a public, immutable manifest declaring legal states,
  transitions, sanctions, and restorative paths. An Oracle/Controller runtime
  interprets the manifest: the Oracle turns public market outcomes into
  evidence-backed cases, a policy program selects eligible transitions, and the
  Controller enforces only manifest-declared transitions while writing a
  cryptographically keyed, append-only governance log (SHA-256 semantic and
  byte-level digests for regime identity and provenance). The institution does NOT
  rewrite agent proposals; behavior change comes from incentives and public context.
- Deterrence condition (the mechanism-level objective): let Delta-pi = per-round
  collusive rent (collusive profit minus competitive Cournot-Nash profit), p =
  probability evidence triggers escalation within the monitoring window, S = expected
  discounted sanction loss given escalation. A sufficient deterrence condition is
  p * S >= Delta-pi. Monitoring/escalation raise p, the sanction schedule sets S, and
  restorative paths make de-collusion (not concealment) the viable response. Crucially
  this does NOT require internalized norms: purely self-interested agents comply as a
  strategic best response.
- Institutional grammar: norms authored in an ABDICO grammar (extending ADICO,
  Crawford and Ostrom 1995) and bound to manifest transitions:
  Attribute (who), Deontic (obligated/permitted/prohibited), aIm (the regulated
  action/outcome), Object (what it applies to), Condition (trigger, operationalized
  by the Oracle as evidence-backed detections), Or-else (institutional response, e.g.
  Active -> Warning, Warning -> Fined). ABDICO records are the join layer between
  human-legible rules and machine-executable graph edges.
- Experiment: replicate the LLM-Cournot collusion case of Lin et al. 2024 (profit-
  seeking language firms learn supra-competitive market division without explicit
  communication). Three regimes: Ungoverned (baseline incentives), Constitutional
  (prompt-only written anti-collusion "constitution", policy-as-prompt), Institutional
  (governance-graph-based). Six model configurations including cross-provider pairs,
  N = 90 runs per condition.
- Results (stated):
  - Institutional regime: mean collusion tier falls from 3.1 to 1.8 (Cohen's d =
    1.28); severe-collusion incidence drops from 50% to 5.6%.
  - The prompt-only Constitutional baseline yields no reliable improvement:
    "declarative prohibitions do not bind under optimisation pressure".
- Positioning: complementary to Distributional AGI Safety (Tomasev et al. 2025),
  which argues safe agent economies need incentive-compatible market design plus
  accountability infrastructure (persistent cryptographic identity, immutable
  ledgers, smart contracts). This paper instantiates a lighter, portable governance
  object and enforces via a manifest interpreter rather than smart contracts. It
  surveys the normative-MAS lineage (social laws [Shoham-Tennenholtz], electronic
  institutions, NorMAS regimentation/enforcement/meta-governance) and the
  algorithmic-collusion lineage (Calvano et al. supra-competitive Q-learning pricing).

## Interpretation (my reading, labeled inference)

- This is the single closest existing system to the repo's Institutional layer done
  as an enforceable artifact. The governance graph (states, transitions, sanctions,
  restorative paths) + append-only evidence-keyed log is structurally the repo's
  ledger-of-evidence-events idea, raised to a declared finite-state institution. The
  ABDICO grammar is the formal, machine-readable version of the repo's "norm =
  situation-type + expectation + violation/repair refs" (the Bicchieri/Ostrom rows in
  the sociology theme), and it is exactly the Crawford-Ostrom lineage the repo already
  cites.
- The deterrence inequality p*S >= Delta-pi is a usable, quantitative Institutional
  prediction the repo could in principle score: given verified evidence of a
  cost-imposing or commons-depleting pattern (the "collusion" analogue is "a
  cooperating subset depleting a weak commons at outsiders' expense"), whether
  sanction probability times sanction size exceeds the rent predicts whether the
  pattern persists.
- The clean negative result (prompt-only constitution does nothing under optimization
  pressure) is a strong, on-theme caution: a written norm that is not bound to
  evidence-conditioned consequences is not an institution. This is the repo's own
  "an unenforced regularity is not yet an institution" (North row) demonstrated
  empirically.

## Mechanically useful vs research contribution

- Mechanically useful:
  - The ABDICO record + governance-graph schema is a directly adoptable structure for
    the repo's norm/obligation declarations: Attribute/Deontic/aIm/Object/Condition
    map onto a scenario's actors, permission modality, regulated action, target
    resource, and the verifier-detected trigger; Or-else maps onto a recorded
    sanction/repair event.
  - The append-only, evidence-keyed governance log is the same shape as the repo's
    artifact/evidence ledger and supports the same audit/provenance use.
  - The deterrence condition gives a measurable Institutional-layer hypothesis.
- Research contribution (where the repo MUST diverge):
  - This system ENFORCES (the Controller attaches binding consequences) and operates
    in an abstract Cournot market (the "good" is a quantity, the "harm" is a price
    signature). The repo's hard rule is observe-only at the Institutional layer: the
    runtime records whether a norm was maintained and whether a sanction occurred; it
    does not enforce one and does not gate outcomes on a predicted judgment. So the
    repo borrows the governance-graph/ABDICO *representation* and the deterrence
    *diagnostic*, but runs them as advisory predictions scored against verifier
    evidence, not as a runtime enforcement engine.
  - The repo grounds the "evidence" in physical/material deltas (an actual item, an
    actual depleted store), where this paper's evidence is a market-structure
    statistic.

## Reproducibility

- Self-reported. Numbers (mean tier 3.1 -> 1.8, d = 1.28; severe collusion 50% ->
  5.6%; N = 90/condition, six model configs) are quoted from the abstract/results.
  Uses multiple frontier models in a replication of a prior LLM-Cournot setup, so
  exact reruns are provider-dependent; the transferable content is the
  governance-graph/ABDICO formalism, the deterrence condition, and the prompt-only
  null result.
