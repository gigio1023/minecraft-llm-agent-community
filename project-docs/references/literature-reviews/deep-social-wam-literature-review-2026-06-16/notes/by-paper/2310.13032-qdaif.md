## Primary-source facts (what the paper states/shows)

- **Research question**: Quality-Diversity (QD) search returns a diverse archive of high-quality solutions, but it needs hand-crafted measures of quality and of the diversity descriptors. For subjective qualities (story genre, tone) those are infeasible to hand-code. Can a language model supply both the quality score and the diversity descriptor in natural language?
- **The mechanism (LM replaces hand-chosen QD descriptors/fitness; MAP-Elites)**: extends MAP-Elites, which discretizes the diversity space into a grid (archive) and keeps the highest-quality solution per bin. The loop: select a parent from the archive, mutate it, evaluate quality and diversity, replace the bin occupant if higher quality. QDAIF changes four parts: archive init (few-shot from 3 hand-chosen seeds spanning the axis), mutation (LMX language-model crossover, "Here is a random example of"; plus QDAIF-Rewrite for poetry, an instruction-following rewrite to a different genre/tone), evaluation, and grid discretization. Quality = prompt the LM yes/no on "high quality / on-topic," scored by the log-probability of one response vs the other; diversity = prompt the LM to judge a trait (e.g. sentiment positive vs negative) via log-probabilities, mapped to a non-uniform bin (bins denser toward range ends because LM logits are non-linearly calibrated).
- **Validity gate (note for the lane)**: there is NO physical solvability gate. The only admission rule is the MAP-Elites archive rule: a solution enters if it "fills an empty bin or perform[s] better than the solution already in its bin." The labeler is the LM evaluator itself, which the authors flag as gameable.
- **Numbers / results as stated** (quoted by sub-agent):
  - Setup: "2000 iterations, repeated with 5 different random seeds"; "maximum possible QD score is 20 (100 for 2D archive)."
  - QDAIF "achieves significantly better QD scores than all baseline methods in Opinions and Stories."
  - Human eval (avg over Opinions, Stories-Genre, Stories-Ending): QDAIF Human QD score 0.772 (best), Fixed-Few-Shot 0.767, Shuffling-Few-Shot 0.696, LMX Quality-Only 0.650, Random-Search 0.606. Human-AI agreement: QDAIF 0.833 (highest).
  - Poetry (GPT-4, 200 iterations): "QDAIF achieves a higher QD score of 130 (CI: 118 - 145)" vs Random-200 "76 (CI: 67 - 85)" and seed-rewrite ablation "99 (CI: 72 - 117)"; significance Mann-Whitney U, p <= 0.05.
  - Per-diversity-metric QD scores (Stories, max 20): Spy-vs-politician 19.6 (all bins filled), Historical-vs-SciFi 18.9, Third-vs-First-person 16.6, Adults-vs-Children 17.7, Poetic-vs-Formal 12.7 (many poetic bins unfilled).
  - Models: LMX generator = luminous-base 13B default; AI feedback = adapter-finetuned 70B (finetuning lifted ANLI R1 34% -> 58%, SST-2 60% -> 96%).
- **Reproducibility status**: SELF-REPORTED with human-validation of the AI feedback (human-AI agreement, 5 seeds, bootstrapped CIs). The "verifier" is itself an LM; there is no environment/runtime verifier. Project page qdaif.github.io.
- **Stated limitation (the load-bearing one for this review)**: "we suspect reward hacking happening when using LMs to generate feedback ... the correlation drops when the evaluated quality is in the range 0.995 to 1 ... The text generation might have exploited certain attributes or phrasings that allow an LM to give a high-quality estimate, but not what humans would agree is good." Proposed mitigations: RLHF-finetuned detectors, or an ensemble of evaluators with uncorrelated blind spots.

## Interpretation (flagged as inference)

- QDAIF maps not to scenario geometry but to SCENARIO DIVERSITY-AND-CURATION: MAP-Elites with LM-judged behavior descriptors is the mechanism for generating a comparable, coverage-controlled portfolio of seeds that systematically span chosen axes. For the repo, the axes could be the 4 layers (e.g. bins over social-conflict level x institutional-rule type), each bin holding the most informative seeded scenario, giving comparable coverage instead of ad-hoc scenario picks.
- Its admission rule is an archive-validity gate, but the labeler is an LM evaluator that the authors EXPLICITLY warn is reward-hackable (correlation collapses near the top of the quality range). This is the opposite of the repo's deterministic-verifier requirement: QDAIF must not be the validity gate itself.
- The right composition for the repo is QDAIF-style MAP-Elites for diversity/coverage of WHICH scenarios to generate, ProcTHOR-style seedable parameterized generation plus reject-and-resample for resettable scenarios, and a ProcTHOR/Holodeck/PCGRL-style deterministic gate (extended from physical reachability to all 4 layers) as the validity oracle that REPLACES QDAIF's LM judge.

## Mechanically useful vs research contribution

- **Mechanically useful**: (1) MAP-Elites coverage as the way to ensure a generated scenario portfolio spans the 4 layers rather than clustering; (2) "fill-an-empty-bin-or-beat-the-occupant" as a cheap, principled archive admission rule for a scenario bank; (3) the explicit reward-hacking-of-the-LM-judge finding as direct evidence for keeping the success/validity label deterministic-verifier-owned, never LM-owned; (4) the ensemble-of-uncorrelated-evaluators idea as a fallback for any necessarily-subjective axis (e.g. an advisory interestingness score), kept advisory only.
- **Not a contribution to copy / overclaim risk**: QDAIF's central claim (LM can supply quality and diversity feedback) is exactly the thing the repo must NOT do for the success label, because the paper itself measures the gaming. The repo should borrow the diversity-coverage machinery and the cautionary finding, and must not adopt an LM as the validity gate.

## WAM layer(s) informed

Method-level, cross-layer (diversity/coverage of the scenario portfolio). Provides no solvability or physical grounding; it is the curation layer, and its LM-judge is the documented failure mode the repo's deterministic verifier exists to avoid.
