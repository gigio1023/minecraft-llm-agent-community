# AIGS: Generating Science from AI-Powered Automated Falsification (Baby-AIGS)

- **title**: AIGS: Generating Science from AI-Powered Automated Falsification
- **authors**: Zijun Liu, Kaiming Liu, Yiqi Zhu, Xuanyu Lei, Zonghan Yang, Zhenhe Zhang, Peng Li, Yang Liu (Tsinghua University et al.)
- **year**: 2024 (arXiv 2024-11-18)
- **venue/source**: arXiv preprint
- **arxiv_id**: 2411.11910
- **urls**: paper https://arxiv.org/abs/2411.11910
- **source availability**: latex (extracted, `papers/latex/2411.11910/framework_v1.tex`)

## Primary-source facts (from framework_v1.tex)

- Central thesis: re-derives the scientific method from Popper and argues "falsification is the essence of both human research process and the design of an AIGS system" (abstract; sec:principle, line 266). Prior AI-science systems "either lack the [falsification] part in their design, or rely heavily on existing verification engines that narrow the use in specialized domains."
- Three design principles (line 269): **falsification**, **creativity**, **executability**. Mapping: ablation studies = falsification; methodology must be executable to gather empirical results; creativity is the objective, identified BY falsification. Key claim (line 271): "human trust in AI-generated findings relies heavily on a convincing falsification process that ensures scientific rigor and transparency."
- **Baby-AIGS** is a "baby-step" multi-agent demonstration. Two phases (lines 299-303): **Pre-Falsification** (idea formation, methodology design, experiment execution, result analysis; iterates M turns) and **Falsification** (explicitly automates ablation studies; the agent hypothesizes key factors and runs T ablation experiments; only hypotheses that pass are accepted as discoveries).
- Agents (lines 315-322): ProposalAgent (ideas + DSL-format methodology), ExperimentAgent (executes DSL as code), ReviewAgent (reviews on empirical results), **FalsificationAgent** (runs ablations, derives discoveries from the multi-turn log). Optional: LiteratureAgent, SecurityAgent, EnvironmentAgent, Domain-specific ExpAgent, Human Interface.
- A human-designed **Domain-Specific Language (DSL)** bridges idea -> executable instruction via a predefined action space; it raises executability but "may constrain idea diversity" (lines 309, 963-966).
- Experiments on three tasks (data engineering among them) "preliminarily show that Baby-AIGS could produce meaningful scientific discoveries, though not on par with experienced human researchers" (abstract).
- **Limitations** (sec:actionableinsights, lines 958-994): "more efforts are required to strengthen the modules related to knowledge falsification" (line 976); current peer-review-style feedback may not be the most effective evaluation for large-scale settings (line 970); the empirical-only scope (ML, runnable in computers) does not extend to fields where the experiment environment "could be hardly automated."

## Interpretation (inference, flagged)

- AIGS is the lane's conceptual cornerstone for the falsification angle and the closest match to the autoresearch thesis. Its core claim, that an autoresearch system's trustworthiness rests on an explicit falsification step rather than on the generative quality of its prose, is the same separation the repo enforces: the LLM proposes a hypothesis (here, "factor X causes effect Y"), but acceptance requires a verifier-grounded test (here, an ablation experiment).
- The repo analog is precise: discovering a WAM predictive rule ("placing a chest at location L makes item I retrievable by actor B") is a hypothesis; the runtime verifier running the scenario and checking the typed-delta outcome is the falsification test. A WAM rule that the verifier refutes must be dropped, exactly as Baby-AIGS drops hypotheses that fail ablation.
- Honest bound: Baby-AIGS's falsification lives inside ML ablation, where the metric is crisp and the DSL guarantees executability. The authors themselves flag that this does not transfer to fields where the environment cannot be automated. The repo's Physical/Material layers have an executable verifier (falsifiable); the Social/Institutional layers largely do not (no crisp ablation), which bounds how far the falsification design transfers up the layer stack.

## Mechanically useful vs research contribution

- **Mechanically useful**: the two-phase shape (refine under feedback, THEN explicitly falsify via controlled ablation) is a directly adoptable structure for validating WAM rules: propose-and-refine a predictive rule, then run verifier-grounded ablation scenarios that try to break it. The DSL-as-executable-action-space idea parallels the repo's schema-bound action cards (prose never supplies executable parameters).
- **Research contribution to NOT overclaim**: Baby-AIGS is a "baby-step" prototype that does not match human researchers; do not cite it as demonstrated autonomous discovery. The repo's borrow is the falsification PRINCIPLE (test, do not trust prose), not Baby-AIGS's results.

Method-level, cross-layer, with strongest grip on Physical/Material (where falsification via verifier is clean). Directly informs the thesis claim that "falsification equals a verifier-grounded hypothesis test."
