# The LAW: Language Models, Agent Models, and World Models for Reasoning and Planning

- **title**: Language Models, Agent Models, and World Models: The LAW for Machine Reasoning and Planning
- **authors**: Zhiting Hu, Tianmin Shu (UC San Diego, Johns Hopkins)
- **year**: 2023 (arXiv 2023-12-08)
- **venue/source**: arXiv position paper (NeurIPS 2023 tutorial lineage); same group as RAP
- **arxiv_id**: 2312.05230
- **urls**: paper https://arxiv.org/abs/2312.05230
- **source availability**: latex (single .tex, deep-read of the framework sections)

## Primary-source facts (LaTeX-verified)

- **The proposed conceptual framework (LAW)**: world models and agent models are the abstractions for reasoning/planning; language models are an INSTANTIATION (and a backend) of those models, not the top-level object. The argument is that current LMs are weak at reasoning because they lack explicit world and agent models.
- **World model, defined**: "a mental representation that agents use to understand and predict the external world," formulated as state-transition probabilities `p(s' | s, a)`, "a generative, causal mechanism of how the world state changes after an agent's actions." Notably they argue REWARD is part of the agent's mental state, not the world model, and should be separated from the physical environment.
- **Agent model, defined**: contains a world model PLUS the agent's goal, reward, and belief about the current state and other agents. Planning = "searches for actions that maximize its reward or reach its goal by simulating ahead using the world model in its mind."
- **The social extension (the lane-to-repo bridge)**: a level-0 agent model reasons about the world; a level-1 agent model is THEORY OF MIND, "forming mental models of other agents and conducting causal reasoning to interpret other agents' behaviors in terms of their mental states (such as goals and beliefs)." Verbatim thesis: "there is a critical need for AI systems to develop robust social reasoning that combines social commonsense (via level-1 agent models) and physical commonsense (via world models)."
- **Stated gap**: "Recent studies have revealed the lack of human-level social reasoning in LMs." The authors HYPOTHESIZE social reasoning can be enhanced "by building explicit world models and level-1 agent models and by training LLMs on experiences collected from interacting with the world and other agents." This is a hypothesis, not a result.

## Relevance to this repo (strongest conceptual anchor for the 4-layer tie)

- **This paper is the cleanest published mapping of the repo's layered structure onto the LLM-as-world-model framing, written by the RAP authors themselves.** Its world model `p(s'|s,a)` = the repo's Physical/Material dynamics; its level-1 agent model (theory of mind: beliefs about other agents' goals and beliefs) = the repo's Social layer. The paper states the exact dependency the repo's hard rule encodes: social reasoning must COMBINE social commonsense with physical commonsense via a grounded world model. That is the repo's "social prediction matters only when grounded in physical and material dynamics," arrived at from the LLM-reasoning side.
- **Separating reward from the world model is directly useful**: the repo's WAM predicts state deltas (the world model), and trust/obligation are ledger values updated by the agent/institution, not properties of physical dynamics. The LAW's reward-belongs-to-the-agent-model stance supports keeping the advisory WAM a pure state-transition predictor and keeping social ledger updates as a separate, verifier-driven layer.
- **The honest bound it supplies**: the LAW's social claims are explicitly hypotheses ("we hypothesize"), and it notes LMs lack human-level social reasoning. So this paper is the citation for WHY a structured social WAM is worth building, not evidence that one works. It also implies the repo's level-1 (theory-of-mind) predictions are the least-grounded and most contribution-heavy.
- **Research contribution vs support**: the framework is borrowable scaffolding. The contribution is instantiating the level-1 agent model as VERIFIER-GROUNDED social-ledger prediction (beliefs/trust updated only from verified events), which the LAW calls for but does not build.
