## Primary-source facts

- **Research question**: VLAs need fine-tuning for new robot setups, but "how to most
  effectively fine-tune them is unclear." Which decoding scheme, action representation,
  and learning objective give the best speed + success?
- **The OFT recipe** (Optimized Fine-Tuning): combine (1) **parallel decoding** (emit a
  whole action chunk at once, not autoregressively), (2) **action chunking**, (3) a
  **continuous action representation**, and (4) a simple **L1 regression** objective.
- **Results**: OpenVLA-OFT sets a new state of the art on LIBERO, raising OpenVLA's
  average success across four task suites from **76.5% to 97.1%** and increasing action
  generation throughput **26x**. In the real world it enables OpenVLA to do dexterous
  high-frequency bimanual control (ALOHA), outperforming pi_0 and RDT under their default
  recipes and strong from-scratch imitation policies (Diffusion Policy, ACT) by up to 15%.
- **Observation / action**: same as OpenVLA (images + proprioception in; continuous
  action chunks out after OFT). Pixel/motor manipulation.
- **Availability**: OFT code and pretrained checkpoints released (openvla-oft.github.io).

## Interpretation (clearly labeled)

- OpenVLA-OFT is a pure-VLA engineering result: it makes the reactive `p(a | o, l)`
  mapping faster and more accurate by fixing the *action decoder*, with no forward
  prediction of `o'` and no world-modeling objective. It is the strongest single
  datapoint that VLA inference can be made cheap and high-frequency (26x throughput) -
  which matters for the balanced trade in Part B: the speed/simplicity advantage is
  squarely on the VLA side, and recipes like OFT widen it.
- It also shows the action-representation choices (chunking, continuous, parallel
  decoding) are orthogonal to the VLA-vs-WAM question; both VLAs and WAMs use action
  chunking. The differentiator is prediction-of-`o'` + coupling, not the action head.

## Mechanically useful vs research contribution

- **Useful**: nothing directly mechanical for a symbolic-state Minecraft runtime (this is
  a continuous-control fine-tuning recipe). Its value here is as evidence for the
  "VLA is the fast, simple side of the trade" claim.
- **Avoid / overclaim**: do not read LIBERO manipulation numbers as relevant to social or
  Minecraft tasks. Not a source for any WAM layer.
