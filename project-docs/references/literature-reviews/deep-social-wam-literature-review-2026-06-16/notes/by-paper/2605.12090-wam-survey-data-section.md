## Primary-source facts

- Core thesis: WAM training is "fundamentally bottlenecked by the availability and
  quality of embodied data." Unlike LLMs (passively scraped text), WAMs need
  physical grounding for state transitions.
- Data-type taxonomy:
  - VLA needs strictly paired `(o_t, a_t)` trajectories, costly, scarce
    (teleoperation).
  - Pure World Models thrive on action-free `(o_t, o_{t+1})` web video, but lack
    control grounding.
  - WAM's advantage = "unified data digestion": uses `(o_t, a_t, o_{t+1})` triplets
    where available AND ingests massive *unpaired* action-free video.
- Four data paradigms surveyed, all pixel/sensor modality:
  1. Robot-centric teleoperation (e.g. OXE 1M+ traj across 22 robots; AgiBot World
     1M+; DROID 76k). Modalities RGB/depth/proprioception/tactile/audio/point-cloud.
  2. Portable human demos (UMI-style): RealOmin 1M traj / 3000+ envs; RDT2 ~10,000
     hours.
  3. Simulation: SynGrasp-1B 10M traj; RoboCasa 100k+; InternData-A1 630k. Physics
     engine = "exact computational world model" with privileged state.
  4. Human / egocentric video: Ego4D 3,670 h; HowTo100M 136M clips; DreamDojo
     43,827 h; **PLAICraft 10K+ h (crowdsourced Minecraft, RGB+audio)**.
- Every dataset table column is pixel-grounded (RGB, D, P, PC, T, A). There is **no
  structured/symbolic state transition dataset** in the survey's taxonomy.

## Interpretation (inference)

- The canonical WAM data ecosystem is enormous, expensive, and pixel-first. The
  cheapest scaling lever the survey identifies is action-free web video (no labels),
  which is precisely why latent-action methods (LAPA et al.) exist.
- A grep of this survey's eval (060) and opportunities (070) sections returns zero
  hits for "minecraft", "structured state", or "symbolic". The survey simply does
  not consider a typed-state instantiation. Per the shared contract §2, structured
  state is inside the canonical WAM definition's "implicit physical representation"
  branch, but the survey's *data* discussion never instantiates it.

## Mechanically useful vs research contribution

- Mechanically useful: the `(o_t, a_t, o_{t+1})` triplet framing is the exact unit
  this repo can emit per Actor Turn, but as typed fields, not pixels. The survey's
  "privileged information from simulation" point is the strongest analogy: Mineflayer
  is a deterministic world where before/after state is exactly observable, so the
  repo gets simulation-grade ground truth without rendering or sim-to-real gap.
- Research-contribution framing (defensible, modest): a structured social-material
  transition WAM occupies a quadrant the survey's data landscape leaves empty , 
  cheap to scale (verifier auto-labels) AND high social-semantic content. The
  contribution is the *representation choice and benchmark*, not the data tooling.
