# V-STaR: Training Verifiers for Self-Taught Reasoners

- **title**: V-STaR: Training Verifiers for Self-Taught Reasoners
- **authors**: Arian Hosseini, Xingdi Yuan, Nikolay Malkin, Aaron Courville, Alessandro Sordoni, Rishabh Agarwal (Mila, University of Montreal, Microsoft Research, Google DeepMind)
- **year**: 2024
- **venue/source**: arXiv 2402.06457 (COLM 2024)
- **arxiv_id**: 2402.06457
- **urls**: paper https://arxiv.org/abs/2402.06457
- **source availability**: latex (papers/latex/2402.06457/)

## Primary-source facts (what the paper states/shows)

- Diagnoses a waste in STaR-style self-improvement: STaR keeps only the self-generated solutions
  whose answer is correct and **discards all incorrect solutions**, "potentially neglecting
  valuable information."
- V-STaR uses BOTH correct and incorrect self-generated solutions: the correct ones fine-tune the
  generator (as in STaR), and the correct-vs-incorrect pairs train a **verifier via DPO** that
  judges solution correctness. At inference, the verifier does **best-of-N** selection over
  candidate solutions.
- Iterating both (generator improves, verifier improves) yields "progressively better reasoners
  and verifiers."
- **Result**: 4% to 17% test-accuracy improvement over prior self-improvement and verification
  approaches on code generation and math reasoning, with LLaMA2 models.

## Interpretation (flagged as inference)

- V-STaR is the "verify-then-add" refinement of STaR and stays on the admissible side: the
  correctness label that defines correct vs incorrect (and thus the verifier's DPO preference
  pairs) is the GROUND-TRUTH outcome, an external check. The verifier is bootstrapped from
  externally-verified labels, not from the model's self-opinion.
- The single most transferable idea for this repo: failures are training signal. STaR throws away
  verifier-failed transitions; V-STaR shows they are exactly what trains a verifier to recognize
  failure. The repo's runtime emits verifier-FAILED transitions for free (an action the actor
  proposed, that the runtime gated or that failed verification). Those are the negatives an
  advisory WAM needs to learn to PREDICT failure, not just success. A predictor trained only on
  successes cannot warn.
- V-STaR also separates two roles the repo already separates: the generator (the actor proposes)
  and the verifier (the runtime checks / an advisory predictor scores). V-STaR's verifier is
  best-of-N selection at inference; the repo keeps selection with the actor and the verifier as
  the runtime's truth, but the principle that a verifier trained on labeled successes-and-failures
  is more useful than a generator alone is directly relevant to shaping the advisory WAM.

## Mechanically useful vs research contribution

- **Mechanically useful**: train/condition the advisory WAM (or any social-transition predictor)
  on BOTH verifier-passed and verifier-failed transitions, so it can predict failure modes, not
  only confirm successes. This is a concrete data-curation rule for the repo's near-zero-cost
  transition stream: do not filter out the failures the way STaR does. Best-of-N verifier
  selection is a candidate pattern for ranking author_mineflayer_action trial candidates, scored
  by the runtime, though final selection stays with the actor per repo rules.
- **Research contribution it is NOT**: V-STaR's contribution is the correct+incorrect DPO verifier
  for self-taught reasoners. The repo borrows the "keep the failures, they train the verifier"
  insight as engineering.
