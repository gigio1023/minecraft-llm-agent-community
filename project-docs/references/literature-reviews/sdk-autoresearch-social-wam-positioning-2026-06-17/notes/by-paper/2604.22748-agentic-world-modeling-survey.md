## Primary-source facts (abstract-verified)

- A "levels x laws" taxonomy synthesizing 400+ papers (model-based RL, video generation,
  web/GUI agents, multi-agent simulation, AI-driven discovery).
- Three capability LEVELS:
  - L1 Predictor: one-step transitions.
  - L2 Simulator: multi-step action-conditioned rollouts.
  - L3 Evolver: autonomous model revision (i.e. a world model that improves itself).
- Four governing LAW regimes: physical, digital, social, scientific.

## Cross-product placement (the Lane B point)

This survey's axes map almost one-to-one onto the repo's four axes, which is useful and also
sobering:
- "social" is one of four named law regimes -> social world modeling is an explicitly
  recognized sub-area (not unclaimed turf), consistent with S3AP and 2510.21219.
- L3 "Evolver" (a self-revising world model) is the survey's name for the self-improvement
  axis -> "self-improving world model" is a named capability level, also not unclaimed.
- BUT the taxonomy keeps physical and social as SEPARATE regimes (matching 2510.21219's
  "separate silos" claim), and the survey is a map, not a system - it does not point to a
  single work that is simultaneously embodied-Minecraft + social-material + self-improving +
  verifier-grounded.

Use for positioning:
- Cite to show the field now has vocabulary for every individual axis (predictor/simulator/
  evolver levels; physical/digital/social/scientific laws). This RAISES the bar for novelty
  claims: the repo cannot claim to have invented "social world models" or "self-improving
  world models" as categories.
- The defensible space is the unoccupied INTERSECTION: a social-material (physical+social
  unified) world model at L1/L2 that stays advisory, in embodied Minecraft, refined by a
  verifier-grounded loop - a specific cell the taxonomy names the pieces of but does not fill.
