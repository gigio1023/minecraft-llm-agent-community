## Primary-source facts (abstract-verified)

- Problem: long-horizon (LH) tasks in open-ended worlds; the two named failures are
  reliance on human-created data/curricula and catastrophic forgetting of world knowledge.
- Mechanism: a curriculum self-evolving agent built around a "continual World Model (WM)";
  it completes LH tasks "through self-planning, self-control, and self-reflection, without
  human intervention". A planner-controller-reflector closed loop.
- Substrate: experiments include Minecraft (abstract page reports a large success-rate gain
  over prior methods).
- Self-evolution scoring (as stated): a "self-verification mechanism" plus curriculum-based
  experience selection.

## Cross-product placement (the Lane B point)

Axes hit: Minecraft (yes), world model (yes, "continual WM"), self-improvement loop (yes,
self-planning/control/reflection). This is one of the CLOSEST triples on Minecraft +
world-model + self-improvement.

What it MISSES relative to the repo's 4-way frame:
- SOCIAL: none. Single embodied agent; no second actor, no possession/obligation/trust/claim;
  the WM predicts physical/task state, not social-material deltas. The whole social-material
  axis (the repo's primary contribution cell) is absent.
- Verifier discipline: the loop's success signal is "self-verification" by the agent's own
  reflection, i.e. the agent scores its own success. That is exactly the pattern the repo
  names "progress laundering" and forbids (the deterministic verifier must own truth, an LLM
  judge or self-reflection must not). So even on the self-improvement axis it diverges on the
  one rule the repo enforces: who is allowed to score.

Net: EvolvingAgent shows Minecraft + WM + self-evolution is occupied, but only for
single-agent physical/task competence with a self-graded loop. It does not touch
material-grounded social consequence, and its scoring choice is the inadmissible one.
