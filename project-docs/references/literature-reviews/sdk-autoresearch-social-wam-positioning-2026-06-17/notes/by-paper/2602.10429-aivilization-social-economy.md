## Primary-source facts (abstract-verified)

- A publicly deployed large-scale artificial society coupling a "resource-constrained sandbox
  economy" with a unified LLM-agent architecture, aiming at long-horizon autonomy.
- Three components:
  - hierarchical branch-thinking planner: decomposes life goals into parallel objective
    branches with "simulation-guided validation plus tiered re-planning to ensure
    feasibility";
  - adaptive agent profile with dual-process memory (short-term execution traces vs long-term
    semantic consolidation);
  - human-in-the-loop steering interface (objectives propagate through memory, not prompt
    overrides).
- Environment: "physiological survival costs, non-substitutable multi-tier production, an
  AMM-based price mechanism, and a gated education-occupation system."

## Cross-product placement (the Lane B point)

Axes hit: SOCIAL with MATERIAL grounding (yes - survival costs, production tiers, AMM
prices, occupations are economic-material state that constrains social behavior). Forward
prediction (partial - "simulation-guided validation" is a feasibility check before acting,
a Cascaded-WAM-flavored move).

What it MISSES relative to the 4-way frame:
- MINECRAFT / embodied open world: no. It is a custom economic sandbox, not a physically
  embodied 3D world with blocks/position/durability and an external runtime like Mineflayer.
  Its "material" layer is an abstract economy (prices, production), not physical possession of
  a located, durable item you mined or carried.
- SELF-IMPROVEMENT / autoresearch loop: no. "Simulation-guided validation" is a per-agent
  feasibility check inside a single run, not a research loop that edits code/prompts/skills
  and keeps verified improvements.
- VERIFIER-as-truth: the economy is engine-computed (a strength, like GLEE's terminal states),
  but social outcomes are not separately verified against a deterministic external scorer in
  the repo's sense; the architecture is the contribution, not an admissible scoring rule.

Net: AIvilization is the closest recent "social grounded in material/economic dynamics"
system, and it even has a forward feasibility-simulation step. But it is a non-embodied
economic sandbox, single-run (no self-improvement loop), and its materiality is abstract
economics, not physically located, mined/carried/durable Minecraft items. The repo's
embodied-physical grounding (a specific pickaxe, lent, used, returned, durability dropping)
is exactly what this abstract-economy society does not have.
