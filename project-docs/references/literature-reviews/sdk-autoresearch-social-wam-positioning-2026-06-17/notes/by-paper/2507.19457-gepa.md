## Primary-source facts (abstract-verified via HF papers info; numbers are claim-only)

- **Problem framing**: LLMs are commonly adapted via RL methods like GRPO (Group Relative Policy Optimization) that "often require thousands of rollouts." GEPA's thesis: "the interpretable nature of language can often provide a much richer learning medium for LLMs, compared with policy gradients derived from sparse, scalar rewards."
- **GEPA = Genetic-Pareto**, "a prompt optimizer that thoroughly incorporates natural language reflection to learn high-level rules from trial and error."
- **The mechanism (reflective prompt mutation)**: "Given any AI system containing one or more LLM prompts, GEPA samples system-level trajectories (e.g., reasoning, tool calls, and tool outputs) and reflects on them in natural language to diagnose problems, propose and test prompt updates, and combine complementary lessons from the Pareto frontier of its own attempts." Sample-efficiency claim: "it can often turn even just a few rollouts into a large quality gain."
- **Results (abstract-stated, claim-only)**: "Across four tasks, GEPA outperforms GRPO by 10% on average and by up to 20%, while using up to 35x fewer rollouts. GEPA also outperforms the leading prompt optimizer, MIPROv2, by over 10% across two LLMs." Also "promising results as an inference-time search strategy for code optimization." (Note a fetch discrepancy: one arxiv.org HTML fetch returned "6% on average" and "six tasks"; the authoritative HF published summary says "10% on average" and "four tasks." This note uses the HF/canonical figures; the "6%/six" reading is treated as a fetch artifact.)

## The loop primitives (from the GitHub README; what GEPA exposes as a programmable loop)

GEPA's loop is "reflective text evolution":
1. **Select** a candidate from the Pareto frontier (non-dominated across task subsets).
2. **Execute** the candidate on a minibatch, capturing full execution traces.
3. **Reflect**: a `reflection_lm` reads the traces (errors, logs, evaluator feedback) and diagnoses failures in natural language.
4. **Mutate**: generate an improved candidate from the diagnosed lessons.
5. **Accept**: keep it if improved, update the Pareto front; repeat under a `max_metric_calls` budget.

- **Primary API**: `gepa.optimize(seed_candidate, trainset, valset, task_lm, reflection_lm, max_metric_calls, ...)`.
- **User SUPPLIES (the fixed parts)**: `seed_candidate` (the initial text artifact, e.g. `{"system_prompt": ...}`), the trainset/valset, and an evaluation function that returns a `score` PLUS textual feedback (the README's "Actionable Side Information"). The `GEPAAdapter` interface has `evaluate()` (run system, return score + diagnostic feedback) and `make_reflective_dataset()` (structure traces for reflection).
- **GEPA OPTIMIZES (the variable parts)**: textual parameters - prompts, instructions, code, agent configs, even vector graphics. It does NOT touch the evaluator/metric, which is user-provided and unchanged across the run.

## Interpretation (flagged as inference)

- GEPA is the strongest current statement of a NON-RL, prompt-only autoresearch loop whose success signal is an external user-supplied evaluator. It sits next to DSPy/MIPRO (`../../../../2026-06-16/deep-social-wam-literature-review/notes/by-paper/2310.03714-dspy-and-mipro.md`, same Khattab/Zaharia lineage): MIPRO optimizes instructions+demonstrations against a metric; GEPA adds natural-language reflection over full traces and Pareto selection, and claims to beat both MIPROv2 and RL with far fewer rollouts.
- The decisive property for this repo: GEPA keeps the metric/feedback function FIXED and user-owned, and only mutates the textual artifact. That is the proposer/scorer separation expressed as an optimizer API: the thing being improved (prompts) is structurally distinct from the thing that scores (the evaluator). If the repo supplied the deterministic runtime verifier as the GEPA evaluator (score) plus the verifier's structured deltas as the textual feedback, GEPA would evolve advisory-WAM/actor prompts against real physical checks, not an LLM judge.
- The sample-efficiency claim matters because the repo's social scenarios are costly to reset (no free regeneration like GitHub issues). An optimizer that "turns a few rollouts into a large gain" via reflection is more attractive than RL that needs thousands of rollouts, AT THE PHYSICAL/MATERIAL LAYER where the evaluator is clean.

## Mechanically useful vs research contribution

- **Mechanically useful**: the reflect-on-traces-then-mutate-the-prompt loop; the Pareto frontier of candidates (keep variants strong on different scenario subsets rather than collapsing to one scalar, an anti-overfit-to-one-metric pattern); the evaluator-returns-score-PLUS-textual-feedback contract (the verifier's structured deltas become the reflection signal); the `max_metric_calls` fixed budget (Karpathy's "fixed budget makes runs comparable" expressed as a cap).
- **Not a contribution to copy**: GEPA results are on QA/math/instruction tasks with answer-checkable metrics. Do not claim GEPA tunes embodied social policy. The borrow is the optimizer pattern and the fixed-evaluator discipline.
- **Honest bound**: GEPA is only as safe as its evaluator. Its reflection reads the evaluator's feedback; if that feedback is an LLM judge of social success, GEPA optimizes the judge's bias (the AgentRxiv reward-hacking risk, `../../../../2026-06-16/deep-social-wam-literature-review/notes/by-paper/2503.18102-agentrxiv.md`). It is admissible at Physical/Material where the evaluator is a deterministic verifier; it is NOT a license to optimize against an interpretive social score.

## WAM layer(s) informed

Method-level, cross-layer, honest only where the supplied evaluator is a real held-out verifier. The most directly adoptable PROMPT-improvement loop for refining the advisory WAM and Actor Turn prompts against the runtime verifier, sample-efficiently, at Physical/Material. Social/Institutional use is bounded by the evaluator-quality limit GEPA inherits.
