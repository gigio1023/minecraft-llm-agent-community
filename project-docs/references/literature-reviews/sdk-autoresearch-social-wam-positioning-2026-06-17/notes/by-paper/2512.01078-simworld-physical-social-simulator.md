## Primary-source facts (abstract-verified)

- Motivation: LLM/VLM agents are strong in math/coding/computer-use but weak in complex
  physical and social environments; building agents that "survive and thrive in the real
  world (for example, by autonomously earning income or running a business)" needs
  large-scale embodied interaction, reasoning, training, and evaluation.
- Gap it targets: existing world simulators rely on limited hand-crafted environments,
  simulate simplified game-like physics and social rules, and lack native LLM/VLM agent
  support.
- Build: an open-ended realistic simulator on Unreal Engine 5; open-sourced.

## Cross-product placement (the Lane B point)

Axes hit (by intent): PHYSICAL + SOCIAL world (the simulator explicitly spans both), embodied,
open-ended, with native LLM/VLM agent support.

What it MISSES relative to the 4-way frame:
- MINECRAFT / reproducible-substrate axis: it is a NEW UE5 simulator, not Minecraft. Relative
  to the repo's "Minecraft as a wild, natural, already-reproducible open-world substrate"
  argument, SimWorld is a freshly built world (its realism is a strength; its
  ecosystem/reproducibility maturity and the natural-vs-authored question differ from a
  decade-old Minecraft world that researchers already share). This is a substrate-choice
  contrast, not a defeater - SimWorld is a legitimate alternative substrate.
- PREDICTIVE WORLD MODEL: no learned `p(o'|o,l)` predictor in the abstract; it is a
  simulator/environment (the world itself), not an advisory transition predictor.
- SELF-IMPROVEMENT / autoresearch loop: none described.
- VERIFIER discipline / social-material delta scoring: it is an environment; the abstract does
  not claim a deterministic external scorer for social-material transitions in the repo's sense.

Net: SimWorld is the strongest recent statement that "physical + social, embodied, open-ended"
is a live research substrate, and it is open-sourced. But it is the WORLD, not a world-model;
it is UE5, not Minecraft; and it carries neither an advisory predictive WAM nor a
self-improving research loop. It sharpens the substrate debate (Minecraft vs purpose-built UE5
world) more than it occupies the repo's cell. (Substrate trade-offs are Lane C's territory;
flagged here only for the cross-product census.)
